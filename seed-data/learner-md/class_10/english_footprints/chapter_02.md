## Big Ideas

This story is not only about theft. It is about a young thief discovering that trust and education may offer him a better future than crime.

Hari Singh is skilled at deception. He lies about his name, lies about cooking, and steals small amounts from shopping. Yet Anil treats him with kindness and begins teaching him to read and write.

When Hari steals Anil's money, he realises that the real loss will not be money. It will be the loss of trust and the loss of his chance to become educated. That realisation makes him return.

## Opening Situation

Hari Singh is fifteen years old and already an experienced thief. He approaches Anil because Anil looks kind, simple, and easy to deceive.

Hari does not meet Anil for friendship. He meets him as a target. But the relationship changes because Anil's trust affects him.

# Unit 2B: Meeting Anil

## Hari Singh's Strategy

Hari chooses Anil after observing him at a wrestling match.

| Hari's Action | Purpose |
|---|---|
| Uses flattery | To start conversation and gain favour. |
| Gives a false name | To hide identity from police and former employers. |
| Follows casually | To continue contact without seeming desperate. |
| Offers to work | To enter Anil's life and gain access. |
| Lies about cooking | To make himself useful. |

## Why Hari Uses New Names

Hari changes his name every month to stay ahead of:

- the police;
- people he has robbed;
- former employers;
- anyone who may recognise him.

This shows he is experienced despite being young.

## Anil's First Impression

Anil seems:

- easy-going;
- kind;
- simple;
- trusting;
- not rich;
- open to helping Hari.

Hari sees these qualities as weaknesses at first. Later, they become the reasons he changes.

## Cooking Failure

Hari lies that he can cook. The meal he prepares is terrible, and Anil gives it to a stray dog. He tells Hari to leave.

Hari does not leave. He smiles appealingly. Anil laughs, forgives him, and says he will teach him to cook.

This is the first sign that Anil is different from ordinary employers.

# Unit 2C: Life with Anil

## What Hari Gets from Anil

| Need | What Anil Provides |
|---|---|
| **Food** | Anil feeds him. |
| **Shelter** | Hari sleeps on the balcony. |
| **Work** | Hari makes tea and buys supplies. |
| **Learning** | Anil teaches him to write his name, sentences, and numbers. |
| **Trust** | Anil gives him a key and lets him come and go. |
| **Dignity** | Anil treats him as teachable, not useless. |

## Hari's Small Cheating

Hari makes about a rupee a day by cheating in the shopping. Anil probably knows but does not make an issue of it.

This detail matters because:

- Hari has not fully changed yet;
- Anil is observant but tolerant;
- trust is being tested gradually;
- small dishonesty prepares us for the larger theft.

## Why Education Excites Hari

Hari believes that if he learns to write like an educated man, there will be no limit to what he can achieve.

At first, he may imagine using education for cleverer crime. But later, he sees education as a path to becoming respectable.

## Anil's Income

Anil earns irregularly. He writes for magazines, borrows at times, lends at other times, and celebrates when money arrives.

This matters because Anil is not wealthy. Stealing from him is not like stealing from someone who can easily replace the money.

# Unit 2D: The Theft

## Why Hari Decides to Steal

Hari has been with Anil for almost a month and has not done "real work" as a thief. He feels out of practice.

He also tells himself:

- Anil does not pay him;
- Anil may waste the money on friends;
- he has an easy chance;
- he should not miss the opportunity.

These are excuses. They help him silence his conscience.

## The Money

Anil comes home with a bundle of notes after selling a book to a publisher. At night, Hari sees him place the money under the mattress.

Hari steals the money while Anil sleeps.

## Escape Plan

Hari plans to catch the 10.30 Express to Lucknow. He reaches the station without buying a ticket, because he has never bought one in his life.

The train is moving slowly enough for him to board, but he hesitates and loses the chance.

## Why the Hesitation Matters

This is the turning point. Hari does not fully understand why he hesitates, but the reader can infer:

- he feels guilty;
- he is attached to Anil;
- he fears losing the chance to learn;
- he does not want to betray trust;
- he is tired of living without real relationships.

# Unit 2E: Moral Conflict

## Hari's Study of Faces

Hari has watched how people react after being robbed.

| Type of Person | Reaction |
|---|---|
| **Greedy man** | Shows fear. |
| **Rich man** | Shows anger. |
| **Poor man** | Shows acceptance. |
| **Anil** | Would show sadness at loss of trust. |

This shows Hari is observant and psychologically sharp.

## Why Anil Is Difficult to Rob

Hari says it is difficult to rob a careless man because such a man may not notice the loss. But Anil is difficult for a deeper reason: he trusts Hari.

Stealing money is easy. Bearing the betrayal of trust is hard.

## Rain and Loneliness

After missing the train, Hari wanders in the bazaar and maidan. It is cold, rainy, and lonely.

The weather reflects his inner discomfort:

- he is physically wet and cold;
- he is emotionally unsettled;
- the stolen notes become damp;
- he has no friend except the man he robbed.

## Education vs Stolen Money

Hari realises that the money may last a week or two, but education can change his life.

| Stolen Money | Education |
|---|---|
| Short-term gain | Long-term possibility |
| Risk of being caught | Path to respect |
| Keeps him a thief | May help him become "a big man" |
| Breaks trust | Builds future |

## Why Hari Returns

Hari returns because:

- he feels guilty;
- he values Anil's trust;
- he wants to learn reading and writing;
- he senses a chance at dignity;
- he does not want to remain only a thief.

# Unit 2F: Return and Silence

## Returning the Money

Hari returns to Anil's room and quietly puts the notes back under the mattress. He finds it harder to return the money undetected than to steal it.

This line is important because returning requires moral courage. It is not just fear; it is a choice.

## The Next Morning

Anil gives Hari a fifty-rupee note and says he will now pay him regularly. Hari notices that the note is still wet from the rain.

This means Anil knows what happened.

## Does Anil Realise He Was Robbed?

Yes. The damp note proves that he knows. But he does not expose Hari.

## Why Anil Does Not Hand Hari to the Police

Anil understands that Hari has returned the money voluntarily. He chooses to encourage reform rather than punish betrayal.

He may believe:

- trust has already begun to change Hari;
- public shame may destroy the boy's chance;
- education can reform him;
- forgiveness may work better than punishment.

## How Anil Is Different from Other Employers

| Ordinary Employer Might... | Anil... |
|---|---|
| Beat or dismiss the boy | Gives him a chance. |
| Call the police | Remains silent. |
| Stop trusting him | Offers regular pay. |
| Treat him as criminal only | Treats him as a learner. |
| Focus only on money | Understands moral recovery. |

## Ending

Anil says they will start writing sentences that day. Hari smiles naturally, without effort.

The effortless smile shows real change. Earlier he used appealing smiles as a trick. Now the smile comes from gratitude and relief.

# Unit 2G: Thinking About It

## Hari's Reaction to Education

At first, education seems useful to Hari because it may help him achieve more. He thinks that writing like an educated man will open unlimited possibilities.

Later, his understanding deepens. Whole sentences may bring more than stolen money because they can help him become clever, respected, and independent.

## Does His Attitude Change?

Yes.

| Earlier Thought | Later Thought |
|---|---|
| Education may help him succeed, possibly even as a cleverer thief. | Education can help him become a respected person. |
| Theft seems natural work. | Theft seems small and risky. |
| Anil is a target. | Anil becomes teacher and friend. |

## What Makes Him Return?

The combined force of:

- Anil's trust;
- guilt;
- loneliness;
- lost chance to escape;
- value of education;
- desire for a better identity.

## Why Anil's Forgiveness Is Powerful

Anil's silence is not ignorance. It is deliberate kindness. By not humiliating Hari, he makes it possible for Hari to choose honesty.

## Are People Like Anil and Hari Found in Real Life?

Yes. There are trusting, generous people like Anil, and there are young people like Hari who may be pushed toward theft by poverty, neglect, or lack of guidance.

The story is fiction, but its emotional truth is realistic.

## Does Anil Being a Struggling Writer Matter?

Yes. It helps explain:

- why he has irregular income;
- why he is not materialistic;
- why he values education;
- why he may understand human weakness;
- why he is generous despite having little.

Anil's poverty makes his trust even more meaningful.

## Circumstances That Can Turn a Fifteen-Year-Old into a Thief

Possible causes:

- poverty;
- hunger;
- homelessness;
- lack of family support;
- bad company;
- no schooling;
- exploitation by adults;
- survival pressure;
- habit formed early;
- lack of safe work opportunities.

The story does not excuse theft, but it encourages understanding the causes behind it.

## Setting of the Story

Clues include:

- Jumna Sweet Shop;
- Lucknow Express;
- bazaar;
- maidan;
- names such as Hari Singh and Anil.

The setting is likely North India. Languages spoken in such places may include Hindi, Urdu, local dialects, and possibly some English.

It is unlikely that the characters spoke only English to each other. The story is written in English for readers, but the actual conversation may have occurred in Hindi or another local language.

## First-Person Narration

The story is powerful because Hari tells it himself.

Benefits:

- we hear his excuses;
- we see his cleverness;
- we understand his guilt;
- we witness his change from inside;
- the ending feels personal and honest.

## Final Chapter Summary

- Hari Singh is a fifteen-year-old thief who meets Anil at a wrestling match.
- He lies about his name and cooking ability to gain Anil's trust.
- Anil feeds him, gives him shelter, and begins teaching him to write and count.
- Hari steals small amounts from shopping but is not seriously punished.
- One night he steals six hundred rupees from under Anil's mattress.
- At the station, he hesitates and misses the train to Lucknow.
- He thinks about Anil's trust and the value of education.
- He returns the money secretly.
- The next morning, Anil gives him a wet fifty-rupee note, showing he knows.
- Anil does not expose him and instead promises regular pay and writing lessons.
- Hari's natural smile at the end shows the beginning of real change.
