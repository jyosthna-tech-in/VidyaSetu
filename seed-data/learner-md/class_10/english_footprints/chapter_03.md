## Big Ideas

The story shows that a true secret agent does not need to look glamorous. Ausable is not physically impressive, but he has intelligence, calmness, and control over language. Max has a gun, but Ausable has presence of mind.

The central weapon in the story is not a pistol. It is a believable lie: the non-existent balcony.

Fowler comes expecting romantic spy adventure. He gets a real thrill, but not in the way he expects.

# Unit 3A: Before Reading

## Usual Spy Stereotypes

In books and films, secret agents are often shown as:

- slim and athletic;
- sharply dressed;
- mysterious;
- dangerous;
- skilled with weapons;
- surrounded by secret messages;
- involved in chases, gunshots, and dramatic meetings;
- glamorous and confident.

The story deliberately breaks this image through Ausable.

## Fowler's Expectation

Fowler is a writer. He is young and romantic. He expects mystery, danger, and excitement from a secret agent.

Instead, he spends a dull evening with Ausable and feels disappointed.

This contrast prepares the reader for the story's main surprise: Ausable is effective despite not looking like a spy.

# Unit 3B: Ausable's Appearance

## How Ausable Is Different from Other Secret Agents

| Expected Spy Image | Ausable |
|---|---|
| Slim and stylish | Fat and carelessly dressed. |
| Glamorous setting | Gloomy French hotel room. |
| Perfect accent | Keeps his American accent. |
| Dramatic movements | Wheezes and moves heavily. |
| Pistol action | Uses words and calm thinking. |
| Romantic adventure | Ordinary phone calls and patient waiting. |

## Why Fowler Feels Let Down

Fowler feels disappointed because Ausable seems ordinary and unexciting. The hotel corridor is musty, the room is small, and Ausable does not match the romantic spy image.

But this disappointment is part of the lesson: appearances can mislead.

## Ausable's Self-Awareness

Ausable knows Fowler is disappointed. He even explains Fowler's expectations aloud. This shows that Ausable understands people quickly.

That psychological skill later helps him defeat Max.

# Unit 3C: Max Appears

## Fowler's First Authentic Thrill

When Ausable switches on the light, Fowler sees a man with a pistol in the room. This is Fowler's first real thrill of the day.

The danger is now real.

## Who Is Max?

Max is another secret agent. He wants the important report that is expected that night.

| Detail | Information |
|---|---|
| **Appearance** | Slender, not very tall. |
| **Expression** | Crafty, fox-like face. |
| **Weapon** | Small automatic pistol. |
| **Goal** | Get the report about missiles. |
| **Entry method** | Uses a passkey. |

## How Has Max Got In?

Max actually enters through a passkey. He does not use a balcony.

## What Does He Want?

He wants to take the report concerning new missiles. The report is important enough that several people have risked their lives for it.

## Max's Mistake

Max has a weapon, but he does not fully control the situation. He believes Ausable's invented story too quickly.

# Unit 3D: The Balcony Story

## Ausable's False Claim

Ausable acts irritated and says someone has entered his room through the balcony again. This surprises Max because he used a passkey and did not know about any balcony.

There is actually no balcony.

## How Ausable Makes It Convincing

Ausable adds details:

| Detail | Why It Sounds Convincing |
|---|---|
| It belongs to the next apartment. | Explains why it is not obviously his own balcony. |
| The rooms were once part of a larger unit. | Gives architectural background. |
| The balcony extends under his window. | Explains how someone could enter. |
| Empty room two doors down gives access. | Provides a practical route. |
| Someone used it last month. | Makes the problem seem repeated. |
| Management promised to block it. | Adds ordinary hotel complaint realism. |

The lie works because it is not vague. It is detailed, irritated, and practical.

## Why Ausable's Acting Works

Ausable does not sound frightened. He sounds annoyed at hotel management. This makes Max think the balcony is a known nuisance, not a story invented in panic.

## Fowler's Role

Fowler also hears the balcony story and believes it. His confusion helps the reader share the suspense.

# Unit 3E: The Knock

## The Second Bluff: Police

When someone knocks, Ausable says it must be the police. He claims he arranged extra protection for the important report.

This frightens Max because he believes:

- the police are outside;
- they may enter if Ausable does not answer;
- they may shoot;
- escape through the balcony is possible.

## Who Is Actually at the Door?

It is Henry, the waiter, bringing the drink Ausable had ordered.

## Why Max Goes to the Window

Max thinks he can hide on the balcony until the police leave. He climbs out of the window while keeping his gun aimed.

Because there is no balcony, he falls.

## The Twist Ending

Fowler asks about the police and the balcony. Ausable calmly explains:

- there were no police;
- the visitor was only Henry;
- there is no balcony.

This final revelation changes the reader's understanding of the entire scene.

## How Ausable Outwits Max

| Max Has | Ausable Uses |
|---|---|
| Pistol | Calmness |
| Surprise | Quick thinking |
| Threat | Psychological control |
| Information about report | Better understanding of people |
| Physical danger | Language and bluff |

# Unit 3F: Thinking About It

## 1. Spy Appearance in Books and Films

Many spy characters look stylish, strong, and dramatic. They may wear fine suits, drive fast cars, carry hidden weapons, and fight physically.

Ausable is different. He is fat, speaks with an accent, lives in an ordinary hotel room, and does not look glamorous. Yet he is more effective than Max because he thinks quickly.

## 2. How Does Ausable Make Max Believe the Balcony Exists?

He gives a detailed and confident explanation. He speaks as though the balcony is an old irritation. He connects it with the neighbouring apartment, the hotel's structure, an earlier incident, and management's failure.

Max believes him because people often trust detailed complaints more than sudden claims.

## 3. When Did Ausable Think of the Plan?

Most likely, Ausable invents the balcony story after seeing Max in the room. He improvises quickly.

When the waiter knocks, Ausable adapts the plan further by saying it is the police.

This suggests:

- he does not have every detail planned from the beginning;
- he uses events as they happen;
- his real strength is presence of mind.

## Alternative View

One could argue that Ausable begins planning the moment he sees Max and quickly builds the whole idea around a possible interruption. But the waiter Henry's arrival gives him the perfect chance to complete the bluff.

## What Is Presence of Mind?

Presence of mind means:

- staying calm in danger;
- noticing available details;
- speaking carefully;
- predicting the opponent's reaction;
- acting before panic takes over.

Ausable shows this throughout the scene.

# Unit 3G: Talk About It - Real-Life Presence of Mind

## Why Mental Preparedness Matters

If we think about possible dangers before they happen, we are more likely to respond calmly.

The story is not asking learners to become spies. It teaches the value of thinking clearly under pressure.

## Emergency Situations

| Situation | Wise Response |
|---|---|
| **Small kitchen fire** | Turn off gas/electricity if safe, cover pan fire with lid, do not throw water on oil fire, call an adult/fire service. |
| **Child choking** | Call for help, encourage coughing if possible, use age-appropriate first aid, contact emergency services. |
| **Electrical appliance sparks** | Switch off power from main source if safe, do not touch with wet hands, keep distance, call electrician. |
| **Bicycle knocks pedestrian** | Stop, check injury, move only if necessary for safety, call help, do not run away. |
| **Continuous rain for over 24 hours** | Avoid flooded roads, store drinking water, charge phone, follow local warnings. |
| **Family member late** | Call them, contact known friends, check route, inform responsible adults, escalate if needed. |

## Learner Reflection

Presence of mind is not the same as fearlessness. A person may feel afraid but still act wisely.

## Final Chapter Summary

- Fowler expects Ausable to be a glamorous secret agent.
- Ausable appears fat, ordinary, and unimpressive.
- In Ausable's room, Max appears with a pistol and demands a secret report.
- Max has entered with a passkey.
- Ausable invents a story about a balcony outside the window.
- A knock comes at the door; Ausable claims it is the police.
- Max believes him and tries to hide on the supposed balcony.
- There is no balcony, so Max falls.
- The visitor at the door is only Henry, the waiter.
- Ausable wins through calmness, intelligence, and presence of mind.

## Study Strategy for Learners

1. Remember the three central facts: Max has a gun, Ausable invents a balcony, Henry is not police.
2. For character answers, contrast appearance and ability.
3. For suspense answers, explain the balcony as misdirection.
4. For presence of mind, focus on Ausable's calm voice and quick use of the knock.
5. Learn the glossary because spy stories often use atmosphere words.
