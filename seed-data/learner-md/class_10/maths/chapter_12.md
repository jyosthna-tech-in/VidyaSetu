### NCERT Class 10 - Maths

This learner version rewrites the raw chapter into a detailed guide on combined solids. It separates surface-area thinking from volume thinking, because the two are handled differently when solids are joined, mounted, hollowed, or scooped.

## Key Rule

Surface area and volume behave differently.

For surface area:

$$
\text{use only the exposed surface}
$$

For volume:

$$
\text{add volumes of joined solids and subtract volumes removed}
$$

Hidden joined faces are not counted in surface area.

# Unit 12A: Formula Bank

## Cube

If side is `a`:

$$
\text{TSA}=6a^2
$$

$$
\text{volume}=a^3
$$

## Cuboid

If length, breadth, and height are `l`, `b`, and `h`:

$$
\text{TSA}=2(lb+bh+hl)
$$

$$
\text{volume}=lbh
$$

## Cylinder

If radius is `r` and height is `h`:

$$
\text{CSA}=2\pi rh
$$

$$
\text{TSA}=2\pi r(h+r)
$$

$$
\text{volume}=\pi r^2h
$$

## Cone

If radius is `r`, height is `h`, and slant height is `l`:

$$
l=\sqrt{r^2+h^2}
$$

$$
\text{CSA}=\pi rl
$$

$$
\text{TSA}=\pi r(l+r)
$$

$$
\text{volume}=\frac{1}{3}\pi r^2h
$$

## Sphere

If radius is `r`:

$$
\text{surface area}=4\pi r^2
$$

$$
\text{volume}=\frac{4}{3}\pi r^3
$$

## Hemisphere

If radius is `r`:

$$
\text{CSA}=2\pi r^2
$$

$$
\text{TSA}=3\pi r^2
$$

$$
\text{volume}=\frac{2}{3}\pi r^3
$$

---

# Unit 12B: Surface Area Strategy

When solids are joined:

1. identify each basic solid;
2. mark the common joined face;
3. exclude hidden faces;
4. add only exposed curved and flat surfaces.

## Common Patterns

| Composite solid | Surface area to count |
|---|---|
| Cone on hemisphere | CSA of cone + CSA of hemisphere |
| Cylinder with two hemispherical ends | CSA of cylinder + surface area of full sphere |
| Hemisphere on cube | TSA of cube - circular covered base + CSA of hemisphere |
| Cone on cylinder with larger cone base | CSA cone + exposed base ring + CSA cylinder + exposed cylinder base if required |
| Hemispherical depression in cube | TSA cube - circular opening + CSA hemisphere depression |

---

# Unit 12C: Volume Strategy

For volume:

- joined solid: add volumes;
- hollowed or scooped part: subtract removed volume;
- capacity: volume of empty space;
- solid material left: outer volume minus removed volume.

Unlike surface area, hidden joined faces do not matter in volume.

## Question 1: Two Cubes Joined End to End

Each cube has volume:

$$
64\text{ cm}^3
$$

Let side of each cube be `a`.

$$
a^3=64
$$

So:

$$
a=4\text{ cm}
$$

When two cubes are joined end to end, the resulting cuboid has dimensions:

$$
8\text{ cm}\times 4\text{ cm}\times 4\text{ cm}
$$

Surface area:

$$
2(lb+bh+hl)
$$

$$
=2(8\cdot4+4\cdot4+8\cdot4)
$$

$$
=2(32+16+32)
$$

$$
=160
$$

Answer:

$$
160\text{ cm}^2
$$

## Question 2: Hollow Hemisphere Mounted by Hollow Cylinder

Diameter:

$$
14\text{ cm}
$$

So:

$$
r=7\text{ cm}
$$

Total height:

$$
13\text{ cm}
$$

Height of cylindrical part:

$$
13-7=6\text{ cm}
$$

Inner surface area:

$$
\text{CSA of hemisphere}+\text{CSA of cylinder}
$$

$$
=2\pi r^2+2\pi rh
$$

$$
=2\pi r(r+h)
$$

Substitute:

$$
=2\times\frac{22}{7}\times7(7+6)
$$

$$
=44\times13
$$

$$
=572
$$

Answer:

$$
572\text{ cm}^2
$$

## Question 5: Hemispherical Depression in a Cube

Let cube edge be:

$$
l
$$

The hemisphere diameter equals the cube edge, so:

$$
r=\frac{l}{2}
$$

Surface area of remaining solid:

$$
\text{TSA cube}-\text{circular opening}+\text{CSA of hemispherical depression}
$$

$$
=6l^2-\pi r^2+2\pi r^2
$$

$$
=6l^2+\pi r^2
$$

Substitute:

$$
r=\frac{l}{2}
$$

So:

$$
\text{surface area}=6l^2+\pi\left(\frac{l}{2}\right)^2
$$

$$
=6l^2+\frac{\pi l^2}{4}
$$

Answer:

$$
l^2\left(6+\frac{\pi}{4}\right)
$$

# Unit 12F: Common Mistakes and Corrections

| Mistake | Why it is wrong | Correct habit |
|---|---|---|
| Adding total surface areas of joined solids | Joined faces become hidden | Add only exposed surfaces |
| Using cone height for cone CSA | Cone CSA needs slant height | Find `l = sqrt(r^2+h^2)` |
| Using slant height for cone volume | Cone volume needs vertical height | Keep `h` and `l` separate |
| Forgetting radius is half diameter | Formulas use radius | Divide diameter by 2 first |
| Counting base of a tent | Tent base is not covered with canvas | Exclude base unless stated |
| Adding removed volume instead of subtracting | Scooped/cavity material is removed | Subtract depressions or cavities |
| Ignoring unit conversion | `cm^2` to `m^2` changes by 10000 | Convert before final answer |

# Final One-Page Summary

1. Surface area counts exposed area only.
2. Volume counts space occupied or removed.
3. Joined faces disappear from surface-area calculation.
4. For a cone:

$$
l=\sqrt{r^2+h^2}
$$

5. Cone curved surface area:

$$
\pi rl
$$

6. Cone volume:

$$
\frac{1}{3}\pi r^2h
$$

7. Hemisphere curved surface area:

$$
2\pi r^2
$$

8. Hemisphere volume:

$$
\frac{2}{3}\pi r^3
$$

9. A capsule with two hemispherical ends has surface area:

$$
\text{CSA cylinder}+4\pi r^2
$$

10. A scooped hemisphere contributes curved inner surface area, not flat circular area.
