# Unit 11A: Sector and Segment

## Sector

A sector is the part of a circle enclosed by:

- two radii;
- the arc between them.

If the angle at the centre is `theta`, then the sector is the same fraction of the circle as:

$$
\frac{\theta}{360^\circ}
$$

## Segment

A segment is the part of a circle enclosed by:

- a chord;
- the arc corresponding to that chord.

The minor segment is usually found by:

$$
\text{area of segment}=\text{area of sector}-\text{area of triangle}
$$

# Unit 11B: Formula Bank

## Circumference

For radius `r`:

$$
\text{circumference}=2\pi r
$$

## Area of Circle

$$
\text{area of circle}=\pi r^2
$$

## Arc Length

If radius is `r` and central angle is `theta` degrees:

$$
\text{arc length}=\frac{\theta}{360^\circ}\times 2\pi r
$$

## Area of Sector

$$
\text{area of sector}=\frac{\theta}{360^\circ}\times \pi r^2
$$

## Area of Segment

$$
\text{area of segment}=\text{area of sector}-\text{area of triangle formed by the two radii and chord}
$$

## Area of Major Sector

$$
\text{area of major sector}=\pi r^2-\text{area of minor sector}
$$

## Area of Major Segment

$$
\text{area of major segment}=\pi r^2-\text{area of minor segment}
$$

# Unit 11C: Common Triangle Areas Inside Sectors

## Central Angle `60^\circ`

If a chord subtends `60^\circ` at the centre, then the triangle formed by the two radii and the chord is equilateral.

If radius is `r`, then:

$$
\text{area of triangle}=\frac{\sqrt{3}}{4}r^2
$$

## Central Angle `90^\circ`

If the central angle is `90^\circ`, the triangle formed by the two radii is a right triangle.

If radius is `r`, then:

$$
\text{area of triangle}=\frac{1}{2}r^2
$$

## Central Angle `120^\circ`

If two radii of length `r` include an angle of `120^\circ`, then:

$$
\text{area of triangle}=\frac{1}{2}r^2\sin 120^\circ
$$

Since:

$$
\sin 120^\circ=\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}
$$

we get:

$$
\text{area of triangle}=\frac{\sqrt{3}}{4}r^2
$$

for the included-angle formula with `120^\circ`.

# Unit 11D: Worked Examples Rebuilt

## Example 1: Sector and Major Sector

Find the area of a sector of radius `4 cm` and angle `30^\circ`. Use `pi = 3.14`.

Area of sector:

$$
\frac{30}{360}\times 3.14\times 4^2
$$

$$
=\frac{1}{12}\times 3.14\times 16
$$

$$
=4.19\text{ cm}^2\text{ approximately}
$$

Area of circle:

$$
3.14\times 16=50.24\text{ cm}^2
$$

Area of major sector:

$$
50.24-4.19=46.05\text{ cm}^2
$$

## Example 2: Segment

Radius is `21 cm` and central angle is `120^\circ`. Use `pi = 22/7`.

Area of sector:

$$
\frac{120}{360}\times\frac{22}{7}\times 21^2
$$

$$
=462\text{ cm}^2
$$

Area of triangle:

$$
\frac{1}{2}\times 21\times 21\times \sin 120^\circ
$$

$$
=\frac{441\sqrt{3}}{4}
$$

Area of segment:

$$
462-\frac{441\sqrt{3}}{4}
$$

## Question 2: Area of a Quadrant

Circumference is:

$$
22\text{ cm}
$$

Use:

$$
2\pi r=22
$$

Substitute:

$$
2\times\frac{22}{7}\times r=22
$$

So:

$$
r=\frac{7}{2}
$$

Area of quadrant:

$$
\frac{1}{4}\pi r^2
$$

$$
=\frac{1}{4}\times\frac{22}{7}\times\left(\frac{7}{2}\right)^2
$$

$$
=\frac{77}{8}
$$

Answer:

$$
\frac{77}{8}\text{ cm}^2
$$

# Unit 11G: Common Mistakes and Corrections

| Mistake | Why it is wrong | Correct habit |
|---|---|---|
| Using full circle area for a sector | Sector is only a fraction of the circle | Multiply by `theta/360` |
| Confusing sector and segment | Segment excludes the triangle formed by radii | Segment = sector - triangle |
| Forgetting major sector | Major sector is the remaining part of circle | Subtract minor sector from full circle |
| Using diameter as radius | Formula needs radius | Halve the diameter first |
| Ignoring given value of `pi` | Different questions specify different values | Use the value stated in the question |
| Rounding too early | Final answer may change | Keep exact fractions until final step |
| Treating all segments as semicircles | Segment depends on chord and angle | Identify the central angle |

# Final One-Page Summary

1. Area of circle:

$$
\pi r^2
$$

2. Circumference:

$$
2\pi r
$$

3. Arc length:

$$
\frac{\theta}{360^\circ}\times 2\pi r
$$

4. Area of sector:

$$
\frac{\theta}{360^\circ}\times \pi r^2
$$

5. Area of segment:

$$
\text{area of sector}-\text{area of triangle}
$$

6. A quadrant is one-fourth of a circle.
