## Formula Bank

## General Pair

For:

$$
a_1x + b_1y + c_1 = 0
$$

and:

$$
a_2x + b_2y + c_2 = 0
$$

## Intersecting Lines

If:

$$
\frac{a_1}{a_2} \ne \frac{b_1}{b_2}
$$

then the lines intersect at one point.

The pair is consistent and has a unique solution.

## Parallel Lines

If:

$$
\frac{a_1}{a_2} = \frac{b_1}{b_2} \ne \frac{c_1}{c_2}
$$

then the lines are parallel.

The pair is inconsistent and has no solution.

## Coincident Lines

If:

$$
\frac{a_1}{a_2} = \frac{b_1}{b_2} = \frac{c_1}{c_2}
$$

then the lines coincide.

The pair is dependent and consistent with infinitely many solutions.

# Unit 3A: Pair of Linear Equations

## What Is a Pair of Linear Equations?

A pair of linear equations in two variables consists of two equations involving the same two variables, usually `x` and `y`.

Example:

$$
x - 2y = 0
$$

$$
3x + 4y = 20
$$

A solution is an ordered pair `(x, y)` that satisfies both equations.

## Real-Life Example: Fair Ride and Game

Akhila takes rides on a Giant Wheel and plays Hoopla.

Let:

| Variable | Meaning |
|---|---|
| `x` | Number of Giant Wheel rides |
| `y` | Number of Hoopla games |

Given:

The number of Hoopla games is half the number of rides.

$$
y = \frac{x}{2}
$$

Each ride costs Rs 3 and each Hoopla game costs Rs 4.

Total spent is Rs 20.

$$
3x + 4y = 20
$$

So the pair is:

$$
y = \frac{x}{2}
$$

$$
3x + 4y = 20
$$

This pair can be solved by graph, substitution, or elimination.

## Standard Form

For comparison tests, write each equation in standard form:

$$
ax + by + c = 0
$$

Example:

$$
3x + 4y = 20
$$

becomes:

$$
3x + 4y - 20 = 0
$$

# Unit 3B: Graphical Method

## Graph of a Linear Equation

Every linear equation in two variables represents a straight line.

A pair of linear equations represents two straight lines.

The solution depends on how the two lines are placed.

## Three Possibilities

| Graphical Situation | Number of Solutions | Type of Pair |
|---|---:|---|
| Lines intersect at one point | One solution | Consistent |
| Lines are parallel | No solution | Inconsistent |
| Lines coincide | Infinitely many solutions | Dependent and consistent |

## Intersecting Lines

When two lines intersect at exactly one point, the coordinates of that point satisfy both equations.

Example:

$$
x - 2y = 0
$$

$$
3x + 4y - 20 = 0
$$

These lines intersect, so the pair has a unique solution.

## Parallel Lines

Parallel lines never meet.

So there is no ordered pair that satisfies both equations.

Example:

$$
x + 2y - 4 = 0
$$

$$
2x + 4y - 12 = 0
$$

These lines are parallel, so the pair has no solution.

## Coincident Lines

Coincident lines are the same line written in different forms.

Every point on the line satisfies both equations.

Example:

$$
2x + 3y - 9 = 0
$$

$$
4x + 6y - 18 = 0
$$

The second equation is twice the first, so the lines coincide.

# Unit 3C: Ratio Test for Solutions

## General Pair

Write the pair as:

$$
a_1x + b_1y + c_1 = 0
$$

$$
a_2x + b_2y + c_2 = 0
$$

Compare:

$$
\frac{a_1}{a_2}, \quad \frac{b_1}{b_2}, \quad \frac{c_1}{c_2}
$$

## Decision Table

| Condition | Graph | Solution Type |
|---|---|---|
| `a1/a2 != b1/b2` | Intersecting lines | Unique solution, consistent |
| `a1/a2 = b1/b2 != c1/c2` | Parallel lines | No solution, inconsistent |
| `a1/a2 = b1/b2 = c1/c2` | Coincident lines | Infinitely many solutions, dependent consistent |

## Example: Intersecting

For:

$$
x - 2y = 0
$$

$$
3x + 4y - 20 = 0
$$

Ratios:

$$
\frac{a_1}{a_2} = \frac{1}{3}
$$

$$
\frac{b_1}{b_2} = \frac{-2}{4} = -\frac{1}{2}
$$

Since:

$$
\frac{1}{3} \ne -\frac{1}{2}
$$

the lines intersect and the pair has one solution.

## Example: Coincident

For:

$$
2x + 3y - 9 = 0
$$

$$
4x + 6y - 18 = 0
$$

Ratios:

$$
\frac{2}{4} = \frac{3}{6} = \frac{-9}{-18} = \frac{1}{2}
$$

So the lines coincide and there are infinitely many solutions.

## Example: Parallel

For:

$$
x + 2y - 4 = 0
$$

$$
2x + 4y - 12 = 0
$$

Ratios:

$$
\frac{1}{2} = \frac{2}{4}
$$

but:

$$
\frac{-4}{-12} = \frac{1}{3}
$$

Since:

$$
\frac{a_1}{a_2} = \frac{b_1}{b_2} \ne \frac{c_1}{c_2}
$$

the lines are parallel and there is no solution.

# Unit 3D: Substitution Method

## Method

Use substitution when one variable can be easily expressed in terms of the other.

Steps:

1. Choose one equation.
2. Express one variable in terms of the other.
3. Substitute that expression in the second equation.
4. Solve the one-variable equation.
5. Substitute back to get the other variable.
6. Verify in both original equations.

## Worked Example

Solve:

$$
7x - 15y = 2
$$

$$
x + 2y = 3
$$

From the second equation:

$$
x = 3 - 2y
$$

Substitute in first equation:

$$
7(3 - 2y) - 15y = 2
$$

$$
21 - 14y - 15y = 2
$$

$$
-29y = -19
$$

$$
y = \frac{19}{29}
$$

Now:

$$
x = 3 - 2\left(\frac{19}{29}\right)
$$

$$
x = \frac{87 - 38}{29}
$$

$$
x = \frac{49}{29}
$$

Solution:

$$
x = \frac{49}{29}, \quad y = \frac{19}{29}
$$

## True Statement Case

If substitution leads to a true statement like:

$$
18 = 18
$$

then the equations represent the same line.

There are infinitely many solutions.

## False Statement Case

If substitution leads to a false statement like:

$$
-4 = 0
$$

then the equations represent parallel lines.

There is no solution.

# Unit 3E: Elimination Method

## Method

Use elimination when coefficients can be made equal or opposite.

Steps:

1. Multiply equations if needed so that one variable has equal or opposite coefficients.
2. Add or subtract equations to eliminate that variable.
3. Solve for the remaining variable.
4. Substitute back to find the other variable.
5. Verify the solution.

## Worked Example: Income and Expenditure

Income ratio is `9:7`, expenditure ratio is `4:3`, and both save Rs 2000.

Let incomes be:

$$
9x \quad \text{and} \quad 7x
$$

Let expenditures be:

$$
4y \quad \text{and} \quad 3y
$$

Savings give:

$$
9x - 4y = 2000
$$

$$
7x - 3y = 2000
$$

Multiply first by `3`:

$$
27x - 12y = 6000
$$

Multiply second by `4`:

$$
28x - 12y = 8000
$$

Subtract:

$$
x = 2000
$$

Substitute:

$$
9(2000) - 4y = 2000
$$

$$
18000 - 4y = 2000
$$

$$
y = 4000
$$

Incomes:

$$
9x = 18000
$$

$$
7x = 14000
$$

**Answer:** Monthly incomes are Rs `18000` and Rs `14000`.

# Unit 3F: Word Problem Translation Guide

## Cost Problems

Use:

| Variable | Meaning |
|---|---|
| `x` | cost of one item |
| `y` | cost of another item |

Then write total cost equations.

Example:

`5 pencils and 7 pens cost Rs 50` becomes:

$$
5x + 7y = 50
$$

## Age Problems

Use present ages as variables.

Translate:

| Phrase | Algebra |
|---|---|
| Five years ago | subtract 5 |
| Five years hence | add 5 |
| Three times | multiply by 3 |
| Seven times | multiply by 7 |

## Digit Problems

For a two-digit number:

| Variable | Meaning |
|---|---|
| `x` | tens digit |
| `y` | units digit |

Number:

$$
10x + y
$$

Reversed number:

$$
10y + x
$$

## Fraction Problems

Let the fraction be:

$$
\frac{x}{y}
$$

Then translate changes to numerator and denominator carefully.

Example:

If 2 is added to both numerator and denominator:

$$
\frac{x+2}{y+2}
$$

# Unit 3J: Common Mistakes and Corrections

| Mistake | Correct Understanding |
|---|---|
| Forgetting to write equations in standard form before ratio test. | Use `a x + b y + c = 0`. |
| Comparing constants without signs. | Keep signs of `c1` and `c2`. |
| Saying parallel lines have one solution. | Parallel distinct lines have no solution. |
| Saying coincident lines have no solution. | Coincident lines have infinitely many solutions. |
| Reading graph inaccurately for fractional solutions. | Use algebraic methods when graph coordinates are not clear. |
| Substituting into the same equation only. | Substitute into the other equation to solve. |
| Eliminating without matching coefficients. | First make coefficients equal or opposite. |
| In digit problems, writing number as `x + y`. | Two-digit number is `10x + y`. |
| In age problems, mixing present and past ages. | Define present ages first, then add or subtract years. |

## One-Page Chapter Summary

A pair of linear equations in two variables represents two straight lines. If the lines intersect, the pair has a unique solution and is consistent. If the lines are parallel, the pair has no solution and is inconsistent. If the lines coincide, the pair has infinitely many solutions and is dependent consistent.

The ratio test uses coefficients from `a1x + b1y + c1 = 0` and `a2x + b2y + c2 = 0`. If `a1/a2 != b1/b2`, the lines intersect. If `a1/a2 = b1/b2 != c1/c2`, the lines are parallel. If all three ratios are equal, the lines coincide.

Pairs of equations can be solved graphically, by substitution, or by elimination. Substitution is useful when one variable can be easily expressed in terms of the other. Elimination is useful when coefficients can be made equal or opposite. In word problems, define variables carefully and translate each condition into one equation.
