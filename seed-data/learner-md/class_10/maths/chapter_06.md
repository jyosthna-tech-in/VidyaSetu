## Theorem Bank

## Basic Proportionality Theorem

If a line is drawn parallel to one side of a triangle and intersects the other two sides in distinct points, then it divides the other two sides in the same ratio.

In `triangle ABC`, if `DE || BC`, with `D` on `AB` and `E` on `AC`, then:

$$
\frac{AD}{DB} = \frac{AE}{EC}
$$

## Converse of Basic Proportionality Theorem

If a line divides two sides of a triangle in the same ratio, then the line is parallel to the third side.

In `triangle ABC`, if:

$$
\frac{AD}{DB} = \frac{AE}{EC}
$$

then:

$$
DE || BC
$$

## AA or AAA Similarity

If two angles of one triangle are respectively equal to two angles of another triangle, then the triangles are similar.

## SSS Similarity

If corresponding sides of two triangles are proportional, then the triangles are similar.

## SAS Similarity

If one angle of a triangle is equal to one angle of another triangle and the sides including those angles are proportional, then the triangles are similar.

# Unit 6A: Similar Figures

## Similar vs Congruent

| Type | Shape | Size |
|---|---|---|
| Congruent figures | Same | Same |
| Similar figures | Same | May be different |

All congruent figures are similar.

But all similar figures are not necessarily congruent.

## Examples

| Figures | Similar? | Reason |
|---|---|---|
| Any two circles | Yes | Same shape; radius may differ. |
| Any two squares | Yes | Same angles and proportional sides. |
| Any two equilateral triangles | Yes | Same angles and proportional sides. |
| A circle and a square | No | Different shape. |
| A square and a rectangle | Not always | Angles equal, but side ratios may differ. |
| A square and a rhombus | Not always | Sides proportional, but angles may differ. |

## Similar Polygons

Two polygons with the same number of sides are similar if:

1. Corresponding angles are equal.
2. Corresponding sides are in the same ratio.

Both conditions are needed for polygons in general.

# Unit 6B: Similarity of Triangles

## Similar Triangles

Two triangles are similar if:

1. Corresponding angles are equal.
2. Corresponding sides are proportional.

For triangles, we do not always need to check all six conditions. Similarity criteria let us prove similarity using fewer facts.

## Correct Correspondence Matters

If:

$$
\triangle ABC \sim \triangle DEF
$$

then the correspondence is:

| Triangle ABC | Triangle DEF |
|---|---|
| `A` | `D` |
| `B` | `E` |
| `C` | `F` |

So:

$$
\frac{AB}{DE} = \frac{BC}{EF} = \frac{CA}{FD}
$$

and:

$$
\angle A = \angle D,\quad \angle B = \angle E,\quad \angle C = \angle F
$$

Wrong correspondence gives wrong ratios.

# Unit 6C: Basic Proportionality Theorem

## Statement in Learner Form

In a triangle, if a line is parallel to one side, then it cuts the other two sides proportionally.

If:

$$
DE || BC
$$

then:

$$
\frac{AD}{DB} = \frac{AE}{EC}
$$

## Proof Idea

The proof uses areas of triangles.

1. Join `BE` and `CD`.
2. Draw perpendiculars from `D` and `E`.
3. Compare areas of triangles having same altitude.
4. Use the fact that triangles between the same parallels and on the same base have equal areas.
5. Conclude that the side ratios are equal.

## Common Uses

Use BPT when:

1. A line is given parallel to one side of a triangle.
2. You need to find a missing side segment.
3. You need to prove a ratio.

---

# Unit 6D: Converse of BPT

## Statement in Learner Form

If a line cuts two sides of a triangle in the same ratio, then it is parallel to the third side.

If:

$$
\frac{AD}{DB} = \frac{AE}{EC}
$$

then:

$$
DE || BC
$$

## Common Uses

Use converse of BPT when:

1. You are given two equal ratios.
2. You are asked to prove two lines parallel.
3. The figure contains a smaller triangle inside a larger triangle.

# Unit 6E: Criteria for Similarity of Triangles

## AA or AAA Similarity

If two angles of one triangle are equal to two angles of another triangle, the third pair is also equal by angle sum property.

Therefore the triangles are similar.

Use AA similarity when you see:

1. Parallel lines giving alternate or corresponding angles.
2. Vertically opposite angles.
3. Right angles.
4. Common angle.

## SSS Similarity

If:

$$
\frac{AB}{DE} = \frac{BC}{EF} = \frac{CA}{FD}
$$

then:

$$
\triangle ABC \sim \triangle DEF
$$

Use SSS similarity when side lengths are given.

## SAS Similarity

If:

$$
\frac{AB}{DE} = \frac{AC}{DF}
$$

and:

$$
\angle A = \angle D
$$

then:

$$
\triangle ABC \sim \triangle DEF
$$

Use SAS similarity when two sides around an angle are proportional and the included angle is equal.

# Unit 6F: Worked Examples Rebuilt

## Example: Line Parallel to One Side

Given `DE || BC` in `triangle ABC`.

By BPT:

$$
\frac{AD}{DB} = \frac{AE}{EC}
$$

From this, we can also prove:

$$
\frac{AD}{AB} = \frac{AE}{AC}
$$

because:

$$
AB = AD + DB
$$

and:

$$
AC = AE + EC
$$

## Example: Similar Triangles from Parallel Lines

If:

$$
PQ || RS
$$

then alternate interior angles are equal.

Also, vertically opposite angles are equal.

So:

$$
\triangle POQ \sim \triangle SOR
$$

by AAA similarity.

## Example: Shadow Problem

Girl's height:

$$
90 \text{ cm} = 0.9 \text{ m}
$$

Lamp height:

$$
3.6 \text{ m}
$$

Girl walks for 4 seconds at `1.2 m/s`, so distance from lamp post:

$$
1.2 \times 4 = 4.8 \text{ m}
$$

Let shadow length be `x`.

By similar triangles:

$$
\frac{4.8 + x}{x} = \frac{3.6}{0.9}
$$

$$
\frac{4.8 + x}{x} = 4
$$

$$
4.8 + x = 4x
$$

$$
x = 1.6
$$

Shadow length is `1.6 m`.

\frac{AM}{AB} = \frac{AL}{AC}

$$
LN || CD

$$
\frac{AN}{AD} = \frac{AL}{AC}

$$
DE || AC
$$

and:

$$
DF || AE

1. From `DE || AC`, get a ratio involving `BE` and `EC`.
2. From `DF || AE`, get a ratio involving `BF` and `FE`.

Therefore:

$$
\frac{BF}{FE} = \frac{BE}{EC}

## Question 7: Midpoint and Parallel Line

Let `D` be midpoint of `AB`, so:

$$
AD = DB
$$

Draw `DE || BC`, meeting `AC` at `E`.

By BPT:

$$
\frac{AD}{DB} = \frac{AE}{EC}
$$

Since:

$$
\frac{AD}{DB} = 1
$$

we get:

$$
\frac{AE}{EC} = 1
$$

So:

$$
AE = EC
$$

Thus the line bisects the third side.

## Question 8: Line Joining Midpoints

Let `D` and `E` be midpoints of `AB` and `AC`.

Then:

$$
AD = DB
$$

and:

$$
AE = EC
$$

So:

$$
\frac{AD}{DB} = \frac{AE}{EC}
$$

By converse of BPT:

$$
DE || BC
$$

## Question 9: Diagonals of Trapezium

In trapezium `ABCD`, let:

$$
AB || DC
$$

Diagonals intersect at `O`.

Consider:

$$
\triangle AOB
$$

and:

$$
\triangle COD
$$

They are similar by AA:

1. Alternate interior angles because `AB || DC`.
2. Vertically opposite angles at `O`.

Therefore:

$$
\frac{AO}{CO} = \frac{BO}{DO}
$$

## Question 10: Converse - Prove Trapezium

Given diagonals intersect at `O` and:

$$
\frac{AO}{CO} = \frac{BO}{DO}
$$

This proportional division allows the converse similarity argument to show:

$$
\triangle AOB \sim \triangle COD
$$

Hence corresponding alternate angles are equal.

Therefore:

$$
AB || DC
$$

So `ABCD` is a trapezium.

## Question 12: Sides and Median Proportional

Given:

$$
\frac{AB}{PQ} = \frac{BC}{QR} = \frac{AD}{PM}
$$

where `AD` and `PM` are medians.

$$

So:

$$
\frac{BD}{QM} = \frac{BC}{QR}

Thus:

$$
\frac{AB}{PQ} = \frac{BD}{QM} = \frac{AD}{PM}

By SSS:

$$
\triangle ABD \sim \triangle PQM

$$
\angle ABC = \angle PQR

$$
\triangle ABC \sim \triangle PQR

## Question 13: Prove `CA^2 = CB.CD`

$$
\angle ADC = \angle BAC
$$

Also:

$$
\angle ACD = \angle BCA
$$

So:

$$
\triangle ADC \sim \triangle BAC
$$

Therefore:

$$
\frac{CA}{CB} = \frac{CD}{CA}

## Question 14: Sides and Median Proportional

Given:

$$
\frac{AB}{PQ} = \frac{AC}{PR} = \frac{AD}{PM}
$$

where `AD` and `PM` are medians.

Proof strategy:

1. Extend `AD` to `E` so that `AD = DE`; join `CE`.
2. Extend `PM` to `N` so that `PM = MN`; join `RN`.
3. Use congruence to get `AB = CE` and `PQ = RN`.
4. Use SSS similarity on the constructed triangles.
5. Conclude `angle A = angle P`.
6. Use SAS similarity in `triangle ABC` and `triangle PQR`.

Therefore:

$$
\triangle ABC \sim \triangle PQR
$$

## Question 16: Medians in Similar Triangles

Given:

$$
\triangle ABC \sim \triangle PQR
$$

and medians `AD` and `PM`.

From similarity:

$$
\frac{AB}{PQ} = \frac{BC}{QR}
$$

Since medians bisect:

$$
\frac{BD}{QM} = \frac{BC/2}{QR/2} = \frac{BC}{QR}

Thus:

$$
\frac{AB}{PQ} = \frac{BD}{QM}
$$

Also:

$$
\angle ABD = \angle PQM
$$

By SAS similarity:

$$
\triangle ABD \sim \triangle PQM
$$

Therefore:

$$
\frac{AB}{PQ} = \frac{AD}{PM}
$$

# Unit 6J: Common Mistakes and Corrections

| Mistake | Correct Understanding |
|---|---|
| Writing triangle similarity in the wrong order. | Match corresponding vertices carefully. |
| Using BPT without a parallel line. | BPT needs a line parallel to one side of a triangle. |
| Using converse BPT without equal ratios. | Converse needs proportional division of two sides. |
| Thinking equal angles alone prove polygon similarity for all polygons. | For polygons generally, need equal angles and proportional sides. |
| Forgetting triangles are special. | For triangles, AA alone is enough for similarity. |
| Confusing congruent and similar. | Congruent means same shape and size; similar means same shape. |
| Not justifying angle equality. | State reason: alternate angles, corresponding angles, vertically opposite angles, common angle, or right angle. |
| Using side ratios without correspondence. | Compare matching sides only. |

## One-Page Chapter Summary

Similar figures have the same shape but not necessarily the same size. Similar polygons have equal corresponding angles and proportional corresponding sides. For triangles, fewer checks are enough because AA, SSS, and SAS criteria establish similarity.

The Basic Proportionality Theorem says that a line parallel to one side of a triangle divides the other two sides in the same ratio. Its converse says that if a line divides two sides of a triangle in the same ratio, it is parallel to the third side.

Most proofs in this chapter follow a pattern: identify parallel lines, equal angles, or proportional sides; choose the correct theorem; then write the similarity statement in correct vertex order. Similar triangles can then be used to prove side ratios, find missing lengths, and solve indirect measurement problems such as heights from shadows.
