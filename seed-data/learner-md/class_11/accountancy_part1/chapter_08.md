# Chapter 8: Bill of Exchange
### NCERT Class 11 - Accountancy Part 1

---

## Chapter Purpose

This chapter explains bills of exchange, promissory notes, and their accounting treatment. These instruments are used when goods are sold on credit and the seller wants stronger assurance that the buyer will pay on a fixed future date.

The chapter is practical. You should study it through this sequence:

```text
credit sale -> instrument drawn -> acceptance/promise -> maturity -> honour or dishonour -> journal entries
```

# Part 1: Credit Sales And Negotiable Instruments

## Why Bills Are Used

Goods may be sold:

1. for cash, where payment is immediate,
2. on credit, where payment is delayed.

In credit sales, the seller may want written assurance that payment will be made on a specified date. Bills of exchange and promissory notes provide that assurance.

---

## Hundies

Traditional Indian credit instruments were called hundies. Examples include:

| Type | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| Shahjog hundi | payable to a respectable and responsible person called Shah |
| Darshani hundi | payable at sight; similar to demand bill |
| Muddati or miadi hundi | payable after a specified period; similar to time bill |

Modern business uses bills of exchange and promissory notes, governed by the Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881.

---

# Part 2: Bill Of Exchange

## Meaning

A bill of exchange is a written instrument containing an unconditional order, signed by the maker, directing a certain person to pay a certain sum of money to a certain person or to that person's order.

In simple words:

```text
The creditor orders the debtor to pay a fixed amount on a fixed date.
```

---

## Features Of A Bill Of Exchange

| Feature | Explanation |
| --- | --- |
| written instrument | oral order is not enough |
| unconditional order | payment cannot depend on uncertain conditions |
| signed by maker | drawer must sign |
| certain amount | amount payable must be clear |
| certain date | payment date must be determinable |
| certain payee | person to receive payment must be identifiable |
| payable on demand or after fixed period | timing must be clear |
| stamped | must satisfy legal stamp requirement |
| accepted by drawee | draft becomes bill only after acceptance |

---

## Draft And Accepted Bill

Before acceptance, the document is only a draft.

It becomes a bill of exchange when the drawee writes acceptance and signs it.

Example:

```text
Amit sells goods to Rohit for Rs. 10,000 on three months' credit.
Amit draws a bill on Rohit.
Rohit accepts and signs it.
Now the draft becomes a bill of exchange.
```

---

## Parties To A Bill Of Exchange

| Party | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| Drawer | person who draws the bill; usually creditor/seller |
| Drawee | person on whom bill is drawn; usually debtor/buyer |
| Payee | person who receives payment |

Normally, drawer and payee are the same person. But payee can change if the bill is discounted or endorsed.

---

## Acceptor

The drawee becomes the acceptor after accepting the bill.

If another person accepts on behalf of the drawee, that person is the acceptor.

---

# Part 3: Promissory Note

## Meaning

A promissory note is a written instrument containing an unconditional promise, signed by the maker, to pay a certain sum of money to a certain person or to that person's order.

In simple words:

```text
The debtor promises in writing to pay the creditor.
```

---

## Features Of A Promissory Note

| Feature | Explanation |
| --- | --- |
| written instrument | must be in writing |
| unconditional promise | not dependent on uncertain event |
| certain amount | amount must be clear |
| signed by maker | maker/promisor signs it |
| certain payee | person to receive payment is clear |
| stamped | proper stamp required |
| no acceptance needed | maker already promises to pay |

Promissory notes payable to bearer are not allowed under the relevant law.

---

## Parties To A Promissory Note

| Party | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| Maker or promisor | person who promises to pay |
| Payee or promisee | person to whom payment is promised |

There are only two parties, unlike a bill of exchange which normally has three.

---

## Bill Of Exchange Versus Promissory Note

| Basis | Bill Of Exchange | Promissory Note |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Drawer | drawn by creditor | drawn by debtor |
| Nature | contains order to pay | contains promise to pay |
| Parties | drawer, drawee, payee | maker and payee |
| Acceptance | requires acceptance | does not require acceptance |
| Payee | drawer and payee can be same | maker cannot be payee |
| Dishonour notice | notice of dishonour is given | no notice to drawer needed |

---

# Part 4: Advantages Of Bill Of Exchange

## Main Advantages

| Advantage | Explanation |
| --- | --- |
| framework for credit | creates clear relationship between creditor and debtor |
| certainty | amount, due date, interest, and place can be specified |
| convenient credit | buyer gets time; seller has written claim |
| early cash option | seller can discount bill with bank |
| transferable | bill can be endorsed to another person |
| legal evidence | bill is proof of credit transaction |

---

# Part 5: Maturity Of A Bill

## Meaning

Maturity is the date on which the bill or promissory note becomes due for payment.

---

## Days Of Grace

Usually, three days of grace are added to the period of the bill.

Example:

```text
Bill dated March 5, payable 30 days after date.
Add 30 days + 3 days of grace.
Due date = April 7.
```

If the bill is payable one month after March 5:

```text
one month after date = April 5
add 3 days of grace = April 8
```

---

## Holiday Rule

| Situation | Maturity Date |
| --- | --- |
| due date is public holiday | preceding business day |
| due date is emergency holiday declared under Negotiable Instruments Act | next working day |

---

# Part 6: Discounting Of Bill

## Meaning

Discounting means getting the bill encashed from a bank before maturity.

The bank pays the holder after deducting discount.

```text
bank pays = bill amount - discount
```

On maturity, the bank collects the full amount from the acceptor.

---

## Accounting Effect

For the drawer:

```text
Bank A/c Dr.
Discount A/c Dr.
    To Bills Receivable A/c
```

No entry is made by the drawer on maturity if the bill is honoured, because the bank owns the bill after discounting.

---

# Part 7: Endorsement Of Bill

## Meaning

Endorsement is signing and transferring a bill to another person.

The holder signs on the back of the bill and transfers it to a creditor or another party.

---

## Entry In Drawer/Endorser's Books

```text
Creditor's A/c Dr.
    To Bills Receivable A/c
```

No entry is made on maturity in the endorser's books if the bill is honoured.

---

# Part 8: Basic Accounting Treatment

## Key Accounts

| Account | Nature |
| --- | --- |
| Bills Receivable | asset |
| Bills Payable | liability |
| Discount | expense for drawer when bill is discounted |
| Rebate | allowed by holder when bill is retired early |
| Noting Charges | expense ultimately borne by drawee |

---

## In Drawer/Creditor's Books

### On Credit Sale

```text
Debtor's A/c Dr.
    To Sales A/c
```

### On Receiving Accepted Bill

```text
Bills Receivable A/c Dr.
    To Debtor's A/c
```

### If Bill Is Retained And Honoured

```text
Bank/Cash A/c Dr.
    To Bills Receivable A/c
```

### If Bill Is Sent For Collection

When sent:

```text
Bills Sent for Collection A/c Dr.
    To Bills Receivable A/c
```

When collected:

```text
Bank A/c Dr.
    To Bills Sent for Collection A/c
```

### If Bill Is Discounted

```text
Bank A/c Dr.
Discount A/c Dr.
    To Bills Receivable A/c
```

### If Bill Is Endorsed

```text
Creditor's A/c Dr.
    To Bills Receivable A/c
```

---

## In Drawee/Acceptor's Books

### On Credit Purchase

```text
Purchases A/c Dr.
    To Creditor's A/c
```

### On Accepting Bill

```text
Creditor's A/c Dr.
    To Bills Payable A/c
```

### On Honour At Maturity

```text
Bills Payable A/c Dr.
    To Bank/Cash A/c
```

This entry is the same whether the drawer retained, discounted, endorsed, or sent the bill for collection.

---

# Part 9: Dishonour Of A Bill

## Meaning

A bill is dishonoured when the drawee fails to pay on the maturity date.

When dishonour happens, the acceptor's liability is restored. Entries made earlier for the bill are reversed in substance.

---

## Drawer/Creditor's Entries On Dishonour

### If Bill Was Retained

```text
Drawee's A/c Dr.
    To Bills Receivable A/c
```

### If Bill Was Discounted

```text
Drawee's A/c Dr.
    To Bank A/c
```

### If Bill Was Endorsed

```text
Drawee's A/c Dr.
    To Endorsee/Creditor's A/c
```

### If Bill Was Sent For Collection

```text
Drawee's A/c Dr.
    To Bills Sent for Collection A/c
```

---

## Drawee's Entry On Dishonour

```text
Bills Payable A/c Dr.
    To Drawer/Creditor's A/c
```

If noting charges are involved, include them in the creditor's account as explained below.

---

# Part 10: Noting Charges

## Meaning

When a bill is dishonoured, it may be presented to a Notary Public for noting. Noting provides evidence that the bill was properly presented and dishonoured.

The fee charged by the Notary Public is called noting charges.

---

## Facts Usually Noted

1. date of dishonour,
2. fact of dishonour,
3. reason for dishonour,
4. amount of noting charges.

---

## Who Bears Noting Charges?

The person who causes dishonour, the drawee/acceptor, ultimately bears noting charges.

Even if the holder, bank, or endorsee pays first, the drawer debits the drawee.

---

## Drawer/Creditor's Entry For Noting Charges

If drawer pays:

```text
Drawee's A/c Dr.
    To Cash A/c
```

If bank pays:

```text
Drawee's A/c Dr.
    To Bank A/c
```

If endorsee pays:

```text
Drawee's A/c Dr.
    To Endorsee's A/c
```

---

## Drawee's Entry Including Noting Charges

```text
Bills Payable A/c Dr.
Noting Charges A/c Dr.
    To Drawer/Creditor's A/c
```

---

# Part 11: Renewal Of Bill

## Meaning

Renewal happens when the acceptor cannot pay on maturity and requests more time.

The old bill is cancelled and a new bill is drawn and accepted.

---

## Important Points

1. Since cancellation is by mutual agreement, noting is not required.
2. Interest may be charged for the extended period.
3. Interest may be paid in cash or included in the new bill.
4. Sometimes part payment is made and new bill is drawn for the balance plus interest.

---

## Entries In Drawer/Creditor's Books

### Cancel Old Bill

```text
Drawee's A/c Dr.
    To Bills Receivable A/c
```

### Charge Interest

```text
Drawee's A/c Dr.
    To Interest A/c
```

### If Interest Is Received In Cash

```text
Cash A/c Dr.
    To Drawee's A/c
```

### Receive New Bill

```text
Bills Receivable A/c Dr.
    To Drawee's A/c
```

---

## Entries In Drawee/Debtor's Books

### Cancel Old Bill

```text
Bills Payable A/c Dr.
    To Drawer/Creditor's A/c
```

### Interest Charged

```text
Interest A/c Dr.
    To Drawer/Creditor's A/c
```

### If Interest Paid In Cash

```text
Drawer/Creditor's A/c Dr.
    To Cash A/c
```

### Accept New Bill

```text
Drawer/Creditor's A/c Dr.
    To Bills Payable A/c
```

---

# Part 12: Retiring Of Bill

## Meaning

Retiring a bill means paying it before the due date by mutual agreement.

The holder may allow a discount for early payment. This discount is called rebate.

---

## Rebate

Rebate is discount allowed to the drawee for paying the bill before maturity.

It is calculated for the unexpired period between retirement date and maturity date.

---

## Holder/Drawer's Entry

```text
Cash/Bank A/c Dr.
Rebate on Bills A/c Dr.
    To Bills Receivable A/c
```

---

## Drawee's Entry

```text
Bills Payable A/c Dr.
    To Cash/Bank A/c
    To Rebate on Bills A/c
```

---

# Part 13: Bills Receivable Book

## Meaning

Bills Receivable Book records details of bills accepted by debtors and received by the business.

It is a subsidiary book used when bills are numerous.

---

## Details Recorded

Typical columns include:

| Detail |
| --- |
| bill number |
| date received |
| date of bill |
| from whom received |
| drawer |
| acceptor |
| place payable |
| term |
| due date |
| ledger folio |
| amount |
| cash book folio |
| remarks |

---

## Posting

Periodical total:

```text
Bills Receivable A/c Dr.
```

Individual debtors are credited.

Bills Receivable Account is an asset account and normally has a debit balance.

---

# Part 14: Bills Payable Book

## Meaning

Bills Payable Book records details of bills accepted by the business and payable in future.

---

## Details Recorded

Typical columns include:

| Detail |
| --- |
| bill number |
| date of bill |
| to whom given |
| drawer |
| payee |
| place payable |
| term |
| due date |
| ledger folio |
| amount |
| date paid |
| cash book folio |
| remarks |

---

## Posting

Individual creditors are debited.

Periodical total:

```text
Bills Payable A/c Cr.
```

Bills Payable Account is a liability account and normally has a credit balance.

# Part 15: Accommodation Bills

## Meaning

An accommodation bill is drawn and accepted not for a trade transaction, but to provide temporary financial help.

There is no real sale or purchase behind it.

---

## Purpose

Parties use the bill to raise funds from a bank by discounting it. The proceeds may be used by one party or shared between parties.

---

## Basic Flow

```text
Party A draws bill on Party B.
Party B accepts it.
Party A discounts bill with bank.
Proceeds are used or shared.
Before maturity, party responsible for payment sends funds to acceptor.
Acceptor honours the bill.
```

---

## Important Caution

Accommodation bills are different from trade bills.

| Trade Bill | Accommodation Bill |
| --- | --- |
| based on actual credit sale/purchase | based on financial accommodation |
| drawer is creditor | drawer may not be actual creditor |
| liability arises from trade | liability arises from mutual arrangement |

# Part 16: Quick Journal Entry Board

## Bill Received And Honoured

| Event | Drawer/Creditor | Drawee/Debtor |
| --- | --- | --- |
| credit sale/purchase | `Debtor Dr. To Sales` | `Purchases Dr. To Creditor` |
| bill accepted | `Bills Receivable Dr. To Debtor` | `Creditor Dr. To Bills Payable` |
| bill honoured | `Bank Dr. To Bills Receivable` | `Bills Payable Dr. To Bank` |

---

## Bill Discounted

| Event | Drawer/Creditor | Drawee/Debtor |
| --- | --- | --- |
| discounting | `Bank Dr.; Discount Dr.; To Bills Receivable` | no entry |
| honoured at maturity | no entry | `Bills Payable Dr. To Bank` |
| dishonoured | `Drawee Dr. To Bank` | `Bills Payable Dr. To Drawer` |

---

## Bill Endorsed

| Event | Drawer/Endorser | Drawee |
| --- | --- | --- |
| endorsement | `Creditor Dr. To Bills Receivable` | no entry |
| honoured | no entry | `Bills Payable Dr. To Bank` |
| dishonoured | `Drawee Dr. To Creditor/Endorsee` | `Bills Payable Dr. To Drawer` |

---

## Bill Sent For Collection

| Event | Drawer/Creditor | Drawee |
| --- | --- | --- |
| sent to bank | `Bills Sent for Collection Dr. To Bills Receivable` | no entry |
| collected | `Bank Dr. To Bills Sent for Collection` | `Bills Payable Dr. To Bank` |
| dishonoured | `Drawee Dr. To Bills Sent for Collection` | `Bills Payable Dr. To Drawer` |

# Part 17: Common Mistakes

## Mistake 1: Confusing Bill And Promissory Note

Bill contains an order by creditor. Promissory note contains a promise by debtor.

---

## Mistake 2: Forgetting Acceptance

A bill of exchange requires acceptance by the drawee. A promissory note does not.

---

## Mistake 3: Forgetting Days Of Grace

Add three days of grace while calculating maturity unless the instrument is payable on demand or the specific rule says otherwise.

---

## Mistake 4: Recording Drawer Entry On Maturity After Discounting

If the bill is discounted and honoured, the drawer records no entry at maturity.

---

## Mistake 5: Charging Noting Charges To Wrong Person

Noting charges are ultimately borne by drawee because dishonour is caused by failure to pay.

---

## Mistake 6: Treating Rebate As Discounting

Discounting happens before maturity through bank. Rebate is allowed when drawee retires the bill before maturity.

# Part 18: Answer-Writing Frames

## Define Bill Of Exchange

```text
A bill of exchange is a written instrument containing an unconditional order, signed by the maker, directing a certain person to pay a certain sum of money to a certain person or to that person's order.
It is drawn by the creditor on the debtor and requires acceptance by the drawee.
```

---

## Define Promissory Note

```text
A promissory note is a written instrument containing an unconditional promise, signed by the maker, to pay a certain sum of money to a certain person or to that person's order.
It is made by the debtor and does not require acceptance.
```

---

## Explain Dishonour

```text
A bill is dishonoured when the drawee fails to pay on the maturity date.
The liability of the acceptor is restored, and entries are passed to reverse the bill receivable/payable effect.
If noting charges are paid, they are ultimately debited to the drawee.
```

---

## Explain Renewal

```text
Renewal of a bill means cancelling the old bill by mutual agreement and drawing a new bill for an extended period.
Interest may be charged for the extended credit period and may be paid in cash or included in the new bill.
```
