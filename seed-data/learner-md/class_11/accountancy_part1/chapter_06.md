### NCERT Class 11 - Accountancy Part 1

## Chapter at a Glance

This chapter has two connected parts.

First, it explains the trial balance. A trial balance is a statement that lists ledger account balances to check whether total debits equal total credits.

Second, it explains errors. If the trial balance does not agree, errors must be located and corrected. Even if the trial balance agrees, some errors can still remain hidden. Therefore, a learner must understand both the use and the limitations of trial balance.

# Unit 6A: Meaning of Trial Balance

## Definition

A trial balance is a statement showing the balances, or debit and credit totals, of all ledger accounts on a particular date.

Its main purpose is to verify arithmetical accuracy under the double entry system.

## Why Trial Balance Works

Double entry accounting says:

`Total debits = Total credits`

If every transaction has been posted with equal debit and credit, the total of debit balances should equal the total of credit balances.

## Trial Balance Format

| Account Title | L.F. | Debit Balance Rs. | Credit Balance Rs. |
|---|---|---:|---:|
| Cash |  | xxx |  |
| Capital |  |  | xxx |
| Purchases |  | xxx |  |
| Sales |  |  | xxx |
| Total |  | xxx | xxx |

## When Trial Balance Is Prepared

Trial balance is usually prepared:

1. at the end of the accounting year,
2. at the end of a month,
3. at the end of a quarter,
4. before preparing financial statements,
5. whenever ledger accuracy needs checking.

## Steps to Prepare Trial Balance

1. Balance every ledger account.
2. Identify whether each balance is debit or credit.
3. Write account names in the trial balance.
4. Put debit balances in the debit column.
5. Put credit balances in the credit column.
6. Total both columns.
7. Check whether debit total equals credit total.
8. If not equal, search for errors.

# Unit 6C: Objectives of Trial Balance

## 1. To Check Arithmetical Accuracy

Trial balance checks whether ledger postings and balances are arithmetically correct.

If debit total equals credit total, it suggests that:

1. both sides of entries were posted,
2. amounts were probably posted correctly,
3. ledger balances were probably calculated correctly,
4. trial balance totals were probably added correctly.

However, it does not prove that every transaction is correct.

## 2. To Help Locate Errors

If trial balance does not agree, at least one error exists. The difference helps the accountant search for errors.

Examples:

1. If difference is Rs. 1,500, a posting of Rs. 1,500 may be omitted.
2. If difference is divisible by 2, half the difference may have been posted to wrong side.
3. If difference is divisible by 9, there may be transposition of digits.

## 3. To Help Prepare Financial Statements

Trial balance collects all ledger balances in one statement. This helps prepare:

1. trading account,
2. profit and loss account,
3. balance sheet.

Revenue and expense accounts go to profit and loss. Asset, liability, and capital accounts go to balance sheet.

# Unit 6D: Limitations of Trial Balance

## Trial Balance Agreement Is Not Final Proof

A trial balance may agree even when errors exist.

This happens when debit and credit equality is not disturbed.

## Errors Not Revealed by Trial Balance

| Error | Example | Why Trial Balance Still Agrees |
|---|---|---|
| Complete omission | Credit sale not recorded at all | Both debit and credit omitted |
| Error of principle | Furniture purchase debited to Purchases A/c | Debit made, credit made |
| Wrong account | Cash received from A posted to B | Same side, same amount |
| Compensating errors | Purchases overcast and sales return undercast by same amount | One error cancels another |
| Wrong original amount | Sale of Rs. 5,000 recorded as Rs. 500 in both accounts | Equal wrong debit and credit |
| Complete reversal | Cash received recorded as Cash credited and debtor debited | Equal debit and credit, though reversed |

## Errors Revealed by Trial Balance

| Error | Example |
|---|---|
| Posting only one side | Sales posted to Sales A/c but not to debtor |
| Posting wrong side | Debtor credited instead of debited |
| Posting wrong amount on one side | Debtor debited Rs. 700 instead of Rs. 7,000 |
| Wrong total of subsidiary book | Purchases book overcast |
| Wrong balance carried to trial balance | Debit balance put in credit column |
| Omission of ledger balance | Rent account omitted from trial balance |

# Unit 6E: Methods of Preparing Trial Balance

## 1. Totals Method

Under totals method, total of debit side and total of credit side of each ledger account are shown in trial balance.

Format:

| Account | Debit Total Rs. | Credit Total Rs. |
|---|---:|---:|
| Cash | 1,00,000 | 57,000 |
| Sales |  | 70,000 |
| Purchases | 62,000 |  |

Use:

This method checks total postings but is less useful for financial statements.

## 2. Balances Method

Under balances method, only closing balances of ledger accounts are shown.

Format:

| Account | Debit Balance Rs. | Credit Balance Rs. |
|---|---:|---:|
| Cash | 43,000 |  |
| Capital |  | 60,000 |
| Sales |  | 70,000 |

Use:

This is the most widely used method because financial statements need balances.

## 3. Totals-Cum-Balances Method

This method shows both:

1. debit and credit totals,
2. debit and credit balances.

Format:

| Account | Debit Total Rs. | Credit Total Rs. | Debit Balance Rs. | Credit Balance Rs. |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| Cash | 1,00,000 | 57,000 | 43,000 |  |

Use:

It gives more information but is time consuming.

# Unit 6G: Classification of Errors

## Four Main Types

| Error Type | Meaning | Trial Balance Effect |
|---|---|---|
| Error of commission | Clerical mistake in recording, posting, casting, or balancing | May affect or may not affect |
| Error of omission | Transaction omitted fully or partly | Complete omission does not affect; partial omission affects |
| Error of principle | Accounting principle violated | Usually does not affect |
| Compensating errors | Two or more errors cancel each other | Does not affect |

## Errors of Commission

These are clerical errors.

Examples:

1. posting Rs. 25,000 as Rs. 2,500,
2. posting to wrong account,
3. posting on wrong side,
4. overcasting sales book,
5. undercasting purchases book,
6. wrong balancing of an account.

Example:

Payment to Preetpal Traders Rs. 25,000 is posted to Preetpal A/c as Rs. 2,500 only. This is an error of commission.

## Errors of Omission

### Complete Omission

The whole transaction is not recorded.

Example:

Credit sales to Mohan Rs. 10,000 not entered in sales book.

Effect:

Mohan is not debited and Sales is not credited. Trial balance still agrees because both sides are omitted.

### Partial Omission

Only one part of the transaction is omitted.

Example:

Credit sales are recorded in sales book but not posted to Mohan's account.

Effect:

Sales has been credited, but Mohan has not been debited. Trial balance disagrees.

## Errors of Principle

These arise when accounting principles are violated, usually by confusing capital and revenue items.

Examples:

| Wrong Treatment | Correct Treatment |
|---|---|
| Furniture purchased debited to Purchases A/c | Debit Furniture A/c |
| Wages for installation of machinery debited to Wages A/c | Debit Machinery A/c |
| Repairs of old machine after purchase debited to Repairs A/c | Debit Machinery A/c if needed to make asset usable |
| Sale of old car credited to Sales A/c | Credit Car or Asset A/c |
| Owner's personal expense debited to Rent A/c | Debit Drawings A/c |

Errors of principle usually do not affect trial balance because equal debit and credit are still made.

## Compensating Errors

Compensating errors are two or more errors whose net effect cancels out.

Example:

Purchases book overcast by Rs. 10,000 causes excess debit in Purchases A/c.

Sales Return book undercast by Rs. 10,000 causes short debit in Sales Return A/c.

Net debit effect is cancelled, so trial balance agrees.

# Unit 6H: Searching for Errors

If trial balance does not agree, follow a systematic search.

## Step-by-Step Error Search

1. Re-total debit and credit columns of trial balance.
2. Check whether all ledger balances are copied into trial balance.
3. Check whether debit balances are placed in debit column and credit balances in credit column.
4. Compare current trial balance with previous period trial balance.
5. Recheck ledger account balances.
6. Recheck posting from subsidiary books.
7. Recheck totals of subsidiary books.
8. Check whether difference equals an omitted posting.
9. If difference is divisible by 2, check if half the difference was posted to wrong side.
10. If difference is divisible by 9, check transposition or decimal placement errors.

## Divisible by 2 Rule

If trial balance difference is Rs. 1,500:

Half = Rs. 750.

Possibility:

A credit item of Rs. 750 may have been posted to debit side. This creates double difference:

`Rs. 750 + Rs. 750 = Rs. 1,500`

## Divisible by 9 Rule

If Rs. 459 is posted as Rs. 954:

Difference:

`Rs. 954 - Rs. 459 = Rs. 495`

Rs. 495 is divisible by 9. This suggests a transposition error.

# Unit 6I: Rectification of Errors

## Meaning

Rectification means correcting errors in accounting records.

The method depends on whether:

1. the error affects trial balance,
2. suspense account has been opened,
3. error is found before or after final accounts,
4. error affects one account or two accounts.

## Rectification Principles

| Situation | Treatment |
|---|---|
| Account short debited | Debit that account |
| Account excess debited | Credit that account |
| Account short credited | Credit that account |
| Account excess credited | Debit that account |
| Wrong account debited | Credit wrong account and debit correct account |
| Wrong account credited | Debit wrong account and credit correct account |
| One-sided error | Use Suspense A/c |
| Two-sided error | Rectify through normal journal entry |

## Correcting Without Erasing

Accounting records should not be erased or overwritten. Corrections should be made through:

1. proper rectification entries,
2. additional postings,
3. explanatory notes where allowed.

This preserves reliability of records.

# Unit 6K: Errors Affecting Trial Balance

These are usually one-sided errors. They are corrected using suspense account if a journal entry is required.

## What Is Suspense Account?

Suspense account is a temporary account opened when the trial balance does not agree and the accountant wants to proceed before finding all errors.

If debit column is short, suspense account is placed on debit side.

If credit column is short, suspense account is placed on credit side.

## Purpose of Suspense Account

Suspense account:

1. temporarily balances the trial balance,
2. allows financial statements to proceed,
3. stores unexplained difference,
4. is cleared when errors are located,
5. has no permanent accounting nature.

## Example: Omission of Posting to Debtor

Sales book total includes sale to Diwakar Rs. 15,000. Sales Account is credited, but Diwakar Account was not debited.

Trial balance:

1. credit side includes Sales Rs. 15,000,
2. debit side lacks Diwakar Rs. 15,000.

Rectification:

| Particulars | Debit Rs. | Credit Rs. |
|---|---:|---:|
| Diwakar A/c Dr. | 15,000 |  |
| To Suspense A/c |  | 15,000 |

## Example: Purchases Book Overcast

Purchases book overcast by Rs. 1,000.

Wrong effect:

Purchases A/c debited Rs. 1,000 extra.

Rectification:

| Particulars | Debit Rs. | Credit Rs. |
|---|---:|---:|
| Suspense A/c Dr. | 1,000 |  |
| To Purchases A/c |  | 1,000 |

Why Suspense is debited:

Purchases had excess debit. To reduce purchases, credit Purchases A/c. The other side is Suspense A/c.

# Unit 6L: Suspense Account Decision Table

| Error Effect | Rectification Entry |
|---|---|
| Short debit in an account | Debit that account, credit Suspense |
| Excess debit in an account | Debit Suspense, credit that account |
| Short credit in an account | Debit Suspense, credit that account |
| Excess credit in an account | Debit that account, credit Suspense |

## Quick Examples

| Error | Entry |
|---|---|

| Mohan A/c debited Rs. 12,000 instead of Rs. 10,000 | Suspense A/c Dr. To Mohan A/c |

# Unit 6M: Guiding Rules for Rectification

## Rule 1: Error in Original Entry

If error is made in a book of original entry, assume all postings were made according to that wrong entry.

Example:

Credit purchase from Raghu recorded in sales book.

Assume it was posted as sales unless question states otherwise.

## Rule 2: Error at Posting Stage

If error is at posting stage, assume original record is correct.

Example:

Credit sales to Mohan recorded correctly but posted to Karan.

Sales Account is correct. Only debtor account needs correction.

## Rule 3: Wrong Account Without Side Mentioned

If posting to wrong account is mentioned without side and amount, assume it was posted on correct side with correct amount.

## Rule 4: Correct Account With Wrong Amount

If posting is to correct account but wrong amount, assume side is correct unless stated otherwise.

## Rule 5: Cash Book Entry

If a transaction is recorded in cash book, cash or bank side is normally assumed correct. Error usually relates to the other account unless stated otherwise.

## Rule 6: Subsidiary Book Total Error

Error in total of subsidiary book affects only the account where the total is posted.

Examples:

1. Sales book overcast affects Sales A/c only.
2. Purchases book undercast affects Purchases A/c only.
3. Sales return book overcast affects Sales Return A/c only.
4. Purchase return book undercast affects Purchases Return A/c only.

# Unit 6P: Suspense Account Worked Example

## Question

Trial balance shows excess credit Rs. 10,000. The difference is placed in Suspense Account. Later, these errors are found:

1. Sales return book overcast by Rs. 1,000.
2. Purchases book undercast by Rs. 600.
3. Sales book carry-forward errors caused net overcasting by Rs. 400.
4. Goods returned to Ram Rs. 1,000 recorded through sales book.
5. Credit purchases from M and Co. Rs. 8,000 recorded through sales book.
6. Credit purchases from S and Co. Rs. 5,000 recorded through sales book, but S and Co. correctly credited.
7. Salary paid Rs. 2,000 debited to employee's personal account.

## Rectification Entries

| No. | Entry | Debit Rs. | Credit Rs. |
|---|---|---:|---:|
| 1 | Suspense A/c Dr. To Sales Return A/c | 1,000 | 1,000 |
| 2 | Purchases A/c Dr. To Suspense A/c | 600 | 600 |
| 3 | Sales A/c Dr. To Suspense A/c | 400 | 400 |

# Unit 6Q: Rectification in Next Accounting Year

## Rule

If an error of a previous year is found after final accounts have been prepared, do not disturb the current year's nominal accounts directly for last year's expenses or incomes.

Use Profit and Loss Adjustment Account for prior-period income or expense effects.

## Why

If last year's salary error is corrected through current year's Salary A/c, current year's profit becomes incorrect. Profit and Loss Adjustment Account keeps the effect separate.

## Basic Treatment

| Previous-Year Error Type | Use |
|---|---|
| Expense understated last year | Debit Profit and Loss Adjustment A/c |
| Expense overstated last year | Credit Profit and Loss Adjustment A/c |
| Income understated last year | Credit Profit and Loss Adjustment A/c |
| Income overstated last year | Debit Profit and Loss Adjustment A/c |

# Unit 6R: Short Answer Solutions

## 1. State the meaning of trial balance.

Trial balance is a statement showing ledger balances, or debit and credit totals, of all accounts on a particular date to check arithmetical accuracy of ledger posting.

## 2. Give two examples of errors of principle.

Examples:

1. Furniture purchased debited to Purchases A/c.
2. Wages paid for installation of machinery debited to Wages A/c instead of Machinery A/c.

## 3. Give two examples of errors of commission.

Examples:

1. Sales book overcast.
2. Amount posted to correct account but with wrong amount.

Other examples include wrong balancing, wrong posting, or posting to wrong side.

## 4. What are the methods of preparing trial balance?

The three methods are:

1. totals method,
2. balances method,
3. totals-cum-balances method.

## 5. Steps to locate errors in trial balance.

1. Re-total trial balance columns.
2. Compare trial balance balances with ledger balances.
3. Check debit and credit columns.
4. Recheck ledger balancing.
5. Recheck posting from books of original entry.
6. Check totals of subsidiary books.
7. Use half-difference test.
8. Use divisibility by 9 test.
9. Compare with previous year's trial balance.

## 6. What is suspense account?

Suspense account is a temporary account used to record trial balance difference until errors are found and rectified.

It is not necessary that suspense account will always close immediately after some errors are rectified. If some one-sided errors remain undetected, the balance remains and is carried forward until those errors are found.

## 7. Errors causing difference in trial balance and errors not revealed by it.

Errors causing difference:

1. one-sided posting omission,
2. wrong side posting,
3. wrong amount posted on one side,
4. wrong total of subsidiary book,
5. balance placed in wrong trial balance column,
6. omission of account balance from trial balance.

Errors not revealed:

1. complete omission,
2. errors of principle,
3. wrong account but correct side and amount,
4. compensating errors,
5. equal debit and credit recorded with wrong amount.

## 8. Limitations of trial balance.

Trial balance only checks arithmetical equality of debits and credits. It cannot prove that all transactions are recorded correctly, classified correctly, or posted to the correct accounts. It does not reveal complete omission, compensating errors, errors of principle, or wrong account errors that preserve debit-credit equality.

# Unit 6S: Long Answer Solutions

## 1. Purpose of trial balance

Trial balance is prepared to check arithmetical accuracy of ledger accounts. It proves that total debit balances equal total credit balances. It also helps locate errors when the totals disagree.

Trial balance also helps in preparing final accounts because all balances are collected in one place. Revenue and expense accounts are transferred to trading and profit and loss account, while asset, liability, and capital accounts are used in balance sheet.

However, agreement of trial balance is not absolute proof of accuracy because several errors may still remain hidden.

## 2. Errors of principle with rectification

Errors of principle happen when accounting principles are violated, often by treating capital expenditure as revenue expenditure or revenue receipt as capital receipt.

Example 1:

Furniture purchased Rs. 10,000 debited to Purchases A/c.

Rectification:

| Particulars | Debit Rs. | Credit Rs. |
|---|---:|---:|
| Furniture A/c Dr. | 10,000 |  |
| To Purchases A/c |  | 10,000 |

Example 2:

Repairs before using second-hand machinery Rs. 2,000 debited to Repairs A/c.

Rectification:

| Particulars | Debit Rs. | Credit Rs. |
|---|---:|---:|
| Machinery A/c Dr. | 2,000 |  |
| To Repairs A/c |  | 2,000 |

## 3. Errors of commission with rectification

Errors of commission are clerical errors such as wrong amount, wrong account, wrong side, wrong casting, or wrong balancing.

Example 1:

Credit sales to Mohan Rs. 7,000 recorded as Rs. 700.

Short recording:

`Rs. 7,000 - Rs. 700 = Rs. 6,300`

Rectification:

| Particulars | Debit Rs. | Credit Rs. |
|---|---:|---:|
| Mohan A/c Dr. | 6,300 |  |
| To Sales A/c |  | 6,300 |

Example 2:

Sales book overcast by Rs. 700.

Rectification:

| Particulars | Debit Rs. | Credit Rs. |
|---|---:|---:|
| Suspense A/c Dr. | 700 |  |
| To Sales A/c |  | 700 |

## 4. Different types of errors in recording business transactions

Errors can be classified as:

1. errors of commission,
2. errors of omission,
3. errors of principle,
4. compensating errors.

They may also be classified as:

1. errors affecting trial balance,
2. errors not affecting trial balance.

Errors affecting trial balance are usually one-sided. Errors not affecting trial balance are usually two-sided or self-cancelling.

## 5. Office equipment and creditors misposting case

Correct transaction:

Credit purchase of pen drive Rs. 3,500.

Correct entry should have been:

| Particulars | Debit Rs. | Credit Rs. |
|---|---:|---:|
| Office Equipment A/c Dr. | 3,500 |  |
| To Creditors A/c |  | 3,500 |

Actual posting:

Office Equipment debited Rs. 3,500 and Creditors also debited Rs. 3,500.

Answers:

| Part | Answer |
|---|---|
| Office Equipment balance | Correctly stated if the debit to Office Equipment was correct |
| Creditors balance | Understated by Rs. 7,000 compared with correct credit balance |
| Debit column total | Overstated by Rs. 3,500 because creditors were wrongly debited |
| Credit column total | Understated by Rs. 3,500 because creditors were not credited |
| If debit total before correction is Rs. 2,40,000 | Credit total is Rs. 2,33,000 |

Reason for last answer:

Wrong posting creates a trial balance difference of Rs. 7,000. Debit total is higher by Rs. 7,000.

`Credit total = Rs. 2,40,000 - Rs. 7,000 = Rs. 2,33,000`

# Unit 6V: Common Mistakes to Avoid

| Mistake | Correct Approach |
|---|---|
| Believing a tallied trial balance proves all records correct | It only proves arithmetical equality |
| Putting drawings in credit column | Drawings has debit balance |
| Putting purchases return in debit column | Purchases return has credit balance |
| Treating suspense as permanent account | It is temporary |
| Using suspense for all errors | Use suspense only for one-sided errors or trial balance difference |
| Ignoring capital versus revenue distinction | Check error of principle carefully |
| Rectifying wrong account errors with suspense | If both accounts are known and trial balance is not affected, use direct journal entry |
| Forgetting double effect of wrong side posting | Correct amount plus reversal amount may be needed |
| Treating overcast and undercast the same | Overcast means too much; undercast means too little |
| Erasing records | Pass proper rectification entries |

# Unit 6W: Final Revision Sheet

## Trial Balance

| Point | Recall |
|---|---|
| Meaning | Statement of ledger balances or totals |
| Main purpose | Check debit-credit equality |
| Best method | Balances method |
| Prepared after | Ledger posting and balancing |
| Helps prepare | Trading account, profit and loss account, balance sheet |
| Limitation | Does not reveal all errors |

## Error Types

| Type | Example | Trial Balance Effect |
|---|---|---|
| Commission | Wrong posting amount | May affect |
| Omission | Transaction not recorded | Complete omission does not affect |
| Principle | Capital item treated as revenue | Usually does not affect |
| Compensating | Two errors cancel out | Does not affect |

## Rectification Logic

| Problem | Action |
|---|---|
| Wrong debit | Credit wrong account, debit correct account |
| Wrong credit | Debit wrong account, credit correct account |
| Short debit | Debit account |
| Excess debit | Credit account |
| Short credit | Credit account |
| Excess credit | Debit account |
| One account missing | Use Suspense A/c |
| Two accounts known | Pass direct rectification entry |

## Final Study Summary

Trial balance is a useful checkpoint, not a guarantee of perfect accounts. It confirms that debit totals and credit totals agree, but it cannot detect complete omissions, wrong account errors, errors of principle, or compensating errors.

Error rectification is a logic exercise. First find the wrong effect. Then find the correct effect. The rectification entry is the difference between the two. If only one account is affected, use Suspense Account. If two or more accounts are clearly affected and trial balance is not disturbed, correct them directly through journal entry.
