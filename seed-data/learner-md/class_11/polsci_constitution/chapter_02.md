## Learner Explanation

Ordinary rights may be changed by the legislature by ordinary process of law making, but a fundamental right may only be changed by amending the Constitution itself. While ordinary legal rights are protected and enforced by ordinary law, Fundamental Rights are protected and guaranteed by the constitution of the country.

As we shall study below in this chapter, judiciary has the powers and responsibility to protect the fundamental rights from violations by actions of the Indian Constitution at Work government. However, fundamental rights are not absolute or unlimited rights.
