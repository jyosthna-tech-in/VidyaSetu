# Chapter 7: Birth
### NCERT Class 11 English Snapshots

---

## Chapter Purpose

"Birth" by A. J. Cronin presents a young doctor's first major test of skill, courage, and responsibility. Dr Andrew Manson must save both a mother and a newborn child in a tense medical emergency. The story shows how professional duty becomes meaningful when it saves human life.

The central idea is:

> True professional achievement comes from dedication, persistence, and the courage to act under pressure.

---

## Learning Outcomes

After studying this chapter, you should be able to:

1. Summarise the plot of the story.
2. Describe Andrew Manson's character.
3. Explain the medical crisis in the story.
4. Understand why Andrew says he has done something real.
5. Discuss the themes of responsibility, perseverance, and hope.

## 2. Plot Summary

Andrew Manson is called to attend Mrs Morgan, who is about to give birth. He is emotionally disturbed by his own personal thoughts, but the medical case demands his full attention.

At the house, Mrs Morgan's mother and the midwife wait anxiously. Andrew remains with the patient through the night. After the delivery, he faces a severe crisis: the mother is weak and needs care, and the newborn child appears lifeless.

Andrew first stabilises the mother. Then he turns to the child. Though the midwife believes the baby is stillborn, Andrew refuses to give up. He tries different methods to revive the baby: rubbing, pressing the chest, and using hot and cold water treatment. After long effort, the baby finally cries.

Andrew leaves exhausted but deeply fulfilled. He feels that he has done something real at last.

| Trait | Explanation |
|---|---|
| Responsible | He stays through the night and does not abandon the case |
| Skilled | He applies medical knowledge under pressure |
| Persistent | He keeps trying even when the child seems dead |
| Compassionate | He understands the family's emotional stake |
| Courageous | He acts decisively during crisis |
| Self-critical | He wants his work to have real meaning |

Andrew's greatness in the story lies not in confidence alone, but in refusing to stop when failure seems certain.

---

## 4. The Medical Crisis

Andrew faces two urgent tasks:

1. Save the mother.
2. Revive the newborn child.

The difficulty is that both need attention, and time is critical. Andrew must decide quickly and act in the correct order.

This crisis tests:

- Medical knowledge
- Emotional control
- Physical endurance
- Moral responsibility
- Professional courage

---

## 5. Why Andrew Says He Has Done Something Real

At the end, Andrew says he has done something real because he has saved a life through his own effort.

This statement means:

- He has performed meaningful work.
- He has proved his worth as a doctor.
- He has turned knowledge into action.
- He has brought hope to a family.
- He has experienced professional fulfilment.

The word "real" contrasts with routine work or empty ambition. This case gives him a deep sense of purpose.

## 8. Tension in the Story

The story builds tension through:

- Night-time setting
- Waiting family members
- Difficult childbirth
- Mother's danger
- Lifeless newborn
- Midwife's despair
- Andrew's repeated attempts
- Final cry of the child

The tension makes the ending emotionally powerful.

2. Explain that Andrew saves the child.
3. Mention his long struggle and refusal to give up.
4. Connect it to professional fulfilment.
5. Conclude that saving life gives meaning to his work.

5. Conclude that persistence creates hope.
