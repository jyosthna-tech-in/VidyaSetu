# Chapter 8: Thinking
### NCERT Class 11 Psychology

---

## Chapter Purpose

This chapter explains thinking as a higher mental process. Thinking involves manipulating information through images, words, concepts, reasoning, problem solving, decision-making, and creativity. It helps us understand the world, solve problems, and plan action.

The central idea is:

> Thinking is the mental process through which we organise, transform, and use information to solve problems and create meaning.

## 1. Meaning of Thinking

Thinking is a mental process involving manipulation and analysis of information.

It includes:

- Remembering
- Imagining
- Reasoning
- Problem solving
- Judging
- Deciding
- Creating

Thinking may use words, images, symbols, or mental actions.

---

## 2. Building Blocks of Thought

### Mental Images

Mental images are internal representations of objects or events.

Example:

Imagining your classroom without seeing it.

### Concepts

A concept is a mental category based on common features.

Examples:

- Tree
- Vehicle
- Emotion
- Democracy
- Triangle

Concepts help us organise knowledge and think efficiently.

---

## 3. Reasoning

Reasoning is the process of drawing conclusions from information.

### Deductive Reasoning

Deductive reasoning moves from general rule to specific conclusion.

Example:

All mammals breathe. A whale is a mammal. Therefore, a whale breathes.

### Inductive Reasoning

Inductive reasoning moves from specific observations to a general conclusion.

Example:

You observe that several metals expand when heated, and conclude that metals generally expand when heated.

---

## 4. Problem Solving

Problem solving is thinking directed toward reaching a goal when the path is not immediately clear.

Steps:

1. Identify the problem.
2. Understand the goal.
3. Generate possible solutions.
4. Evaluate alternatives.
5. Choose a strategy.
6. Apply the solution.
7. Review the outcome.

---

## 5. Barriers to Problem Solving

| Barrier | Meaning | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Mental set | Using an old strategy even when it does not fit | Solving every problem with the same formula |
| Functional fixedness | Seeing an object only in its usual function | Not using a book as a paperweight |
| Lack of motivation | Not trying deeply enough | Giving up early |
| Emotional block | Anxiety interferes with thinking | Exam panic |
| Limited knowledge | Missing required information | Not knowing the rule needed |

---

## 6. Judgement and Decision-Making

Judgement is evaluating information and forming an opinion.

Decision-making is choosing among alternatives.

Example:

Judgement: This course seems useful.

Decision: I will choose this course.

Good decision-making requires:

- Relevant information
- Clear goals
- Awareness of consequences
- Avoiding bias
- Reviewing alternatives

---

## 7. Creative Thinking

Creative thinking produces ideas or solutions that are novel and useful.

It is not limited to artists or scientists. Everyday creativity appears in cooking, study methods, conversation, design, and problem solving.

Characteristics:

- Originality
- Flexibility
- Fluency
- Elaboration
- Openness
- Willingness to take intellectual risks

---

## 8. Convergent and Divergent Thinking

| Type | Meaning | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Convergent thinking | Finding one correct answer | Solving a math equation |
| Divergent thinking | Generating many possible answers | Listing uses of a brick |

Divergent thinking is important in creativity because it expands possibilities.

---

## 9. Stages of Creative Thinking

| Stage | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Preparation | Understanding the problem and gathering information |
| Incubation | Letting ideas develop unconsciously |
| Illumination | Sudden insight or solution |
| Verification | Testing and refining the idea |

Creativity needs both freedom and evaluation.

---

## 10. Vertical and Lateral Thinking

| Type | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Vertical thinking | Step-by-step logical movement |
| Lateral thinking | Looking for alternative approaches and fresh perspectives |

Both are useful. Vertical thinking checks logic; lateral thinking opens new possibilities.

---

## 11. Language and Thought

Language is a system of symbols governed by rules and used for communication.

Language helps thought by:

- Naming objects
- Organising ideas
- Communicating meaning
- Supporting reasoning
- Preserving culture

But thinking can also occur without language, such as through images, imitation, and practical problem solving.

### Linguistic Relativity

The linguistic relativity hypothesis suggests that language influences thought and perception. It does not mean language completely controls thinking, but it can shape how people categorise and express experience.

---

## 12. Common Mistakes

| Mistake | Correct understanding |
|---|---|
| Saying thinking always needs language | Thinking can use images and actions too. |
| Confusing reasoning and decision-making | Reasoning draws conclusions; decision-making chooses actions. |
| Thinking creativity means only art | Creativity appears in everyday problem solving. |
| Ignoring divergent thinking | Creativity needs many possible ideas before selection. |
| Treating mental set as always useful | Old strategies can block new solutions. |
