# Unit 3A: Meaning of Market

In marketing, market does not mean only a physical place. It means a set of actual and potential buyers of a product or service.

Examples:

1. Market for electric scooters.
2. Market for online education.
3. Market for packaged food.
4. Market for insurance.

Buyers may be spread across regions and connected digitally.

# Unit 3B: Meaning of Marketing

Marketing is a social and managerial process by which individuals and organisations obtain what they need and want through creating, offering, and exchanging products of value.

In simple business terms, marketing means identifying customer needs and satisfying them profitably.

## Core Elements

1. Need, want, and demand.
2. Product or service.
3. Utility and value.
4. Exchange.
5. Customer satisfaction.
6. Market offering.

# Unit 3C: Features of Marketing

## 1. Needs and Wants

Marketing starts with customer needs and wants.

## 2. Creating Market Offering

Business creates product or service that satisfies customer.

## 3. Customer Value

Customer compares benefits received with cost paid.

## 4. Exchange Mechanism

Marketing involves exchange between buyer and seller.

## 5. Customer Satisfaction

Long-term marketing success depends on satisfying customers.

# Unit 3D: Marketing vs Selling

| Basis | Marketing | Selling |
|---|---|---|
| Starting point | Customer needs. | Product already made. |
| Focus | Customer satisfaction. | Sales volume. |
| Scope | Broad. | Narrow. |
| Orientation | Outside-in. | Inside-out. |
| Means | Integrated marketing efforts. | Persuasion and promotion. |
| End | Profit through customer satisfaction. | Profit through sales. |
| Time horizon | Long-term. | Often short-term. |

## Key Line

Selling is part of marketing, but marketing is much wider than selling.

# Unit 3E: Marketing Management Philosophies

## Production Concept

Customers prefer products that are widely available and affordable.

Focus: production efficiency and distribution.

Risk: ignoring product quality or customer preference.

## Product Concept

Customers prefer products with superior quality, performance, and features.

Focus: product improvement.

Risk: ignoring actual customer need or affordability.

## Selling Concept

Customers will not buy enough unless company undertakes aggressive selling and promotion.

Focus: persuasion and sales volume.

Risk: customer dissatisfaction if product does not solve real need.

## Marketing Concept

The key to success is identifying customer needs and satisfying them better than competitors.

Focus: customer satisfaction.

## Societal Marketing Concept

Business should satisfy customers while also considering society's long-term welfare.

Focus: customer satisfaction plus social responsibility.

## Philosophy Comparison

| Philosophy | Main Focus | Typical Risk |
|---|---|---|
| Production | Availability and low cost. | Ignores customer preference. |
| Product | Quality and features. | Overlooks actual need. |
| Selling | Aggressive promotion. | Short-term customer pressure. |
| Marketing | Customer satisfaction. | Needs deep customer understanding. |
| Societal marketing | Customer and society welfare. | More complex balancing. |

# Unit 3F: Functions of Marketing

Marketing involves many functions.

## 1. Gathering and Analysing Market Information

Business studies customers, competitors, and market trends.

## 2. Marketing Planning

Marketing goals, strategies, budgets, and programmes are prepared.

## 3. Product Designing and Development

Product features, quality, style, and packaging are planned.

## 4. Standardisation and Grading

Standards ensure uniform quality. Grading classifies products into quality groups.

## 5. Packaging and Labelling

Packaging protects and promotes the product. Labelling informs customers.

## 6. Branding

Branding gives product identity and helps differentiation.

## 7. Customer Support Services

Services such as delivery, installation, complaints handling, warranty, and maintenance build satisfaction.

## 8. Pricing

Price is set after considering cost, demand, competition, objectives, and legal factors.

## 9. Promotion

Promotion informs and persuades customers.

## 10. Physical Distribution

Product is moved and stored so it reaches customers at right time and place.

## 11. Transportation

Transportation moves goods from production point to consumption point.

## 12. Storage and Warehousing

Storage balances production and demand timing.

# Unit 3G: Marketing Mix

Marketing mix is the set of marketing tools used by a firm to achieve marketing objectives.

The four main elements are:

1. Product.
2. Price.
3. Place.
4. Promotion.

## Marketing Mix Table

| Element | Main Decisions |
|---|---|
| Product | Quality, design, features, brand, packaging, labelling, variety. |
| Price | List price, discounts, credit terms, pricing strategy. |
| Place | Channel, transport, warehousing, inventory, coverage. |
| Promotion | Advertising, personal selling, sales promotion, public relations. |

# Unit 3H: Product

Product means anything offered to a market to satisfy a need or want.

It may be:

1. Physical good.
2. Service.
3. Experience.
4. Idea.
5. Person.
6. Place.

## Product Decision Areas

1. Quality.
2. Features.
3. Design.
4. Brand.
5. Packaging.
6. Labelling.
7. After-sale service.

# Unit 3I: Branding

Branding gives identity to product and differentiates it from competitors.

## Qualities of a Good Brand Name

1. Short and simple.
2. Easy to pronounce.
3. Easy to remember.
4. Distinctive.
5. Suggests product benefit if possible.
6. Adaptable to packaging and advertising.
7. Legally protectable.

## Advantages to Marketers

1. Product differentiation.
2. Customer loyalty.
3. Easier advertising.
4. Supports new product launch.
5. Allows premium pricing if brand is strong.

## Advantages to Customers

1. Helps identification.
2. Assures quality consistency.
3. Reduces purchase effort.
4. Gives status or confidence in some cases.

# Unit 3J: Packaging

Packaging means designing and producing container or wrapper for product.

## Levels

| Level | Example |
|---|---|
| Primary package | Tube containing toothpaste. |
| Secondary package | Cardboard box around toothpaste tube. |
| Transportation package | Carton used for shipping many boxes. |

## Functions of Packaging

1. Product protection.
2. Convenience in handling.
3. Product identification.
4. Promotion.
5. Prevention of adulteration.
6. Information display.
7. Support for self-service retailing.

# Unit 3K: Labelling

Labelling means providing information on product or package.

## Functions

1. Describes product and contents.
2. Identifies product or brand.
3. Grades product.
4. Helps promotion.
5. Provides legal information.
6. Gives instructions and warnings.

## Example Details on Label

1. Product name.
2. Ingredients.
3. Quantity.
4. Manufacturing details.
5. Expiry date.
6. Directions for use.
7. Safety warnings.
8. Customer care information.

# Unit 3L: Price

Price is the amount of money paid by buyer to obtain product or service.

## Factors Affecting Price Determination

| Factor | Effect |
|---|---|
| Product cost | Price must normally cover cost in long run. |
| Utility and demand | Higher perceived value may allow higher price. |
| Competition | Competitor prices influence price. |
| Government and legal regulation | Some prices may be regulated. |
| Marketing methods used | Heavy promotion or premium packaging may raise cost. |
| Pricing objective | Survival, profit, market share, or quality leadership affects price. |

# Unit 3M: Place and Distribution

Place decisions ensure product reaches target customers.

## Channels of Distribution

Channel of distribution is the route through which goods move from producer to consumer.

## Common Channels

1. Producer -> consumer.
2. Producer -> retailer -> consumer.
3. Producer -> wholesaler -> retailer -> consumer.
4. Producer -> agent -> wholesaler -> retailer -> consumer.

## Factors Affecting Channel Choice

| Factor | Effect |
|---|---|
| Product type | Perishable products need shorter channels. |
| Product value | Expensive products may use direct channels. |
| Market size | Large markets may need intermediaries. |
| Customer location | Widely scattered customers need wider distribution. |
| Company resources | Strong firms may use own distribution. |
| Competitive practice | Industry pattern affects choice. |

## Components

2. Transportation.
3. Warehousing.
4. Inventory control.

## Importance

1. Product available at right place.
2. Product available at right time.
3. Lower delivery cost.
4. Better customer service.
5. Reduced stockouts.

# Unit 3O: Promotion

Promotion communicates product information and persuades customers.

## Promotion Mix

1. Advertising.
2. Personal selling.
3. Sales promotion.
4. Public relations.

# Unit 3P: Advertising

Advertising is paid, non-personal communication by an identified sponsor.

## Merits

1. Wide reach.
2. Repeated message.
3. Builds brand image.
4. Useful for mass products.

## Limitations

1. Impersonal.
2. One-way communication.
3. Can be costly.
4. Effect may be difficult to measure precisely.

# Unit 3Q: Personal Selling

Personal selling is personal communication between salesperson and prospective buyer.

## Features

1. Face-to-face or direct interaction.
2. Two-way communication.
3. Immediate feedback.
4. Relationship building.
5. Flexible presentation.

## Useful When

1. Product is technical.
2. Product is expensive.
3. Customer needs explanation.
4. Industrial selling is involved.
5. Relationship matters.

# Unit 3R: Sales Promotion

Sales promotion includes short-term incentives to encourage purchase.

## Examples

1. Discount.
2. Refund.
3. Product combination.
4. Quantity gift.
5. Instant draw.
6. Sampling.
7. Usable benefit coupon.

## Purpose

1. Attract attention.
2. Encourage trial.
3. Clear stock.
4. Support dealer push.
5. Increase short-term sales.

# Unit 3S: Public Relations

Public relations builds and maintains favourable image of business among public groups.

## Tools

1. Press releases.
2. Events.
3. Community programmes.
4. Speeches.
5. Annual reports.
6. Crisis communication.

## Importance

1. Builds trust.
2. Handles negative publicity.
3. Supports brand image.
4. Improves relations with stakeholders.

# Unit 3T: Promotion Tool Comparison

| Tool | Nature | Best Use |
|---|---|---|
| Advertising | Paid, non-personal, mass communication. | Brand awareness and mass reach. |
| Personal selling | Direct personal interaction. | Technical, costly, or customised products. |
| Sales promotion | Short-term incentive. | Quick sales boost or trial. |
| Public relations | Image and relationship building. | Trust, goodwill, and reputation. |

# Unit 3V: Case Answer Framework

1. Identify marketing concept or mix element.

5. Add one advantage, limitation, or managerial caution.

## High-Quality Example

Case: A company selling premium water purifiers sends trained salespeople to homes, demonstrates water quality, explains filter life, and answers questions before purchase.

Answer:

1. The promotion tool is personal selling.
2. Personal selling involves direct interaction between salesperson and buyer.
3. Water purifiers are technical and costly, so customers need explanation.
4. The salesperson can customise the message and handle doubts.
5. This builds confidence and improves chance of purchase.

# Unit 3W: Common Mistakes and Corrections

| Mistake | Correct Understanding |
|---|---|
| Marketing means only selling | Marketing includes need identification, product, price, place, promotion, and satisfaction. |
| Market means only physical place | Market means actual and potential buyers. |
| Branding and packaging are same | Branding gives identity; packaging contains and protects product. |
| Labelling is only decoration | Labelling provides information, legal details, and promotion. |
| Advertising and personal selling are same | Advertising is non-personal; personal selling is direct interaction. |
| Sales promotion builds long-term loyalty by itself | It usually gives short-term incentive; long-term loyalty needs value and satisfaction. |
| Price depends only on cost | Demand, competition, objectives, and law also matter. |

Marketing is customer-centred business thinking. The learner should always ask: Who is the customer, what need is being satisfied, what is the product, what price is suitable, how will it reach the buyer, and how will the buyer be informed and persuaded?
