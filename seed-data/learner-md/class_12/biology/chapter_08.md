# Unit 8A: Microbes Around Us

Microbes are present in soil, water, air, plants, animals, and extreme environments. Many are harmful, but many are useful.

Useful microbes help in:

1. Food preparation.
2. Medicine.
3. Industrial production.
4. Wastewater treatment.
5. Renewable energy.
6. Soil fertility.
7. Pest control.

# Unit 8B: Microbes in Household Products

## Curd

Curd is produced by lactic acid bacteria. These bacteria convert lactose in milk into lactic acid.

Effects of lactic acid:

1. Coagulates milk proteins.
2. Gives curd its texture.
3. Improves digestibility.
4. Helps suppress harmful microbes in gut.

## Bread

Baker's yeast, Saccharomyces cerevisiae, is used in bread making. Yeast ferments sugars and releases carbon dioxide. The gas makes dough rise.

## Fermented Foods

Many traditional foods are made using microbes. Fermentation improves taste, texture, shelf life, and sometimes nutritional value.

## Cheese

Different microbes give different cheeses their flavour and texture.

Examples:

1. Swiss cheese has large holes due to carbon dioxide produced by bacteria.
2. Roquefort cheese is ripened using specific fungi.

# Unit 8C: Microbes in Industrial Products

Industrial production uses microbes in large vessels called fermenters.

## Alcoholic Beverages

Yeast ferments sugars to ethanol and carbon dioxide.

| Product | Raw Material or Process |
|---|---|
| Wine | Fermentation of fruit juice. |
| Beer | Fermentation of malted grains. |
| Whisky, rum, brandy | Fermented product followed by distillation. |

## Antibiotics

Antibiotics are chemical substances produced by some microbes that inhibit or kill disease-causing microbes.

Penicillin was discovered from Penicillium fungus and became a major medical breakthrough.

## Organic Acids

| Product | Microbe |
|---|---|
| Citric acid | Aspergillus niger |
| Acetic acid | Acetobacter aceti |
| Butyric acid | Clostridium butylicum |
| Lactic acid | Lactobacillus |

## Enzymes

| Enzyme | Use |
|---|---|
| Lipase | Detergents and fat removal. |
| Pectinase | Clearing fruit juices. |
| Protease | Detergents and leather processing. |
| Streptokinase | Dissolves blood clots in medical treatment. |

## Bioactive Molecules

Some microbes produce molecules used as medicines, such as immunosuppressants and cholesterol-lowering agents.

# Unit 8D: Sewage Treatment

Sewage contains organic matter and microbes. It must be treated before release into water bodies.

## Primary Treatment

Primary treatment is physical removal of large and small particles.

Steps:

1. Filtration removes large debris.
2. Sedimentation settles grit and solids.
3. Primary sludge forms at bottom.
4. Effluent moves to secondary treatment.

## Secondary Treatment

Secondary treatment is biological treatment using microbes.

## Activated Sludge Process

1. Effluent is aerated in large tanks.
2. Aerobic microbes grow into flocs.
3. Microbes consume organic matter.
4. Biochemical oxygen demand decreases.
5. Flocs settle as activated sludge.
6. Part of sludge is reused as inoculum.
7. Remaining sludge goes to anaerobic digesters.

## Why BOD Matters

High BOD means water has much organic matter and microbes will use more oxygen to decompose it. This reduces oxygen available for aquatic life.

Low BOD after treatment indicates cleaner water.

# Unit 8E: Biogas Production

Biogas is produced by anaerobic digestion of organic waste.

## Methanogens

Methanogens are microbes that produce methane. They are found in anaerobic sludge and rumen of cattle.

## Biogas Components

1. Methane.
2. Carbon dioxide.
3. Hydrogen.
4. Small amounts of other gases.

## Biogas Plant Steps

1. Cow dung and water are mixed as slurry.
2. Slurry enters digester.
3. Anaerobic microbes break down organic matter.
4. Methane-rich gas collects.
5. Gas is used as fuel.
6. Spent slurry is used as manure.

## Benefits

1. Renewable fuel.
2. Waste management.
3. Reduced dependence on firewood.
4. Nutrient-rich manure.
5. Useful for rural energy needs.

# Unit 8F: Microbes as Biocontrol Agents

Biocontrol uses living organisms to control pests and diseases.

## Why Biocontrol Is Useful

Chemical pesticides may:

1. Harm non-target organisms.
2. Pollute soil and water.
3. Create resistant pests.
4. Accumulate in food chains.

Biocontrol is often more specific and environmentally safer.

## Examples

| Agent | Use |
|---|---|
| Ladybird beetle | Controls aphids. |
| Dragonfly | Controls mosquitoes. |
| Bacillus thuringiensis | Controls insect larvae. |
| Trichoderma | Controls plant pathogens in soil. |
| Baculoviruses | Control specific insect pests. |

## Bacillus thuringiensis

Bacillus thuringiensis produces proteins toxic to certain insect larvae. These proteins damage the insect gut after ingestion.

# Unit 8G: Microbes as Biofertilisers

Biofertilisers increase nutrient availability in soil.

## Nitrogen Fixers

| Organism | Role |
|---|---|
| Rhizobium | Symbiotic nitrogen fixation in legume root nodules. |
| Azotobacter | Free-living nitrogen fixation. |
| Azospirillum | Associative nitrogen fixation near roots. |
| Cyanobacteria | Nitrogen fixation in paddy fields and aquatic systems. |

## Mycorrhiza

Mycorrhiza is association between fungi and plant roots. The fungus improves absorption of phosphorus and water. The plant provides food to fungus.

## Benefits of Biofertilisers

1. Improve soil fertility.
2. Reduce chemical fertiliser use.
3. Support sustainable agriculture.
4. Improve plant growth.
5. Help maintain soil health.

# Unit 8H: Common Mistakes and Corrections

| Mistake | Correct Understanding |
|---|---|
| All microbes are harmful | Many microbes are useful and essential. |
| Fermentation always needs oxygen | Many fermentation processes are anaerobic. |
| Primary sewage treatment uses microbes | Primary treatment is mainly physical; secondary treatment is biological. |
| High BOD means clean water | High BOD means high organic pollution. |
| Biogas is pure methane | It is a mixture, mainly methane. |
| Biofertilisers kill pests | Biofertilisers improve nutrients; biocontrol agents manage pests. |

## Final Revision

Microbes support human welfare by transforming materials. Learn each example as a three-part link: microbe, process, and product. The same biological principle, microbial metabolism, explains curd, antibiotics, sewage treatment, biogas, pest control, and soil fertility.
