# Chapter 6: Tertiary and Quaternary Activities
### NCERT Class 12 - Geography Human

## Chapter at a Glance

Tertiary activities provide services rather than producing goods directly. Quaternary activities involve knowledge, information, research, and advanced services. Together, these sectors dominate many modern economies and are closely linked with urbanisation, technology, globalisation, and human development.

The chapter explains trade, transport, communication, services, tourism, medical services, quaternary activities, and outsourcing.

## Learning Outcomes

By the end of this chapter, you should be able to:

- define tertiary activities;
- distinguish tertiary, quaternary, and quinary activities;
- explain types of services;
- understand trade, transport, and communication as services;
- explain tourism as a service activity;
- describe quaternary activities and knowledge economy;
- explain outsourcing and its effects;
- write sector-comparison answers with examples.

# Unit 6A: Meaning of Tertiary Activities

Tertiary activities provide services. They help move, sell, finance, communicate, repair, educate, heal, and organise economic life.

Examples:

- shopkeeping;
- transport;
- banking;
- insurance;
- teaching;
- medical care;
- tourism;
- communication.

Tertiary activities connect primary and secondary sectors with consumers.

# Unit 6B: Types of Services

| Service type | Examples |
|---|---|
| Personal services | Domestic help, hairdressing |
| Business services | Banking, insurance, advertising |
| Public services | Administration, police, defence |
| Professional services | Teaching, medicine, law |
| Transport services | Road, rail, air, water transport |
| Communication services | Postal, telephone, internet |

# Unit 6C: Trade as a Service

Trade means buying and selling goods and services.

## Retail Trade

Retail trade sells goods directly to consumers.

Examples:

- shops;
- supermarkets;
- street vendors;
- online retail.

## Wholesale Trade

Wholesale trade buys goods in bulk and sells to retailers or institutions.

Wholesale trade links producers and retail markets.

# Unit 6D: Transport as a Service

Transport moves people and goods from one place to another. It creates place utility because goods become more useful when available where needed.

Transport supports:

- trade;
- industry;
- agriculture;
- tourism;
- defence;
- urban growth.

# Unit 6E: Communication Services

Communication transmits information, ideas, messages, and data.

Modern communication includes:

- telephone;
- radio;
- television;
- internet;
- satellites;
- mobile networks;
- digital platforms.

Communication reduces the friction of distance and supports global economic integration.

# Unit 6F: Tourism

Tourism is travel for recreation, culture, religion, health, business, or adventure.

## Factors Promoting Tourism

- climate;
- landscape;
- heritage;
- culture;
- transport;
- hotels;
- safety;
- political stability;
- income levels;
- marketing.

## Types of Tourism

| Type | Example |
|---|---|
| Cultural tourism | Heritage sites, festivals |
| Eco-tourism | Nature-based responsible travel |
| Medical tourism | Travel for health treatment |
| Adventure tourism | Trekking, rafting |
| Religious tourism | Pilgrimage |
| Business tourism | Conferences and meetings |

# Unit 6G: Tourism Impacts

Positive impacts:

- employment;
- foreign exchange;
- infrastructure growth;
- cultural exchange;
- local market expansion.

Negative impacts:

- pollution;
- crowding;
- cultural commercialisation;
- pressure on water and land;
- seasonal employment.

Sustainable tourism tries to reduce harm and benefit local communities.

# Unit 6H: Quaternary Activities

Quaternary activities are knowledge-oriented services.

Examples:

- research and development;
- information technology;
- data analysis;
- education;
- software services;
- consultancy;
- media production;
- library and information services.

These activities depend more on skill and information than on raw materials.

# Unit 6I: Quinary Activities

Quinary activities involve high-level decision making and specialised knowledge.

Examples:

- top government decision makers;
- senior corporate executives;
- high-level scientific advisers;
- policy planners;
- specialised consultants.

Quinary activities guide other sectors.

# Unit 6J: Outsourcing

Outsourcing means contracting work to another company or location instead of doing it internally.

Examples:

- call centres;
- software development;
- medical transcription;
- accounting services;
- customer support;
- data processing.

## Why Outsourcing Happens

- lower labour cost;
- skilled workforce;
- time-zone advantage;
- communication technology;
- specialisation;
- efficiency.

## Effects

Positive:

- employment in service sector;
- foreign exchange earnings;
- skill development;
- global integration.

Concerns:

- job insecurity;
- pressure on workers;
- dependence on external demand;
- uneven regional development.

# Unit 6K: Services and Development

As economies develop, the share of services usually rises. Developed economies often have large tertiary and quaternary sectors.

Reasons:

- higher income increases demand for services;
- industry needs finance, transport, and communication;
- education and health expand;
- technology creates information jobs;
- urbanisation increases service demand.

# Unit 6M: Common Mistakes and Corrections

| Mistake | Correction |
|---|---|
| Saying tertiary activities produce no value | Services add value by enabling exchange and support. |
| Confusing tertiary and quaternary | Quaternary is knowledge and information-based. |
| Treating tourism as only recreation | It includes culture, health, religion, and business. |
| Ignoring negative tourism impacts | Tourism can harm environment and culture if unmanaged. |
| Defining outsourcing as migration | Work moves contractually; workers may not migrate. |

## Final Revision

- Tertiary activities provide services.
- Trade, transport, communication, banking, and tourism are tertiary activities.
- Quaternary activities are knowledge-based.
- Quinary activities involve high-level decision making.
- Tourism creates income but can create environmental pressure.
- Outsourcing is enabled by communication technology and skill differences.
