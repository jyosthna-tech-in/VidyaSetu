# Chapter 4: Primary Activities
### NCERT Class 12 - Geography Human

## Chapter at a Glance

Primary activities are directly dependent on the natural environment. They include hunting, gathering, pastoralism, fishing, forestry, mining, and agriculture. These activities use land, water, vegetation, animals, and minerals directly.

This chapter explains how primary activities vary across regions because climate, relief, soil, technology, culture, and market access differ.

## Learning Outcomes

By the end of this chapter, you should be able to:

- define primary activities;
- distinguish gathering, hunting, pastoralism, fishing, forestry, mining, and agriculture;
- explain subsistence and commercial agriculture;
- compare intensive subsistence farming and extensive commercial grain farming;
- explain plantation agriculture;
- describe mixed farming, dairy farming, and Mediterranean agriculture;
- understand mining as a primary activity;
- write comparative answers with examples.

# Unit 4A: Meaning of Primary Activities

Primary activities involve direct extraction or use of natural resources.

Examples:

- collecting forest products;
- grazing animals;
- growing crops;
- catching fish;
- cutting timber;
- mining coal or iron ore.

They form the base of many economies, especially in rural and less industrialised regions.

# Unit 4B: Gathering

Gathering involves collecting products from nature, such as fruits, nuts, roots, leaves, gums, medicinal plants, and fibres.

It is common in:

- tropical forests;
- tribal regions;
- remote areas with limited modern economic development.

Gathering usually has low capital investment and depends heavily on local ecological knowledge.

# Unit 4C: Pastoralism

Pastoralism is animal rearing based on natural pastures.

## Pastoral Nomadism

Pastoral nomads move with herds in search of pasture and water.

Common animals:

- sheep;
- goats;
- camels;
- cattle;
- yaks;
- reindeer.

It is found in dry, mountainous, or cold regions where crop farming is difficult.

## Commercial Livestock Rearing

Commercial livestock rearing is more organised and market-oriented. It uses large ranches, scientific breeding, veterinary care, and transport links.

# Unit 4D: Agriculture

Agriculture is cultivation of crops and rearing of animals. It is shaped by:

- climate;
- soil;
- relief;
- water;
- labour;
- technology;
- land ownership;
- market access.

# Unit 4E: Subsistence Agriculture

Subsistence agriculture is mainly for self-consumption.

## Primitive Subsistence Agriculture

Includes shifting cultivation. Forest is cleared, crops are grown for a short period, and land is left fallow when fertility declines.

Features:

- simple tools;
- family labour;
- low output;
- small plots;
- dependence on nature.

## Intensive Subsistence Agriculture

This is common in densely populated regions of monsoon Asia.

Features:

- small holdings;
- high labour input;
- intensive use of land;
- irrigation and manure;
- rice or wheat dominance depending on region;
- high output per unit area but low output per worker.

# Unit 4F: Commercial Agriculture

Commercial agriculture is market-oriented. Crops and livestock products are produced for sale.

It uses:

- machines;
- improved seeds;
- fertilisers;
- irrigation;
- transport;
- storage;
- market information.

# Unit 4G: Extensive Commercial Grain Farming

This type is practised in large farms of temperate grasslands.

Features:

- large landholdings;
- low population density;
- high mechanisation;
- wheat or other grains;
- low labour per unit area;
- high output per worker.

Regions include prairies, steppes, pampas, and downs.

# Unit 4H: Plantation Agriculture

Plantation agriculture is large-scale commercial farming of a single cash crop.

Features:

- large estates;
- monoculture;
- capital investment;
- scientific management;
- hired labour;
- processing units;
- export orientation.

Examples:

- tea;
- coffee;
- rubber;
- sugarcane;
- banana;
- cocoa;
- oil palm.

# Unit 4I: Mixed Farming

Mixed farming combines crop cultivation and livestock rearing.

Features:

- both crops and animals are important;
- manure from animals supports crops;
- crop residues feed animals;
- common in developed temperate regions;
- reduces risk by diversifying income.

# Unit 4J: Dairy Farming

Dairy farming focuses on milk and milk products.

Features:

- high capital and labour input;
- scientific animal care;
- refrigeration and transport;
- location near urban markets;
- cooperative organisation in many places.

# Unit 4K: Mediterranean Agriculture

Mediterranean agriculture is found around Mediterranean-type climates with wet winters and dry summers.

Products:

- grapes;
- olives;
- citrus fruits;
- vegetables;
- wheat;
- wine products.

It is specialised and market-oriented.

# Unit 4L: Mining

Mining is extraction of minerals from Earth.

Mining depends on:

- size and quality of mineral deposit;
- depth of deposit;
- technology;
- labour;
- transport;
- market demand;
- capital investment.

Types:

- surface mining;
- underground mining;
- drilling for petroleum and gas.

# Unit 4M: Comparison Toolkit

| Type | Main feature | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Shifting cultivation | Moving fields after fertility decline | Tropical forest regions |
| Intensive subsistence | High labour on small farms | Monsoon Asia |
| Extensive grain farming | Large mechanised farms | Temperate grasslands |
| Plantation | Single cash crop on estate | Tea, rubber, coffee |
| Mixed farming | Crops plus livestock | Western Europe |
| Dairy farming | Milk production | Near urban markets |

# Unit 4N: Common Mistakes and Corrections

| Mistake | Correction |
|---|---|
| Calling all agriculture subsistence | Agriculture may be subsistence or commercial. |
| Confusing intensive and extensive farming | Intensive uses high input per area; extensive uses large area. |
| Treating plantation as small farming | Plantations are large, specialised, and commercial. |
| Ignoring market access | Commercial primary activities need transport and markets. |
| Saying mining is secondary | Extraction is primary; processing minerals is secondary. |
