# Chapter 7: Transport and Communication
### NCERT Class 12 - Geography Human

## Chapter at a Glance

Transport and communication connect places, people, goods, services, and ideas. Transport moves physical objects and people, while communication moves information. Together they reduce distance, support trade, integrate markets, and shape settlement and economic development.

The chapter explains modes of transport, transport networks, pipelines, waterways, airways, communication systems, satellites, and the role of connectivity in globalisation.

## Learning Outcomes

By the end of this chapter, you should be able to:

- define transport and communication;
- compare road, rail, water, air, and pipeline transport;
- explain factors affecting transport network development;
- understand the importance of nodes and links;
- describe inland waterways, sea routes, and air routes;
- explain pipelines as transport mode;
- explain modern communication systems;
- connect transport and communication with trade and development.

## Key Terms

| Term | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Transport | Movement of people and goods from one place to another. |
| Communication | Transmission of information, ideas, and messages. |
| Network | System of nodes and links. |

# Unit 7A: Meaning of Transport

Transport creates place utility by moving goods and people where they are needed.

It supports:

- agriculture;
- industry;
- trade;
- tourism;
- defence;
- urbanisation;
- disaster response;
- regional integration.

# Unit 7B: Transport Networks

A transport network consists of nodes and links.

Examples of nodes:

- towns;
- ports;
- railway stations;
- airports;
- bus terminals;
- warehouses.

Examples of links:

- roads;
- railway lines;
- sea routes;
- air routes;
- pipelines.

A dense and efficient network improves economic development.

# Unit 7C: Road Transport

Road transport is flexible and provides door-to-door service.

Advantages:

- suitable for short distances;
- connects rural areas;
- flexible routes;
- useful for perishable goods;
- links farms to markets;
- supports last-mile delivery.

Limitations:

- congestion;
- pollution;
- accidents;
- limited capacity for very heavy bulk goods;
- affected by weather and terrain.

# Unit 7D: Rail Transport

Rail transport is suitable for long-distance movement of heavy goods and many passengers.

Advantages:

- efficient for bulk goods;
- lower cost over long land distances;
- high carrying capacity;
- energy efficient compared with road in many cases.

Limitations:

- fixed routes;
- high construction cost;
- less flexible;
- requires terminals and transshipment.

# Unit 7E: Water Transport

Water transport is one of the cheapest modes for bulky goods.

## Inland Waterways

Rivers, canals, and lakes can be used for transport if they have:

- sufficient depth;
- gentle flow;
- navigable channels;
- terminals;
- connection with markets.

## Ocean Routes

Sea routes connect continents and carry large volumes of international trade.

Advantages:

- low cost for bulk goods;
- high carrying capacity;
- suitable for petroleum, coal, ores, grains, containers.

Limitations:

- slow;
- needs ports;
- affected by storms and maritime risks.

# Unit 7F: Air Transport

Air transport is the fastest mode.

Advantages:

- very fast;
- crosses mountains, deserts, and oceans;
- useful in emergencies;
- carries high-value, light, and perishable goods;
- important for passenger movement.

Limitations:

- expensive;
- limited cargo capacity;
- high fuel use;
- affected by weather;
- needs airports and technology.

# Unit 7G: Pipeline Transport

Pipelines transport liquids, gases, and slurry.

Examples:

- petroleum;
- natural gas;
- water;
- refined products;
- mineral slurry.

Advantages:

- continuous flow;
- low operating cost after construction;
- reduces road and rail congestion;
- safe for certain materials if maintained.

Limitations:

- high initial cost;
- fixed route;
- leakage risk;
- limited to specific commodities.

# Unit 7H: Factors Affecting Transport Development

| Factor | Effect |
|---|---|
| Relief | Plains favour dense networks; mountains make construction difficult. |
| Climate | Snow, floods, deserts, and storms affect routes. |
| Economy | Trade and industry increase transport demand. |
| Technology | Better engineering expands routes. |
| Population | Dense settlements need more transport. |
| Politics | Borders and policies influence routes. |
| Capital | Infrastructure needs large investment. |

# Unit 7I: Communication

Communication is transmission of information. It may be personal, mass, or digital.

Forms:

- postal services;
- telephone;
- radio;
- television;
- internet;
- mobile networks;
- satellite systems;
- fibre-optic cables.

Communication reduces time-distance barriers and supports coordination across space.

# Unit 7J: Satellite and Digital Communication

Satellites help in:

- broadcasting;
- weather forecasting;
- navigation;
- disaster warning;
- remote sensing;
- internet connectivity;
- defence communication.

Digital communication has transformed:

- banking;
- education;
- trade;
- governance;
- health services;
- social interaction.

# Unit 7K: Transport, Communication and Globalisation

Globalisation depends on movement of goods, people, capital, and information.

Transport supports physical movement. Communication supports coordination, finance, and information exchange.

Together they enable:

- global supply chains;
- international trade;
- tourism;
- outsourcing;
- migration networks;
- cultural exchange.

# Unit 7L: Comparison Toolkit

| Mode | Main advantage | Main limitation |
|---|---|---|
| Road | Flexible and door-to-door | Congestion and lower bulk capacity |
| Rail | Heavy long-distance movement | Fixed routes |
| Water | Cheap bulk transport | Slow and port-dependent |
| Air | Fastest | Costly |
| Pipeline | Continuous flow | Commodity-specific |
| Digital communication | Instant information | Digital divide and infrastructure dependence |

# Unit 7M: Common Mistakes and Corrections

| Mistake | Correction |
|---|---|
| Treating transport and communication as same | Transport moves people and goods; communication moves information. |
| Saying air transport is best for all goods | It is costly and best for urgent or high-value goods. |
| Ignoring terrain | Relief strongly shapes transport routes. |
| Calling pipelines only for oil | They also carry gas, water, and slurry. |
| Forgetting nodes and links | Networks need both points and connecting routes. |
