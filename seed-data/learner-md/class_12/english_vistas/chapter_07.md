## Answer-Writing Frame

1. Begin with the situation in the text.
2. Identify the speaker, character, or event.
3. Explain what it reveals about theme, conflict, or emotion.
4. Use a short textual reference or paraphrase.
5. Close with the larger significance.

## Common Mistakes To Avoid

- Do not retell the whole story when the question asks for interpretation.
- A theme is not a one-word topic; it is an idea about life or society.
- Vocabulary questions require context, not only dictionary meaning.
