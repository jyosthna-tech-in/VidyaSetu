# Chapter 1: Accounting for Share Capital
### NCERT Class 12 - Accountancy Part 2

## Chapter at a Glance

| Item | Details |
|---|---|
| Main idea | A company raises ownership capital by issuing shares and records each stage of application, allotment, call, forfeiture, and reissue. |
| Main share classes | Equity shares and preference shares. |
| Main issue prices | At par and at premium; issue at discount is generally restricted by law. |
| Main accounts | Share capital, securities premium, share application, share allotment, share call, calls in arrears, calls in advance, forfeited shares. |
| Most important skill | Trace amount due, amount received, and unpaid amount at each stage. |
| Final focus | Calculate capital reserve on reissue of forfeited shares. |

## Learning Outcomes

By the end of this chapter, you should be able to:

1. Explain the main features of a company.
2. Distinguish equity shares from preference shares.
3. Record issue of shares at par.
4. Record issue of shares at premium.
5. Treat over-subscription and pro-rata allotment.
6. Treat under-subscription.
7. Record calls in arrears and calls in advance.
8. Record issue of shares for consideration other than cash.
9. Record forfeiture of shares for non-payment.
10. Record reissue of forfeited shares.
11. Calculate capital reserve from forfeiture and reissue.
12. Present share capital-related balances correctly.

|---|---|

# Unit 1A: Features of a Company

A company is a legal form of business organisation created under law. It is distinct from its members and can own property, enter contracts, sue, and be sued in its own name.

## Main Features

| Feature | Explanation |
|---|---|
| Separate legal entity | Company is separate from shareholders. |
| Limited liability | Shareholders are liable only up to unpaid amount on shares. |
| Perpetual succession | Company continues despite death, insolvency, or change of members. |
| Transferability of shares | Shares of public companies are generally transferable. |
| Common seal or official authorisation | Company acts through authorised signatories and formal documents. |
| Artificial person | It is created by law, not by natural birth. |
| Capacity to sue and be sued | It can enforce rights and face legal claims. |

# Unit 1B: Kinds of Companies by Liability

| Type | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Company limited by shares | Members' liability is limited to unpaid amount on shares held. |
| Company limited by guarantee | Members' liability is limited to amount guaranteed in winding up. |
| Unlimited company | Members may have unlimited liability. |

# Unit 1C: Share Capital of a Company

Share capital is the amount raised by issue of shares.

## Capital Categories

| Category | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Authorised capital | Maximum capital company may issue. |
| Issued capital | Portion offered to public or others. |
| Subscribed capital | Portion actually taken up. |
| Called-up capital | Amount demanded on subscribed shares. |
| Paid-up capital | Amount actually received. |
| Uncalled capital | Amount not yet demanded. |
| Reserve capital | Uncalled amount that company resolves to call only on winding up. |

## Example

Authorised capital: 1,00,000 shares of Rs. 10 = Rs. 10,00,000.

Issued capital: 60,000 shares = Rs. 6,00,000.

Subscribed capital: 50,000 shares = Rs. 5,00,000.

If Rs. 8 per share is called and Rs. 7.50 per share is received on average, called-up capital and paid-up capital differ because of arrears.

# Unit 1D: Classes of Shares

## Preference Shares

Preference shares carry preferential rights:

1. Preference in dividend payment.
2. Preference in repayment of capital at winding up.

## Types of Preference Shares

| Type | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Cumulative | Unpaid dividend accumulates. |
| Non-cumulative | Unpaid dividend does not accumulate. |
| Participating | May receive extra dividend after equity shareholders. |
| Non-participating | Receives only fixed preference dividend. |
| Convertible | Can be converted into equity shares. |
| Non-convertible | Cannot be converted. |
| Redeemable | Repaid after a specified period or as per terms. |

## Equity Shares

Equity shareholders are real risk bearers. They receive dividend after preference shareholders and have voting rights. Their return is not fixed.

| Preference Shares | Equity Shares |
|---|---|
| Preferential dividend. | Dividend after preference dividend. |
| Usually fixed rate. | Variable dividend. |
| Less risk than equity. | Higher risk. |
| Limited voting rights in many cases. | Main voting rights. |
| Repayment preference in winding up. | Residual claim after prior claims. |

# Unit 1E: Issue of Shares

A company may issue shares:

1. For cash.
2. For consideration other than cash.
3. At par.
4. At premium.

## Stages in Cash Issue

| Stage | What Happens |
|---|---|
| Application | Applicant applies and pays application money. |
| Allotment | Company allots shares and allotment money becomes due. |
| First call | Company demands part of remaining amount. |
| Final call | Company demands final balance. |

# Unit 1F: Issue of Shares at Par

When shares are issued at face value, they are issued at par.

## Journal Entries

| Event | Debit | Credit |
|---|---|---|
| Application money received | Bank A/c | Share Application A/c |
| Application transferred on allotment | Share Application A/c | Share Capital A/c |
| Allotment money due | Share Allotment A/c | Share Capital A/c |
| Allotment money received | Bank A/c | Share Allotment A/c |
| Call money due | Share Call A/c | Share Capital A/c |
| Call money received | Bank A/c | Share Call A/c |

## Amount Logic

Share capital is credited only when shares are allotted or calls become due, not merely when money is received in advance.

# Unit 1G: Issue of Shares at Premium

When shares are issued above face value, the excess is securities premium.

Example: Share face value Rs. 10 issued at Rs. 12.

Premium = Rs. 2 per share.

## Entry When Premium Is Due on Allotment

| Debit | Credit |
|---|---|
| Share Allotment A/c | Share Capital A/c and Securities Premium A/c |

## Uses of Securities Premium

Securities premium is a capital reserve. It may be used only for purposes allowed by law, such as:

1. Issuing fully paid bonus shares.
2. Writing off preliminary expenses.
3. Writing off commission, discount, or expense on issue of securities.
4. Providing premium payable on redemption of preference shares or debentures.
5. Buy-back-related purposes as permitted by law.

# Unit 1H: Calls in Arrears

Calls in arrears means amount called by company but not paid by shareholders.

## Accounting Treatment

If calls in arrears account is opened:

| Debit | Credit |
|---|---|
| Calls in Arrears A/c | Relevant Call A/c |

When arrears are received:

| Debit | Credit |
|---|---|
| Bank A/c | Calls in Arrears A/c |

## Presentation

Calls in arrears are deducted from called-up share capital.

# Unit 1I: Calls in Advance

Calls in advance means money received from shareholders before the company has called it.

## Entry

| Debit | Credit |
|---|---|
| Bank A/c | Calls in Advance A/c |

When the call becomes due:

| Debit | Credit |
|---|---|
| Calls in Advance A/c | Relevant Call A/c |

## Important Point

Calls in advance are a liability. They are not part of share capital until the call becomes due.

# Unit 1J: Over-Subscription and Pro-Rata Allotment

Over-subscription occurs when applications are received for more shares than issued.

The company may:

1. Reject some applications and refund money.
2. Make full allotment to some applicants.
3. Make pro-rata allotment to some applicants.
4. Use excess application money toward allotment or calls.

## Pro-Rata Logic

If 1,00,000 shares are applied for and 80,000 are allotted, allotment ratio is 80,000:1,00,000 = 4:5.

A person applying for 500 shares receives 400 shares.

Excess application money for 100 shares may be adjusted against allotment due.

## Entry for Refund

| Debit | Credit |
|---|---|
| Share Application A/c | Bank A/c |

## Entry for Transfer of Excess to Allotment

| Debit | Credit |
|---|---|
| Share Application A/c | Share Allotment A/c |

# Unit 1K: Under-Subscription

Under-subscription occurs when applications are received for fewer shares than offered.

If minimum subscription requirement is met, allotment may be made for shares applied. If not, application money must be refunded.

# Unit 1L: Issue for Consideration Other Than Cash

A company may issue shares to vendors for purchase of assets or business.

## Entries

| Event | Debit | Credit |
|---|---|---|
| Assets purchased | Assets A/c | Vendor A/c |
| Shares issued to vendor | Vendor A/c | Share Capital A/c and Securities Premium A/c, if any |

If shares are issued at discount in a permitted situation, credit share capital with face value and debit relevant discount or capital adjustment as per applicable law and question terms.

# Unit 1M: Forfeiture of Shares

Forfeiture means cancellation of shares because the shareholder fails to pay due amount.

## Entry for Forfeiture of Shares Issued at Par

| Debit | Credit |
|---|---|
| Share Capital A/c, with called-up amount | Forfeited Shares A/c, with amount received; Calls in Arrears A/c, with unpaid amount |

## Forfeiture of Shares Issued at Premium

Premium treatment depends on whether premium has been received.

| Situation | Treatment |
|---|---|
| Premium received | Do not reverse securities premium. |
| Premium due but not received | Debit securities premium to cancel unpaid premium. |

## Entry When Premium Was Due but Not Received

| Debit | Credit |
|---|---|
| Share Capital A/c and Securities Premium A/c | Forfeited Shares A/c and Calls in Arrears A/c |

# Unit 1N: Reissue of Forfeited Shares

Forfeited shares may be reissued. The discount on reissue cannot exceed the amount forfeited on those shares.

## Entry for Reissue

| Debit | Credit |
|---|---|
| Bank A/c and Forfeited Shares A/c, for discount if any | Share Capital A/c |

If reissued at premium:

| Debit | Credit |
|---|---|
| Bank A/c | Share Capital A/c and Securities Premium A/c |

## Capital Reserve

After reissue, remaining balance in forfeited shares account relating to reissued shares is transferred to capital reserve.

Entry:

| Debit | Credit |
|---|---|
| Forfeited Shares A/c | Capital Reserve A/c |

## Capital Reserve Formula

Capital reserve = Amount forfeited on reissued shares - Discount allowed on reissue

# Unit 1O: Worked Mini Case

A company issued 10,000 shares of Rs. 10 each at par. Rs. 3 on application, Rs. 4 on allotment, Rs. 3 on final call. A shareholder holding 500 shares failed to pay allotment and final call. Shares were forfeited and reissued at Rs. 8 per share fully paid.

## Forfeiture

Called-up amount per share = Rs. 10.

Amount received per share = Rs. 3.

Unpaid per share = Rs. 7.

For 500 shares:

| Item | Amount |
|---|---:|
| Share capital debited | Rs. 5,000 |
| Forfeited shares credited | Rs. 1,500 |
| Calls in arrears credited | Rs. 3,500 |

## Reissue

Reissue price = Rs. 8.

Discount on reissue = Rs. 2 per share x 500 = Rs. 1,000.

Forfeited amount available = Rs. 1,500.

Capital reserve = Rs. 500.

# Unit 1P: Common Mistakes and Corrections

| Mistake | Correct Approach |
|---|---|
| Crediting share capital when application money is received | Credit share application first; transfer on allotment. |
| Treating calls in advance as capital immediately | Show as liability until due. |
| Forgetting securities premium on allotment | Credit securities premium when premium becomes due. |
| Reversing premium even though received | Reverse only unpaid premium. |
| Allowing reissue discount above forfeited amount | Discount cannot exceed amount forfeited on those shares. |
| Transferring full forfeited balance to capital reserve before reissue | Transfer only after relevant shares are reissued. |

## Practice Set

1. Journalise issue of 20,000 shares of Rs. 10 each payable Rs. 2 on application, Rs. 5 on allotment, and Rs. 3 on call.
2. A company issues shares of Rs. 10 at Rs. 12. Premium is payable on allotment. Write the allotment due entry.
3. Applications are received for 60,000 shares when only 40,000 shares are issued. Allotment is pro-rata. Find shares allotted to an applicant for 900 shares.
4. 300 shares of Rs. 10 each are forfeited after Rs. 6 per share has been received. They are reissued at Rs. 8. Find capital reserve.
5. Explain why calls in advance are not share capital.

## Final Revision

Share capital accounting is stage-based. Track what is due, what is received, what is unpaid, and what belongs to capital reserve. For forfeiture and reissue, the controlling rule is simple: the company cannot give a reissue discount greater than the amount already forfeited on those shares.
