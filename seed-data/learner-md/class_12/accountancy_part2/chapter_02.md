# Unit 2A: Meaning of Debenture

A debenture is an instrument acknowledging debt. It shows that the company has borrowed money and agrees to repay principal and pay interest as per terms.

Debentureholders are creditors, not owners. They do not receive dividend. They receive interest.

## Bond and Debenture

Both represent borrowing. The term bond is often used for government and large institutional borrowings, while debenture is common in company accounting. In many contexts, the terms are used similarly.

# Unit 2B: Shares vs Debentures

| Basis | Shares | Debentures |
|---|---|---|
| Nature | Ownership capital. | Borrowed capital. |
| Holder | Shareholder is owner. | Debentureholder is creditor. |
| Return | Dividend. | Interest. |
| Return certainty | Dividend depends on profit and decision. | Interest is payable even if there is loss. |
| Voting rights | Equity shareholders usually vote. | Debentureholders usually do not vote. |
| Security | Not secured by assets. | May be secured by charge on assets. |
| Repayment | Equity is normally not repaid during life of company. | Redeemable as per terms. |
| Risk | Higher. | Lower than equity. |

# Unit 2C: Types of Debentures

## By Security

| Type | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Secured | Carry charge on company assets. |
| Unsecured | No specific asset charge. |

## By Tenure

| Type | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Redeemable | Repaid after a specified period. |
| Irredeemable | No fixed repayment date, though legal provisions may restrict practical use. |

## By Convertibility

| Type | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Convertible | Can be converted into shares or other securities. |
| Non-convertible | Cannot be converted. |
| Fully convertible | Entire debenture converts. |
| Partly convertible | Only part converts; balance remains debt or is redeemed. |

## By Coupon

| Type | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Specific coupon | Carries a stated interest rate. |
| Zero coupon | Issued at deep discount and redeemed later, without regular interest. |

## By Registration

| Type | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Registered | Holder details are recorded by company. |
| Bearer | Transferable by delivery, where permitted. |

# Unit 2D: Issue of Debentures for Cash

Debentures may be issued at:

1. Par.
2. Premium.
3. Discount.

## Issue at Par

Debenture face value equals issue price.

| Event | Debit | Credit |
|---|---|---|
| Application money received | Bank A/c | Debenture Application A/c |
| Debentures allotted | Debenture Application A/c | Debentures A/c |

If collected in stages:

| Event | Debit | Credit |
|---|---|---|
| Allotment due | Debenture Allotment A/c | Debentures A/c |
| Allotment received | Bank A/c | Debenture Allotment A/c |

## Issue at Premium

Issue price is above face value.

| Debit | Credit |
|---|---|
| Bank or Debenture Allotment A/c | Debentures A/c and Securities Premium A/c |

## Issue at Discount

Issue price is below face value.

| Debit | Credit |
|---|---|
| Bank A/c and Discount on Issue of Debentures A/c | Debentures A/c |

Discount is a capital loss and is written off over the period benefiting from the borrowing.

# Unit 2E: Over-Subscription of Debentures

If applications exceed debentures offered, the company may:

1. Reject some applications and refund money.
2. Make pro-rata allotment.
3. Adjust excess application money toward allotment.

The logic is similar to share issue, but the account names are debenture application and debenture allotment.

# Unit 2F: Issue for Consideration Other Than Cash

A company may issue debentures to vendors for purchase of assets or business.

## Entries

| Event | Debit | Credit |
|---|---|---|
| Assets purchased | Assets A/c | Vendor A/c |
| Debentures issued at par | Vendor A/c | Debentures A/c |
| Debentures issued at premium | Vendor A/c | Debentures A/c and Securities Premium A/c |
| Debentures issued at discount | Vendor A/c and Discount on Issue A/c | Debentures A/c |

## Purchase Consideration Logic

If assets are purchased for Rs. 5,00,000 and debentures of Rs. 100 each are issued at par, number of debentures = 5,00,000 / 100 = 5,000.

If issued at Rs. 125, number of debentures = 5,00,000 / 125 = 4,000, but debentures account is credited with face value: 4,000 x Rs. 100 = Rs. 4,00,000. Securities premium = Rs. 1,00,000.

# Unit 2G: Debentures as Collateral Security

Collateral security is secondary security for a loan. If the company repays the main loan, debentures given as collateral are returned or cancelled.

## Method 1: No Journal Entry

No entry is passed. A note is added below the loan in balance sheet that debentures are deposited as collateral security.

## Method 2: Journal Entry

Entry:

| Debit | Credit |
|---|---|
| Debenture Suspense A/c | Debentures A/c |

Presentation:

1. Debentures appear as liability.
2. Debenture suspense appears as deduction or asset as per format used.

The first method is simpler and often preferred unless the question asks for entries.

# Unit 2H: Terms of Issue and Redemption

Issue price and redemption price may differ.

| Case | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Issued at par, redeemable at par | No discount or premium. |
| Issued at discount, redeemable at par | Discount on issue. |
| Issued at premium, redeemable at par | Securities premium on issue. |
| Issued at par, redeemable at premium | Premium payable on redemption. |
| Issued at discount, redeemable at premium | Loss on issue includes discount and redemption premium. |
| Issued at premium, redeemable at premium | Premium received and premium payable treated separately. |

## Premium Payable on Redemption

When premium on redemption is recognised at issue:

| Debit | Credit |
|---|---|
| Loss on Issue of Debentures A/c | Premium on Redemption of Debentures A/c |

The debenture account is credited with face value. Premium payable is a liability.

# Unit 2I: Interest on Debentures

Debenture interest is payable at the coupon rate on face value unless question states otherwise.

Interest = Face value of debentures x Rate x Time / 100

## Entry for Interest Due

| Debit | Credit |
|---|---|
| Debenture Interest A/c | Debentureholders A/c |

## Entry for Tax Deducted at Payment, if applicable

| Debit | Credit |
|---|---|
| Debentureholders A/c | Bank A/c and Income Tax Payable A/c |

## Transfer of Interest

| Debit | Credit |
|---|---|
| Statement of Profit and Loss A/c | Debenture Interest A/c |

Interest is a charge against profit.

# Unit 2J: Writing Off Discount or Loss on Issue

Discount or loss on issue of debentures should be written off over the period benefited by the debenture finance.

## Simple Equal Write-Off

If debentures are redeemed in lump sum at the end of a period, write off equally over that period unless instructed otherwise.

Annual write-off = Total discount or loss / Number of years

## Variable Balance Write-Off

If debentures are redeemed in instalments, write off in the ratio of outstanding debenture balance because benefit declines as debentures are repaid.

# Unit 2K: Redemption of Debentures

Redemption means repayment or discharge of debentures.

Methods:

1. Lump sum payment.
2. Instalment payment.
3. Purchase in open market.
4. Conversion into shares or new debentures.
5. Sinking fund method.

## Debenture Redemption Reserve

Where applicable, a reserve is created out of profits before redemption. It shows that profits are retained for repayment rather than fully distributed.

Entry:

| Debit | Credit |
|---|---|
| Surplus in Statement of Profit and Loss A/c | Debenture Redemption Reserve A/c |

# Unit 2L: Redemption by Lump Sum

## Debentures Due for Redemption at Par

| Debit | Credit |
|---|---|
| Debentures A/c | Debentureholders A/c |

Payment:

| Debit | Credit |
|---|---|
| Debentureholders A/c | Bank A/c |

## Redemption at Premium

When due:

| Debit | Credit |
|---|---|
| Debentures A/c and Premium on Redemption A/c | Debentureholders A/c |

Payment:

| Debit | Credit |
|---|---|
| Debentureholders A/c | Bank A/c |

# Unit 2M: Redemption by Instalments

When debentures are redeemed in instalments, each instalment is treated as debentures becoming due and then paid.

The discount or loss write-off should match the outstanding debentures if required.

# Unit 2N: Purchase in Open Market

A company may buy its own debentures in the market for cancellation.

If purchase price is below face value, there is profit on cancellation.

If purchase price is above face value, there is loss.

## Entry When Debentures Are Purchased for Cancellation

| Debit | Credit |
|---|---|
| Own Debentures A/c | Bank A/c |

Cancellation:

| Debit | Credit |
|---|---|
| Debentures A/c | Own Debentures A/c and Profit on Cancellation A/c |

If there is loss, debit loss on cancellation.

# Unit 2O: Redemption by Conversion

Debentures may be converted into shares or new debentures.

## Conversion into Shares

| Debit | Credit |
|---|---|
| Debentureholders A/c | Share Capital A/c and Securities Premium A/c, if any |

If shares are issued at premium, credit securities premium.

## Conversion into New Debentures

| Debit | Credit |
|---|---|
| Old Debentureholders A/c | New Debentures A/c |

# Unit 2P: Sinking Fund Method

Under sinking fund method, the company sets aside profits every year and invests them so that money is available for redemption.

## Broad Steps

1. Transfer annual amount to sinking fund.
2. Invest the same amount outside the business.
3. Receive interest on investment.
4. Reinvest interest if required.
5. Sell investments at redemption.
6. Use proceeds to redeem debentures.
7. Transfer remaining fund balance as instructed.

## Why It Matters

Sinking fund protects liquidity. It prevents a sudden cash burden at redemption date.

# Unit 2Q: Worked Mini Case

A company issues 1,000 debentures of Rs. 100 each at Rs. 95, redeemable at Rs. 105 after five years.

## Amounts

| Item | Amount |
|---|---:|
| Face value | Rs. 1,00,000 |
| Cash received | Rs. 95,000 |
| Discount on issue | Rs. 5,000 |
| Premium on redemption | Rs. 5,000 |
| Total loss on issue | Rs. 10,000 |

## Issue Entry

| Debit | Credit |
|---|---|
| Bank A/c Rs. 95,000; Loss on Issue of Debentures A/c Rs. 10,000 | Debentures A/c Rs. 1,00,000; Premium on Redemption A/c Rs. 5,000 |

If written off equally for five years:

Annual write-off = Rs. 10,000 / 5 = Rs. 2,000.

# Unit 2R: Common Mistakes and Corrections

| Mistake | Correct Approach |
|---|---|
| Treating debenture interest like dividend | Interest is a charge against profit. |
| Crediting debentures with issue price | Credit debentures with face value. |
| Mixing premium on issue with premium on redemption | Premium on issue is securities premium; premium on redemption is liability. |
| Ignoring loss on issue when redemption is at premium | Loss includes discount plus redemption premium. |
| Passing collateral entry when question says only note | Follow the method required. |
| Writing off discount all at once without instruction | Spread over benefit period. |

## Practice Set

1. Record issue of 2,000 debentures of Rs. 100 each at par.
2. Record issue of 1,000 debentures of Rs. 100 each at 10 per cent premium.
3. Record issue of debentures of Rs. 5,00,000 as collateral security for a bank loan using both methods.
4. Debentures of Rs. 2,00,000 are issued at 5 per cent discount and redeemable at 10 per cent premium after five years. Find total loss on issue and annual write-off if equal method is used.
5. Debentures of Rs. 1,00,000 are redeemed at 105. Pass the due and payment entries.

## Final Revision

Debenture accounting is debt accounting. Always separate face value, issue proceeds, issue premium or discount, redemption premium, and interest. The debenture account normally tracks face value; the difference between cash flow and face value is handled through premium, discount, or loss accounts.
