# Unit 7A: Meaning of Directing

Directing means instructing, guiding, counselling, motivating, leading, supervising, and communicating with people so organisational objectives are achieved.

Directing is the function that converts plans into action.

## Simple Example

A sales manager may explain sales targets, guide salespeople, motivate them with incentives, lead by example, and communicate customer feedback. This is directing.

# Unit 7B: Features of Directing

## 1. Directing Initiates Action

Employees start working toward goals through instructions and guidance.

## 2. Directing Takes Place at Every Level

Every manager directs subordinates.

## 3. Directing Is Continuous

Guidance, motivation, and communication are needed throughout organisational life.

## 4. Directing Flows Downward

Directions usually move from superior to subordinate.

## 5. Directing Is Human-Centred

It deals with people, behaviour, needs, emotions, and relationships.

# Unit 7C: Importance of Directing

## 1. Initiates Action

Without directing, plans remain on paper.

## 2. Integrates Employee Efforts

Directing aligns individual work with organisational goals.

## 3. Guides Employees

Employees understand duties, methods, and expectations.

## 4. Improves Efficiency

Motivated and guided employees perform better.

## 5. Facilitates Change

Good leadership and communication reduce resistance to change.

## 6. Provides Stability and Balance

Directing improves cooperation and reduces conflict.

# Unit 7D: Principles of Directing

| Principle | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Maximum individual contribution | Directing should bring out best effort from each person. |
| Harmony of objectives | Individual and organisational goals should be aligned. |
| Unity of command | One subordinate should receive orders from one superior. |
| Appropriate technique | Directing method should suit people and situation. |
| Managerial communication | Instructions must be clear and understood. |
| Use of informal organisation | Managers should use informal groups constructively. |
| Leadership | Managers should influence and guide by example. |
| Follow-through | Managers should check whether directions are carried out. |

# Unit 7E: Supervision

Supervision means overseeing subordinates at work and guiding them to perform properly.

## Importance of Supervision

1. Maintains direct contact with workers.
2. Links management and workers.
3. Ensures proper use of resources.
4. Provides on-the-spot guidance.
5. Improves discipline.
6. Gives feedback to managers.
7. Helps solve worker problems.

## Motivation Process

4. Search behaviour.
5. Need satisfaction.

## Features of Motivation

# Unit 7H: Maslow's Need Hierarchy

Maslow arranged human needs in a hierarchy.

## Levels

| Level | Need | Workplace Example |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Physiological needs | Salary for food, clothing, shelter. |
| 2 | Safety needs | Job security and safe workplace. |
| 3 | Social needs | Belonging, friendship, team relations. |
| 4 | Esteem needs | Recognition, status, respect. |
| 5 | Self-actualisation needs | Growth, creativity, achievement of potential. |

## Main Idea

People first try to satisfy lower-level needs. Once a need is reasonably satisfied, higher-level needs become more important.

## Limitations

1. People may not always follow the same order.
2. Several needs may operate together.
3. Need priority differs from person to person.

| Financial Incentives | Non-Financial Incentives |
|---|---|
| Monetary in nature. | Psychological or social in nature. |

# Unit 7J: Leadership

Leadership is the ability to influence people so they work willingly toward group goals.

## Importance

1. Influences behaviour.
2. Builds confidence.
3. Creates vision.
4. Handles change.
5. Resolves conflict.
6. Improves motivation.

## Qualities of a Good Leader

1. Physical energy and alertness.
2. Knowledge.
3. Integrity.
4. Initiative.
5. Communication skill.
6. Motivation skill.
7. Self-confidence.
8. Decisiveness.
9. Social skill.
10. Responsibility.

## Leadership Styles

| Style | Meaning | Likely Effect |
|---|---|---|
| Autocratic | Leader makes decisions alone. | Quick decisions but low participation. |
| Democratic | Leader consults subordinates. | Higher participation and morale. |
| Laissez-faire | Subordinates get high freedom. | Works with skilled and responsible teams. |

## Formal Communication

Formal communication follows official channels.

Directions:

1. Downward.
2. Upward.
3. Horizontal.
4. Diagonal.

## Informal Communication

Informal communication flows through social relationships. It is fast but may carry rumours.

## Grapevine Patterns

1. Single strand.
2. Gossip.
3. Probability.
4. Cluster.

# Unit 7M: Communication Barriers

## Semantic Barriers

Problems of words and meaning:

1. Badly expressed message.
2. Symbols with different meanings.
3. Faulty translation.
4. Technical jargon.

## Psychological Barriers

1. Premature evaluation.
2. Lack of attention.
3. Distrust.
4. Fear.
5. Poor retention.

## Organisational Barriers

1. Complex structure.
2. Status differences.
3. Rules and regulations.
4. Lack of facilities.

## Personal Barriers

1. Fear of challenge to authority.
2. Lack of confidence.
3. Unwillingness to communicate.
4. Lack of proper incentives.

# Unit 7N: Improving Communication

1. Clarify ideas before communication.
2. Communicate according to receiver's needs.
3. Use simple language.
4. Be aware of tone and body language.
5. Ensure proper feedback.
6. Be a good listener.
7. Follow up communication.
8. Build trust.
9. Avoid overload.
10. Use both formal and informal channels wisely.

# Unit 7O: Common Mistakes and Corrections

| Mistake | Correct Understanding |
|---|---|
| Directing means only giving orders | It includes supervision, motivation, leadership, and communication. |
| Motivation is same for everyone | Needs differ from person to person. |
| Money is the only motivator | Non-financial incentives can be equally important. |
| Leadership and management are identical | Leadership is influence; management is wider. |
| Communication is complete when message is sent | It is complete only when understood and feedback is possible. |
| Informal communication is always bad | It can support speed and social bonding if handled wisely. |

## Communication Repair Table

| Barrier | Symptom | Remedy |
|---|---|---|
| Technical jargon | Receiver does not understand message. | Use simple language. |
| Premature evaluation | Receiver judges before listening fully. | Encourage patient listening. |
| Status barrier | Subordinate fears speaking truth. | Build trust and openness. |
| Information overload | Receiver misses key points. | Prioritise and summarise. |
| Poor feedback | Sender does not know if message was understood. | Ask for confirmation and response. |
| Faulty translation | Meaning changes across languages or levels. | Use clear translation and examples. |

## Leadership Style Selection

| Situation | Possible Style | Reason |
|---|---|---|
| Emergency requiring quick decision | Autocratic | Speed is essential. |
| Skilled team solving complex problem | Democratic | Participation improves decision quality. |
| Research team with experts | Laissez-faire with review | Experts need freedom, but goals still matter. |
| New workers needing close guidance | Autocratic or coaching-oriented | Clear direction is needed. |
| Change programme affecting employees | Democratic | Participation reduces resistance. |

## Case Answer Framework

1. Identify the element of directing.
2. Define it.
3. Link the case clue to the element.
4. Explain its importance.
5. Add correction if the case shows failure.

## High-Quality Example

Case: A manager announces a new attendance system using a long technical circular. Workers misunderstand the rule and rumours spread that salaries will be cut.

Answer:

1. The issue is ineffective communication.
2. Semantic barriers and poor explanation created misunderstanding.
3. Informal communication spread rumours because formal communication was unclear.
4. The manager should use simple language, hold a short meeting, invite questions, and obtain feedback.
5. Clear communication will reduce fear and improve acceptance.

## Final Revision

Directing is the action function of management. It works through supervision, motivation, leadership, and communication. In case questions, identify whether the issue is guidance, willingness, influence, or information flow.
