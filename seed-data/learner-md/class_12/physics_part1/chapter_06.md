# Chapter 6: Electromagnetic Induction
### NCERT Class 12 - Physics Part 1

## Chapter at a Glance

Electromagnetic induction is the production of emf and current due to changing magnetic flux. This chapter explains Faraday's experiments, magnetic flux, Faraday's law, Lenz's law, motional emf, self-inductance, mutual inductance, magnetic energy, and AC generator.

The central idea is that changing magnetic flux induces emf.

## Learning Outcomes

By the end of this chapter, learners should be able to:

- Define magnetic flux.
- Explain Faraday's induction experiments.
- State Faraday's law of electromagnetic induction.
- Apply Lenz's law to find direction of induced current.
- Understand conservation of energy in induction.
- Calculate motional emf.
- Explain self-inductance and mutual inductance.
- Calculate energy stored in an inductor.
- Explain working of an AC generator.

## Key Terms

| Term | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Magnetic flux | Measure of magnetic field passing through a surface. |
| Induced emf | emf produced due to changing magnetic flux. |
| Induced current | Current produced by induced emf in a closed circuit. |
| Faraday's law | Induced emf equals negative rate of change of magnetic flux. |
| Lenz's law | Induced current opposes the change producing it. |
| Motional emf | emf induced by motion of conductor in magnetic field. |
| Self-inductance | Property by which changing current in a coil induces emf in same coil. |
| Mutual inductance | Changing current in one coil induces emf in nearby coil. |
| AC generator | Device converting mechanical energy into alternating electrical energy. |

# Unit 6A: Faraday's Experiments

Faraday showed that current can be induced in a circuit without a battery if magnetic flux linked with the circuit changes.

Induced current is observed when:

- A magnet moves toward or away from a coil.
- A coil moves near a magnet.
- Current in a nearby coil changes.
- Area or orientation of a loop in magnetic field changes.

## Key Lesson

Magnetic field alone is not enough. Change in magnetic flux is required.

# Unit 6B: Magnetic Flux

Magnetic flux through a surface is:

`Phi = B A cos theta`

where:

- `B` is magnetic field.
- `A` is area.
- `theta` is angle between magnetic field and area vector.

SI unit:

`weber (Wb)`

## Ways To Change Flux

Flux can change by changing:

- Magnetic field `B`.
- Area `A`.
- Angle `theta`.

# Unit 6C: Faraday's Law

Faraday's law states:

`epsilon = -dPhi/dt`

For a coil of `N` turns:

`epsilon = -N dPhi/dt`

Magnitude:

`|epsilon| = N |dPhi/dt|`

## Meaning

Faster change of flux produces larger induced emf.

# Unit 6D: Lenz's Law

The negative sign in Faraday's law is explained by Lenz's law.

Lenz's law:

The direction of induced current is such that it opposes the change in magnetic flux that produces it.

## Conservation of Energy

If induced current helped the change instead of opposing it, energy would be created from nothing. Lenz's law ensures energy conservation.

# Unit 6E: Finding Direction of Induced Current

Use this method:

1. Identify original magnetic flux direction.
2. Decide whether flux is increasing or decreasing.
3. Induced current creates magnetic field opposing the change.
4. Use right-hand rule to find current direction.

## Example Pattern

If north pole of magnet approaches a coil, magnetic flux through coil increases. The coil opposes approach by making its near face north. Current direction is then found from right-hand rule.

# Unit 6F: Motional emf

When a conducting rod of length `l` moves with speed `v` perpendicular to magnetic field `B`, emf induced is:

`epsilon = B l v`

## Reason

Free charges in rod experience magnetic force:

`F = qvB`

This separates charges and creates potential difference.

## Direction

Use `v x B` for positive charges.

# Unit 6G: Rotating Rod

If a rod of length `l` rotates with angular speed `omega` in a magnetic field perpendicular to its plane, emf between centre and end is:

`epsilon = (1/2)B omega l^2`

The far end has larger speed than points near the axis, so integration over length gives factor `1/2`.

# Unit 6H: Eddy Currents

Changing magnetic flux in bulk conductors can induce circulating currents called eddy currents.

## Effects

Eddy currents can cause:

- Heating.
- Energy loss.
- Magnetic braking.

## Reducing Eddy Currents

Use laminated cores in transformers and machines. Thin insulated sheets reduce circulating current paths.

## Uses

Eddy currents are used in:

- Induction furnaces.
- Magnetic braking.
- Speedometers.
- Metal detectors.

# Unit 6I: Self-Inductance

When current in a coil changes, magnetic flux linked with the same coil changes. This induces emf in the coil itself.

Self-induced emf:

`epsilon = -L dI/dt`

where `L` is self-inductance.

SI unit:

`henry (H)`

## Meaning

An inductor opposes change in current.

# Unit 6J: Mutual Inductance

If current in one coil changes, magnetic flux linked with a nearby coil changes, inducing emf in the second coil.

`epsilon2 = -M dI1/dt`

where `M` is mutual inductance.

## Depends On

Mutual inductance depends on:

- Number of turns.
- Geometry of coils.
- Separation.
- Orientation.
- Magnetic material between coils.

# Unit 6K: Energy Stored in an Inductor

Energy stored in an inductor carrying current `I`:

`U = (1/2)L I^2`

This energy is stored in magnetic field.

## Analogy

Capacitor stores energy in electric field:

`U = (1/2)CV^2`

Inductor stores energy in magnetic field:

`U = (1/2)L I^2`

# Unit 6L: AC Generator

An AC generator converts mechanical energy into electrical energy using electromagnetic induction.

A coil rotates in magnetic field, causing magnetic flux through it to change.

Flux:

`Phi = NBA cos(omega t)`

Induced emf:

`epsilon = NBA omega sin(omega t)`

Maximum emf:

`epsilon0 = NBA omega`

## Output

The output emf alternates with time, so it is alternating current.

# Unit 6M: Worked Examples

## Example 1: Changing Flux

Flux through a coil changes from `0.4 Wb` to `0.1 Wb` in `0.2 s`. Find average emf for one turn.

`|epsilon| = |Delta Phi|/Delta t`

`= |0.1 - 0.4|/0.2`

`= 1.5 V`

## Example 2: Motional emf

A rod of length `0.5 m` moves at `4 m/s` perpendicular to `0.2 T` field.

`epsilon = Blv`

`epsilon = (0.2)(0.5)(4) = 0.4 V`

## Example 3: Inductor Energy

If `L = 2 H` and `I = 3 A`:

`U = (1/2)LI^2`

`U = (1/2)(2)(9) = 9 J`

# Unit 6N: Answer-Writing Framework

## To solve induced emf problems

Write in this order:

1. Identify magnetic flux expression.
2. Find change in flux or rate of change.
3. Use `epsilon = -N dPhi/dt`.
4. Use Lenz's law for direction if asked.

## To apply Lenz's law

Write in this order:

1. State whether flux is increasing or decreasing.
2. State direction of original flux.
3. State direction of induced field opposing change.
4. Use right-hand rule for induced current.

## To solve motional emf problems

Write in this order:

1. Check that velocity is perpendicular to field.
2. Use `epsilon = Blv`.
3. Use `v x B` for polarity.
4. State result in volt.

# Unit 6O: Common Mistakes and Corrections

| Mistake | Correction |
|---|---|
| Thinking magnetic field alone induces current | Changing flux is required. |
| Forgetting number of turns | Use `N dPhi/dt` for a coil. |
| Ignoring negative sign meaning | Negative sign represents Lenz's law. |
| Applying `Blv` when motion is not perpendicular | Use perpendicular component of velocity or field. |
| Confusing self and mutual inductance | Self is same coil; mutual involves another coil. |
| Forgetting factor `1/2` in rotating rod emf | Rotating rod emf is `(1/2)B omega l^2`. |
| Saying inductor stores energy in electric field | Inductor stores energy in magnetic field. |

## Final Revision

Remember these points:

- Changing magnetic flux induces emf.
- Faraday's law gives magnitude; Lenz's law gives direction.
- Induced current opposes the cause of flux change.
- Motional emf comes from magnetic force on charges.
- Inductors oppose change in current.
- AC generator works by rotating coil in magnetic field.
