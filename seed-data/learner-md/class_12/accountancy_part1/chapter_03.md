# Chapter 3: Reconstitution of a Partnership Firm - Retirement or Death of a Partner
### NCERT Class 12 - Accountancy Part 1

## Chapter at a Glance

| Item | Details |
|---|---|
| Main idea | Retirement or death changes the partnership while the firm may continue with remaining partners. |
| Main accounting goal | Find the amount due to the outgoing partner or the deceased partner's executor. |
| Core ratios | New profit sharing ratio and gaining ratio. |
| Main adjustments | Goodwill, revaluation, reserves, accumulated losses, profit up to retirement/death, settlement of dues. |
| Main accounts | Revaluation account, retiring partner's capital account, loan account, executor's account. |
| Most important exam skill | Credit the outgoing partner for all entitlements and debit for all deductions. |

## Learning Outcomes

By the end of this chapter, you should be able to:

1. Explain accounting consequences of retirement of a partner.
2. Explain accounting consequences of death of a partner.
3. Calculate new profit sharing ratio of continuing partners.
4. Calculate gaining ratio.
5. Adjust goodwill without opening goodwill account unless required.
6. Write off existing goodwill, if any.
7. Prepare revaluation account.
8. Transfer accumulated profits and losses.
9. Calculate the outgoing partner's share of profit up to the date of retirement or death.
10. Calculate interest on capital, salary, commission, and drawings up to the date.
11. Prepare retiring partner's loan account.
12. Prepare deceased partner's executor's account.
13. Prepare the balance sheet of the continuing firm.

## Key Terms

| Term | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Retirement | Withdrawal of a partner from the firm while remaining partners continue. |
| Death of a partner | End of partner's relationship with firm due to death; amount due is payable to legal representative or executor. |
| New profit sharing ratio | Ratio in which remaining partners share future profits. |
| Gaining ratio | Ratio in which continuing partners gain the outgoing partner's share. |
| Retiring partner | Partner leaving the firm. |
| Deceased partner | Partner who has died. |
| Executor's account | Account opened for amount due to legal representative of deceased partner. |
| Profit and loss suspense account | Temporary account used to credit outgoing partner's share of profit up to date. |
| Hidden goodwill | Goodwill inferred from amount paid for a share when goodwill is not directly stated. |

## Quick Learning Map

| Area | Core Rule |
|---|---|
| Goodwill | Gaining partners compensate the outgoing partner. |
| Revaluation | All partners, including outgoing partner, share profit or loss in old ratio. |
| Reserves | All partners share old reserves in old ratio. |
| Accumulated losses | All partners bear old losses in old ratio. |
| Settlement | Amount due may be paid immediately, transferred to loan, or paid in instalments. |

# Unit 3A: Meaning of Retirement

Retirement occurs when a partner leaves the firm and remaining partners continue the business. It is a reconstitution, not necessarily closure of the firm.

Retirement may happen:

1. With consent of all partners.
2. According to agreement.
3. By notice, if partnership is at will.

## Accounting Matters at Retirement

At retirement, the firm must determine:

1. New profit sharing ratio.
2. Gaining ratio.
3. Goodwill compensation.
4. Revaluation of assets and liabilities.
5. Transfer of reserves and accumulated losses.
6. Retiring partner's share of current year profit or loss.
7. Interest on capital or drawings up to retirement date.
8. Final amount due and mode of payment.

# Unit 3B: Meaning of Death of a Partner

Death of a partner has accounting treatment similar to retirement. The difference is that the amount due is transferred to the deceased partner's executor or legal representative.

## Extra Matters in Case of Death

1. Date of death may fall during the accounting year.
2. Profit up to date of death must be estimated.
3. Interest on capital may be calculated up to date of death.
4. Drawings and interest on drawings up to date must be recorded.
5. Amount due is transferred to executor's account.

# Unit 3C: New Profit Sharing Ratio

After retirement or death, remaining partners may share future profits in:

1. Their old ratio.
2. A new ratio stated in the question.
3. A ratio after taking over the outgoing partner's share.

## If New Ratio Is Not Given

When the question says remaining partners continue in their old ratio, use the old ratio among remaining partners.

Example:

A, B, and C share 3:2:1. C retires. A and B continue in their old ratio 3:2.

# Unit 3D: Gaining Ratio

Gaining ratio shows the benefit gained by continuing partners due to retirement or death of a partner.

Gain = New share - Old share

Gaining ratio is used for goodwill adjustment.

## Example

A, B, and C share profits 3:2:1. C retires. A and B share future profits 2:1.

| Partner | Old share | New share | Gain |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| A | 3/6 | 2/3 = 4/6 | 1/6 |
| B | 2/6 | 1/3 = 2/6 | 0 |

A alone gains. Therefore A compensates C for goodwill.

# Unit 3E: Amount Due to Retiring or Deceased Partner

The outgoing partner is credited with:

1. Balance of capital account.
2. Balance of current account, if credit.
3. Share of goodwill.
4. Share of revaluation profit.
5. Share of reserves and accumulated profits.
6. Share of profit up to date of retirement or death.
7. Interest on capital up to the date, if allowed.
8. Salary or commission up to the date, if allowed.

The outgoing partner is debited with:

1. Drawings.
2. Interest on drawings.
3. Share of revaluation loss.
4. Share of accumulated losses.
5. Any debit balance in current account.
6. Any amount already paid.

## Control Table

| Credit Retiring Partner | Debit Retiring Partner |
|---|---|
| Capital balance | Drawings |
| Current account credit balance | Interest on drawings |
| Goodwill share | Revaluation loss |
| Revaluation profit | Accumulated losses |
| Reserves | Current account debit balance |
| Profit up to date | Cash paid |

# Unit 3F: Goodwill Treatment

At retirement or death, continuing partners gain future profit share. Therefore they compensate the outgoing partner for goodwill.

## Basic Entry Without Opening Goodwill Account

| Debit | Credit |
|---|---|
| Gaining Partners' Capital A/c | Retiring Partner's Capital A/c |

The debit is in gaining ratio.

## If Existing Goodwill Appears in Books

First write it off among all old partners in old ratio:

| Debit | Credit |
|---|---|
| All Partners' Capital A/c | Goodwill A/c |

Then adjust outgoing partner's share of revised goodwill through gaining partners.

## Example

A, B, and C share profits 3:2:1. C retires. Goodwill is valued at Rs. 60,000. A and B continue in 3:2.

C's share of goodwill = Rs. 60,000 x 1/6 = Rs. 10,000.

If A and B gain in 3:2:

| Partner | Debit |
|---|---:|
| A | Rs. 6,000 |
| B | Rs. 4,000 |

Credit C's capital account Rs. 10,000.

# Unit 3G: Hidden Goodwill

Hidden goodwill appears when the outgoing partner receives or is credited with an amount that implies goodwill, but goodwill value is not directly given.

## Pattern

If the retiring partner's capital after adjustments is less than the amount to be paid, the excess may represent his or her share of hidden goodwill.

Hidden goodwill of firm = Outgoing partner's goodwill share x Reciprocal of outgoing partner's old share

## Example

C retires from A, B, and C sharing 2:2:1. C's adjusted capital is Rs. 40,000, but the firm agrees to pay Rs. 52,000.

C's share of hidden goodwill = Rs. 12,000.

C's old share = 1/5.

Firm's hidden goodwill = Rs. 12,000 x 5 = Rs. 60,000.

# Unit 3H: Revaluation of Assets and Liabilities

Before settlement, assets and liabilities may be revalued. The revaluation profit or loss belongs to all partners including the outgoing partner because it relates to the period before retirement or death.

## Entries

| Transaction | Debit | Credit |
|---|---|---|
| Increase in asset | Asset A/c | Revaluation A/c |
| Decrease in asset | Revaluation A/c | Asset A/c |
| Increase in liability | Revaluation A/c | Liability A/c |
| Decrease in liability | Liability A/c | Revaluation A/c |
| Unrecorded asset | Asset A/c | Revaluation A/c |
| Unrecorded liability | Revaluation A/c | Liability A/c |
| Revaluation profit | Revaluation A/c | All Partners' Capital A/c |
| Revaluation loss | All Partners' Capital A/c | Revaluation A/c |

## Ratio Used

Use old profit sharing ratio because all partners were owners before reconstitution.

# Unit 3I: Accumulated Profits and Losses

Reserves and past profits are distributed to all partners in old ratio. Past losses are also borne by all partners in old ratio.

| Item | Debit | Credit |
|---|---|---|
| General reserve | General Reserve A/c | All Partners' Capital A/c |
| Profit and loss credit balance | Profit and Loss A/c | All Partners' Capital A/c |
| Profit and loss debit balance | All Partners' Capital A/c | Profit and Loss A/c |
| Advertisement suspense or fictitious asset | All Partners' Capital A/c | Relevant A/c |

# Unit 3J: Share of Profit up to Date of Retirement or Death

If retirement or death happens during the year, the outgoing partner may be entitled to profit up to that date.

## Bases of Calculation

| Basis Given | Method |
|---|---|
| Last year's profit | Last year's profit x Time fraction x Old profit share |
| Average profit | Average annual profit x Time fraction x Old profit share |
| Sales basis | Current period sales x Profit rate x Old profit share |

## Entry

| Debit | Credit |
|---|---|
| Profit and Loss Suspense A/c | Retiring or Deceased Partner's Capital A/c |

Later, profit and loss suspense account is transferred to continuing partners, usually in gaining ratio unless the question says otherwise.

Alternative entry:

| Debit | Credit |
|---|---|
| Gaining Partners' Capital A/c | Retiring or Deceased Partner's Capital A/c |

# Unit 3K: Settlement of Retiring Partner's Dues

The amount due may be settled in different ways.

## Full Payment

| Debit | Credit |
|---|---|
| Retiring Partner's Capital A/c | Bank A/c |

## Transfer to Loan

| Debit | Credit |
|---|---|
| Retiring Partner's Capital A/c | Retiring Partner's Loan A/c |

## Part Payment and Balance as Loan

| Debit | Credit |
|---|---|
| Retiring Partner's Capital A/c | Bank A/c and Retiring Partner's Loan A/c |

## Instalment Payment With Interest

Interest entry:

| Debit | Credit |
|---|---|
| Interest A/c | Retiring Partner's Loan A/c |

Payment entry:

| Debit | Credit |
|---|---|
| Retiring Partner's Loan A/c | Bank A/c |

# Unit 3L: Executor's Account in Case of Death

After calculating the amount due to the deceased partner, transfer it to executor's account.

| Debit | Credit |
|---|---|
| Deceased Partner's Capital A/c | Deceased Partner's Executor's A/c |

When paid:

| Debit | Credit |
|---|---|
| Deceased Partner's Executor's A/c | Bank A/c |

If the amount is paid in instalments, interest may be credited to executor's account as agreed.

# Unit 3M: Full Working Sequence

Use this order:

1. Read old ratio.
2. Find new ratio of continuing partners.
3. Find gaining ratio.
4. Write off existing goodwill, if any.
5. Adjust outgoing partner's share of goodwill through gaining partners.
6. Revalue assets and liabilities.
7. Transfer revaluation profit or loss to all partners in old ratio.
8. Transfer reserves and accumulated profits to all partners in old ratio.
9. Transfer accumulated losses to all partners in old ratio.
10. Calculate share of current year profit or loss.
11. Record interest on capital, drawings, salary, or commission up to date.
12. Find balance due.
13. Pay, transfer to loan, or transfer to executor's account.
14. Prepare balance sheet of continuing partners.

# Unit 3N: Worked Mini Case

A, B, and C share profits 3:2:1. C retires. A and B will share future profits 3:2. Goodwill is Rs. 60,000. Revaluation profit is Rs. 12,000. General reserve is Rs. 18,000.

## Goodwill

C's old share = 1/6.

C's share of goodwill = Rs. 60,000 x 1/6 = Rs. 10,000.

A and B gain in 3:2.

| Partner | Debit |
|---|---:|
| A | Rs. 6,000 |
| B | Rs. 4,000 |
| C | Credit Rs. 10,000 |

## Revaluation Profit

Shared among A, B, and C in old ratio 3:2:1.

| Partner | Share |
|---|---:|
| A | Rs. 6,000 |
| B | Rs. 4,000 |
| C | Rs. 2,000 |

## General Reserve

Shared in old ratio 3:2:1.

| Partner | Share |
|---|---:|
| A | Rs. 9,000 |
| B | Rs. 6,000 |
| C | Rs. 3,000 |

# Unit 3O: Common Mistakes and Corrections

| Mistake | Correct Approach |
|---|---|
| Using sacrificing ratio at retirement | Use gaining ratio. |
| Giving goodwill benefit to all partners | Only outgoing partner or sacrificing continuing partner is credited; gaining partners are debited. |
| Sharing revaluation in new ratio | Use old ratio. |
| Sharing reserves only among continuing partners | Include outgoing partner. |
| Forgetting profit up to date of death | Estimate and credit it if required. |
| Paying executor before all adjustments | First prepare deceased partner's capital account. |
| Treating retiring partner's loan as capital | Show loan separately after transfer. |

## Practice Set

1. A, B, and C share 5:3:2. C retires and A and B share future profits 3:2. Find gaining ratio.
2. P, Q, and R share 2:2:1. R retires. Goodwill of the firm is Rs. 75,000. P and Q gain equally. Pass goodwill adjustment.
3. X, Y, and Z share 4:3:2. Y retires. Revaluation loss is Rs. 18,000 and general reserve is Rs. 27,000. Show partner-wise effect.
4. A partner dies on 30 September. Last year's profit was Rs. 1,20,000. His profit share was 1/4. Calculate profit up to date assuming six months have passed.
5. A retiring partner is due Rs. 80,000. Rs. 30,000 is paid immediately and balance is transferred to loan. Pass the entry.

## Final Revision

Retirement and death problems are about settlement. The outgoing partner must receive the value of all rights up to the date of exit and bear all obligations up to that date. Goodwill follows gaining ratio, while revaluation and old reserves follow old ratio.
