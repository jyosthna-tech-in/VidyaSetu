# Chapter 5: Dissolution of Partnership Firm

## Chapter Identity

| Item | Details |
| --- | --- |
| Class | 12 |
| Subject | Accountancy Part 1 |
| Chapter | Dissolution of Partnership Firm |
| Core Skill | Closing the books of a dissolved firm through Realisation Account, Partners' Capital Accounts, and Bank/Cash Account |
| Main Idea | Dissolution ends the firm, so all assets are realised, liabilities are paid, partners' accounts are settled, and the books are closed. |

## 1. Big Picture

Dissolution of a partnership firm means the business of the firm comes to an end. The firm stops operating, sells or transfers its assets, pays outside liabilities, settles partner loans and capital balances, and closes its books.

This chapter is a closing-accounts chapter. The key account is Realisation Account. It is used to find profit or loss on the realisation of assets and payment of liabilities.

The main question is:

```text
After selling assets and paying liabilities, how much is payable to each partner or receivable from each partner?
```

## 2. Dissolution of Partnership vs Dissolution of Firm

These two terms are related but not the same.

| Basis | Dissolution of Partnership | Dissolution of Firm |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Meaning | Existing agreement among partners ends. | The firm itself closes down. |
| Business | Business may continue. | Business stops. |
| Books of account | Books need not be closed. | Books are closed. |
| Examples | Admission, retirement, death, change in ratio. | Complete closure of the firm. |
| Relationship | Relationship changes among some partners. | Relationship ends among all partners. |

Important rule:

```text
Dissolution of firm always involves dissolution of partnership, but dissolution of partnership does not always involve dissolution of firm.
```

## 3. Modes of Dissolution of Firm

A firm may be dissolved in the following ways:

| Mode | Explanation |
| --- | --- |
| By agreement | Partners agree to dissolve the firm. |
| Compulsory dissolution | Firm must dissolve due to legal impossibility, such as all partners or all except one becoming insolvent. |
| On happening of contingencies | Fixed term expires, venture is completed, a partner dies, or a partner becomes insolvent, subject to agreement. |
| By notice | In partnership at will, any partner may give written notice of dissolution. |
| By court | Court may order dissolution on grounds such as insanity, misconduct, persistent breach of agreement, transfer of interest, continuous losses, or just and equitable reasons. |

## 4. Settlement of Accounts

When the firm dissolves, accounts are settled in a legal and practical order.

### Order of Payment

Firm assets and partner contributions are used to pay:

1. Outside liabilities.
2. Partner loans or advances.
3. Partners' capital balances.
4. Any remaining surplus to partners in profit sharing ratio.

### Treatment of Losses

Losses, including capital deficiencies, are paid:

1. First out of profits.
2. Then out of partners' capitals.
3. Finally by partners individually in their profit sharing ratio, if required.

## 5. Accounts Prepared on Dissolution

The main accounts are:

| Account | Purpose |
| --- | --- |
| Realisation Account | Finds profit or loss on sale of assets and settlement of liabilities. |
| Partners' Capital Accounts | Settles partner capital balances after all adjustments. |
| Partners' Loan Accounts | Settles loans given by partners to the firm. |
| Bank/Cash Account | Records all actual receipts and payments. |

## 6. Realisation Account

### Meaning

Realisation Account is prepared to record the sale or takeover of assets and the payment or takeover of liabilities at dissolution.

### Nature

It is a temporary account. It is opened only to close the books of the dissolved firm.

### What Goes to Realisation Account

| Debit Side | Credit Side |
| --- | --- |
| Assets transferred at book value, except cash/bank and fictitious assets | Liabilities transferred at book value, except partner loans and capital |
| Payment of liabilities | Cash received from sale of assets |
| Realisation expenses | Assets taken over by partners |
| Liability taken over by partner, if recorded as settlement | Profit transferred to partners |
| Loss transferred to partners, if credit side exceeds debit side |  |

## 7. Items Not Transferred to Realisation Account

| Item | Treatment |
| --- | --- |
| Cash or bank balance | Stays in Cash/Bank Account. |
| Fictitious assets | Transferred to Partners' Capital Accounts in profit sharing ratio. |
| Debit balance of Profit and Loss Account | Transferred to Partners' Capital Accounts. |
| Partner loan | Paid separately through Bank Account. |
| Partners' capital accounts | Settled separately. |
| Reserves and accumulated profits | Transferred to Partners' Capital Accounts. |

## 8. Journal Entries on Dissolution

### Transfer of Assets to Realisation Account

All assets except cash/bank and fictitious assets are transferred:

```text
Realisation A/c Dr.
    To Assets A/cs
```

### Transfer of Outside Liabilities to Realisation Account

```text
Liabilities A/cs Dr.
    To Realisation A/c
```

Do not transfer partner loans to Realisation Account.

### Sale of Assets for Cash

```text
Bank A/c Dr.
    To Realisation A/c
```

### Asset Taken Over by Partner

```text
Partner's Capital A/c Dr.
    To Realisation A/c
```

The partner is debited because taking an asset reduces the amount payable to that partner.

### Payment of Liabilities

```text
Realisation A/c Dr.
    To Bank A/c
```

### Liability Taken Over by Partner

```text
Realisation A/c Dr.
    To Partner's Capital A/c
```

The partner is credited because taking over a liability increases the amount due to that partner.

### Creditor Accepts Asset in Full Settlement

If a creditor accepts an asset in full settlement, no separate cash entry is required for that settlement. The asset and liability have already been transferred to Realisation Account. If there is extra cash paid or received, record only the cash difference.

### Realisation Expenses Paid by Firm

```text
Realisation A/c Dr.
    To Bank A/c
```

### Realisation Expenses Paid by Partner on Behalf of Firm

```text
Realisation A/c Dr.
    To Partner's Capital A/c
```

### Partner Receives Fixed Remuneration for Dissolution Work

```text
Realisation A/c Dr.
    To Partner's Capital A/c
```

If the partner also pays expenses personally, no separate entry is made for actual expenses unless the question specifically requires reimbursement in addition to remuneration.

### Profit on Realisation

```text
Realisation A/c Dr.
    To Partners' Capital A/cs
```

Credit partners in profit sharing ratio.

### Loss on Realisation

```text
Partners' Capital A/cs Dr.
    To Realisation A/c
```

Debit partners in profit sharing ratio.

## 9. Treatment of Accumulated Profits and Losses

### Reserves and Accumulated Profits

Examples: general reserve, credit balance of Profit and Loss Account, contingency reserve not needed for liability.

Entry:

```text
Reserve/Profit and Loss A/c Dr.
    To Partners' Capital A/cs
```

### Accumulated Losses and Fictitious Assets

Examples: debit balance of Profit and Loss Account, advertisement suspense, preliminary expenses.

Entry:

```text
Partners' Capital A/cs Dr.
    To Accumulated Loss/Fictitious Asset A/c
```

Use the profit sharing ratio.

## 10. Treatment of Partner Loans

### Loan from Partner to Firm

Partner loan is not transferred to Realisation Account. It is paid after outside liabilities but before partner capital.

Entry:

```text
Partner's Loan A/c Dr.
    To Bank A/c
```

### Loan from Firm to Partner

If the firm has given a loan to a partner, it is transferred to that partner's Capital Account:

```text
Partner's Capital A/c Dr.
    To Partner's Loan A/c
```

## 11. Partners' Capital Accounts

Partners' Capital Accounts are used to settle final amounts after all dissolution adjustments.

### Credit Side Usually Includes

1. Opening capital balance.
2. Share of reserves and accumulated profits.
3. Liability taken over by partner.
4. Remuneration for realisation work.
5. Realisation expenses paid personally by partner.
6. Profit on realisation.

### Debit Side Usually Includes

1. Drawings or debit balances.
2. Fictitious assets and accumulated losses.
3. Asset taken over by partner.
4. Loss on realisation.
5. Final cash paid to partner.

### If Partner's Capital Shows Debit Balance

The partner must bring in cash:

```text
Bank A/c Dr.
    To Partner's Capital A/c
```

## 12. Bank Account

Bank Account records actual cash movement.

### Debit Side

1. Opening bank/cash balance.
2. Cash received from sale of assets.
3. Cash brought in by partners for deficiency.

### Credit Side

1. Realisation expenses paid.
2. Outside liabilities paid.
3. Partner loans paid.
4. Partners' final capital balances paid.

At the end, Bank Account should close.

## 13. Step-by-Step Dissolution Procedure

Use this sequence in numerical problems:

1. Transfer all assets except cash/bank and fictitious assets to Realisation Account.
2. Transfer all outside liabilities to Realisation Account.
3. Transfer accumulated profits/reserves to Partners' Capital Accounts.
4. Transfer accumulated losses/fictitious assets to Partners' Capital Accounts.
5. Record sale of assets.
6. Record assets taken over by partners.
7. Record payment of liabilities.
8. Record liabilities taken over by partners.
9. Record realisation expenses and remuneration.
10. Find profit or loss on Realisation Account.
11. Transfer realisation profit/loss to partners in profit sharing ratio.
12. Pay partner loans.
13. Settle Partners' Capital Accounts.
14. Close Bank Account.

## 14. Special Situations

### Asset Taken Over by Creditor

If a creditor accepts an asset in full settlement:

1. Asset is already debited to Realisation Account.
2. Liability is already credited to Realisation Account.
3. No further entry is needed if no cash difference exists.

If creditor accepts asset and also receives cash:

```text
Realisation A/c Dr.
    To Bank A/c
```

for the cash paid.

If creditor accepts asset and pays excess cash:

```text
Bank A/c Dr.
    To Realisation A/c
```

for the cash received.

### Unrecorded Asset

If an unrecorded asset is sold:

```text
Bank A/c Dr.
    To Realisation A/c
```

If taken over by partner:

```text
Partner's Capital A/c Dr.
    To Realisation A/c
```

### Unrecorded Liability

If an unrecorded liability is paid:

```text
Realisation A/c Dr.
    To Bank A/c
```

If taken over by partner:

```text
Realisation A/c Dr.
    To Partner's Capital A/c
```

### Provision Against Asset

Examples: provision for doubtful debts, provision for depreciation.

If both asset and provision appear, transfer:

```text
Realisation A/c Dr.
Provision A/c Dr.
    To Asset A/c
```

This effectively transfers the asset at net book value.

Some formats transfer gross asset and provision separately. Follow the method expected by your teacher or the format used in the problem, but keep the final effect correct.

## 15. Realisation Account Format

| Debit | Amount | Credit | Amount |
| --- | ---: | --- | ---: |
| Assets transferred | xxx | Liabilities transferred | xxx |
| Bank: liabilities paid | xxx | Bank: assets realised | xxx |
| Bank: expenses paid | xxx | Partner's Capital: asset taken over | xxx |
| Partner's Capital: liability taken over | xxx |  |  |
| Partners' Capital: profit | xxx | Partners' Capital: loss | xxx |

Note: profit appears on debit side because it is transferred out of Realisation Account. Loss appears on credit side because it is transferred out after debit side exceeds credit side.

## 16. Mini Worked Example

A and B share profits in 3:2. Their firm is dissolved.

| Item | Amount |
| --- | ---: |
| Assets except cash | Rs. 1,00,000 |
| Cash | Rs. 10,000 |
| Creditors | Rs. 30,000 |
| A's capital | Rs. 50,000 |
| B's capital | Rs. 30,000 |

Assets realised Rs. 90,000. Creditors were paid Rs. 28,000. Realisation expenses were Rs. 2,000.

### Realisation Account Logic

Debit:

```text
Assets transferred = Rs. 1,00,000
Creditors paid = Rs. 28,000
Expenses = Rs. 2,000
Total debit before loss/profit = Rs. 1,30,000
```

Credit:

```text
Creditors transferred = Rs. 30,000
Assets realised = Rs. 90,000
Total credit before loss/profit = Rs. 1,20,000
```

Loss on realisation:

```text
1,30,000 - 1,20,000 = Rs. 10,000
```

Loss shared in 3:2:

| Partner | Loss |
| --- | ---: |
| A | Rs. 6,000 |
| B | Rs. 4,000 |

### Bank Account Logic

Cash available:

```text
Opening cash 10,000 + Assets realised 90,000 = Rs. 1,00,000
```

Payments:

```text
Creditors 28,000 + Expenses 2,000 = Rs. 30,000
```

Amount left for partners:

```text
1,00,000 - 30,000 = Rs. 70,000
```

Capital after loss:

| Partner | Opening Capital | Less Loss | Final Payment |
| --- | ---: | ---: | ---: |
| A | Rs. 50,000 | Rs. 6,000 | Rs. 44,000 |
| B | Rs. 30,000 | Rs. 4,000 | Rs. 26,000 |

Total paid to partners:

```text
44,000 + 26,000 = Rs. 70,000
```

Bank closes correctly.

## 17. Common Mistakes and Corrections

| Mistake | Correct Thinking |
| --- | --- |
| Transferring cash/bank to Realisation Account | Cash/bank remains in Bank Account. |
| Transferring partner loan to Realisation Account | Partner loan is settled separately. |
| Forgetting fictitious assets | Transfer them to Partners' Capital Accounts in profit sharing ratio. |
| Sharing realisation profit/loss in capital ratio | Use profit sharing ratio. |
| Crediting partner when partner takes asset | Debit the partner because the partner receives value. |
| Debiting partner when partner takes liability | Credit the partner because the partner accepts an obligation. |
| Recording creditor-asset settlement twice | If no cash difference exists, no extra entry is needed after transfer entries. |
| Leaving Bank Account with a balance | Bank Account should close after final settlements. |

## 18. Exam Answer Frames

### Dissolution of Firm

Dissolution of a firm means the discontinuance of partnership business and the end of economic relations among all partners. On dissolution, the firm sells or transfers its assets, pays its liabilities, settles partner loans and capital accounts, and closes its books.

### Realisation Account

Realisation Account is prepared on dissolution of a partnership firm to record the realisation of assets and settlement of liabilities. Assets except cash/bank and fictitious assets are transferred to its debit side, while outside liabilities are transferred to its credit side. The profit or loss on realisation is transferred to partners' capital accounts in their profit sharing ratio.

### Difference Between Dissolution of Partnership and Firm

Dissolution of partnership means the existing agreement among partners ends, but the business may continue. Dissolution of firm means the business itself is closed and the books are finally settled. Therefore, dissolution of firm always includes dissolution of partnership, but dissolution of partnership may occur without dissolution of firm.

## 21. Final Learning Takeaway

Dissolution is a systematic closing process. Realisation Account measures the result of converting assets and liabilities into cash settlements. Partners' Capital Accounts show how that result affects each partner. Bank Account proves that all money received has been used to pay liabilities, partner loans, and final partner balances. If these three accounts connect properly, the firm's books close correctly.
