# Chapter 4: Water Resources
### NCERT Class 12 - Geography India

## Chapter at a Glance

Water is essential for drinking, agriculture, industry, energy, transport, and ecosystems. India has large water resources, but their distribution is uneven across space and time. Monsoon seasonality, regional rainfall variation, overuse of groundwater, pollution, and growing demand make water management a major challenge.

This chapter explains surface water, groundwater, river basins, water demand, irrigation, multipurpose river valley projects, watershed management, rainwater harvesting, and conservation.

## Learning Outcomes

By the end of this chapter, you should be able to:

- identify major water resources in India;
- explain uneven distribution of water;
- distinguish surface water and groundwater;
- understand river basin and watershed concepts;
- explain irrigation needs;
- describe multipurpose river valley projects;
- identify causes of water scarcity;
- explain water pollution;
- suggest conservation methods such as watershed management and rainwater harvesting.

## Key Terms

| Term | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Surface water | Water found in rivers, lakes, ponds, tanks, and reservoirs. |
| Groundwater | Water stored below Earth's surface in aquifers. |
| River basin | Area drained by a river and its tributaries. |
| Watershed | Smaller drainage unit dividing water flow. |
| Irrigation | Artificial supply of water to crops. |
| Multipurpose project | River project serving irrigation, power, flood control, navigation, and other uses. |
| Water scarcity | Shortage of usable water compared with demand. |
| Rainwater harvesting | Collection and storage of rainwater for use or recharge. |

# Unit 4A: India's Water Resources

India receives water mainly from:

- rainfall;
- rivers;
- lakes;
- tanks;
- reservoirs;
- groundwater;
- glaciers;
- wetlands.

Although total rainfall is large, much of it falls during a few monsoon months. This creates floods in some periods and shortages in others.

# Unit 4B: Surface Water

Surface water includes rivers, lakes, ponds, tanks, and reservoirs.

Major river systems include:

- Indus system;
- Ganga system;
- Brahmaputra system;
- peninsular rivers such as Godavari, Krishna, Kaveri, Narmada, and Mahanadi.

Himalayan rivers are often perennial because they receive water from rainfall and snowmelt. Many peninsular rivers are more seasonal and depend heavily on monsoon rainfall.

# Unit 4C: Groundwater

Groundwater is stored in aquifers and accessed through wells and tube wells.

It is important for:

- drinking water;
- irrigation;
- rural settlements;
- urban supply;
- industry.

Groundwater availability depends on rainfall, geology, soil, recharge, and extraction.

Problems:

- falling water table;
- salinity;
- fluoride and arsenic contamination in some regions;
- overuse in irrigated agriculture;
- urban depletion.

# Unit 4D: Uneven Distribution of Water

Water is uneven because:

- rainfall varies by region;
- monsoon is seasonal;
- river basins differ in size and flow;
- geology affects groundwater storage;
- population density varies;
- demand differs across agriculture, cities, and industry.

Northeast India and western coast receive high rainfall, while western Rajasthan and parts of Deccan are water-scarce.

# Unit 4E: Water Demand

Major water users:

- agriculture;
- domestic use;
- industry;
- power generation;
- livestock;
- urban services.

Agriculture is the largest water user in India because irrigation supports crops during dry periods and in low-rainfall regions.

# Unit 4F: Irrigation

Irrigation is needed because:

- rainfall is seasonal;
- rainfall is uncertain;
- some crops need more water;
- rabi crops grow after monsoon;
- drought-prone areas need support;
- multiple cropping requires assured water.

Major irrigation types:

- wells and tube wells;
- canals;
- tanks;
- sprinkler irrigation;
- drip irrigation.

# Unit 4G: Multipurpose River Valley Schemes

Multipurpose projects are designed to serve many objectives.

Benefits:

- irrigation;
- hydroelectricity;
- flood control;
- drinking water;
- navigation;
- fisheries;
- recreation;
- regional development.

Concerns:

- displacement of people;
- submergence of forests and farmland;
- siltation;
- ecological change;
- inter-state conflict;
- unequal benefit distribution.

# Unit 4H: Water Scarcity

Water scarcity can occur even where rainfall is not extremely low if demand is high or management is poor.

Causes:

- population growth;
- urbanisation;
- industrialisation;
- over-irrigation;
- groundwater depletion;
- pollution;
- inefficient use;
- climate variability;
- poor storage and distribution.

# Unit 4I: Water Pollution

Water pollution reduces usable water.

Major pollutants:

- untreated sewage;
- industrial effluents;
- agricultural chemicals;
- religious and cultural waste;
- solid waste;
- mining discharge.

Pollution affects health, ecosystems, fisheries, and drinking water supply.

# Unit 4J: Watershed Management

Watershed management focuses on conserving soil and water within a drainage unit.

Measures:

- afforestation;
- check dams;
- contour bunding;
- gully plugging;
- percolation tanks;
- controlled grazing;
- soil conservation;
- community participation.

It helps recharge groundwater, reduce erosion, and improve rural livelihoods.

# Unit 4K: Rainwater Harvesting

Rainwater harvesting means collecting and storing rainwater.

Methods:

- rooftop collection;
- recharge pits;
- tanks;
- farm ponds;
- check dams;
- traditional systems such as johads and tankas in dry regions.

Rainwater harvesting reduces runoff and supports groundwater recharge.

# Unit 4L: Water Conservation Strategy

Important steps:

- drip and sprinkler irrigation;
- crop choice suited to rainfall;
- wastewater treatment;
- reuse of treated water;
- rainwater harvesting;
- watershed management;
- pollution control;
- pricing and regulation where appropriate;
- public awareness;
- protection of wetlands.

# Unit 4M: Answer-Writing Framework

For water scarcity:

1. Define scarcity.
2. Explain uneven rainfall and seasonality.
3. Add demand factors such as agriculture, urbanisation, industry.
4. Mention groundwater depletion and pollution.
5. Suggest conservation measures.

For multipurpose projects:

1. Define multipurpose project.
2. List benefits.
3. Explain concerns.
4. Conclude with balanced and sustainable planning.

# Unit 4N: Common Mistakes and Corrections

| Mistake | Correction |
|---|---|
| Saying India has no water problem because rainfall is high | Rainfall is uneven and seasonal. |
| Ignoring groundwater | Groundwater is crucial for irrigation and drinking water. |
| Treating dams as only beneficial | They also create social and ecological concerns. |
| Confusing watershed and river basin | Watershed is a smaller drainage unit. |
| Suggesting only more supply | Demand management and conservation are essential. |
