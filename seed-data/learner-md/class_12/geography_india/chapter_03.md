# Chapter 3: Land Resources and Agriculture
### NCERT Class 12 - Geography India

## Chapter at a Glance

Land is a basic resource for agriculture, settlements, forests, industry, transport, and ecological balance. In India, pressure on land is high because population is large and land uses compete. Agriculture remains central to livelihood, food security, rural economy, and culture.

This chapter covers land-use categories, land degradation, cropping seasons, major crops, agricultural regions, farming problems, and agricultural development.

## Learning Outcomes

By the end of this chapter, you should be able to:

- explain land as a resource;
- identify major land-use categories in India;
- explain land-use change and land degradation;
- distinguish net sown area, forest area, fallow land, and cultivable waste;
- describe cropping seasons: kharif, rabi, and zaid;
- explain major food and non-food crops;
- understand cropping intensity and irrigation;
- explain Green Revolution and its impacts;
- identify major problems of Indian agriculture.

## Key Terms

| Term | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Land use | Purpose for which land is used. |
| Net sown area | Land cultivated at least once in a year. |
| Gross cropped area | Total cropped area counting multiple cropping. |
| Cropping intensity | Ratio of gross cropped area to net sown area, multiplied by 100. |
| Fallow land | Land left uncultivated for a period to restore fertility or due to constraints. |
| Cultivable waste | Land fit for cultivation but not cultivated for several years. |
| Land degradation | Decline in land quality and productivity. |
| Kharif | Monsoon crop season. |
| Rabi | Winter crop season. |
| Zaid | Short summer crop season. |

# Unit 3A: Land as a Resource

Land supports:

- agriculture;
- forests;
- pastures;
- settlements;
- industry;
- transport;
- water storage;
- biodiversity;
- cultural landscapes.

Because land is limited, its careful use is essential.

# Unit 3B: Land-Use Categories

Major land-use categories include:

- forests;
- land not available for cultivation;
- permanent pastures and grazing land;
- land under miscellaneous tree crops;
- cultivable waste;
- fallow land;
- net sown area.

Each category reflects physical conditions, population pressure, development, and policy.

# Unit 3C: Net Sown Area and Gross Cropped Area

Net sown area is land cultivated at least once in a year.

Gross cropped area counts total area sown, including land cultivated more than once.

Cropping intensity:

`Cropping intensity = (gross cropped area / net sown area) x 100`

High cropping intensity indicates multiple cropping and intensive land use.

# Unit 3D: Land Degradation

Land degradation means decline in land quality and productivity.

Causes:

- soil erosion;
- deforestation;
- overgrazing;
- mining;
- waterlogging;
- salinisation;
- industrial waste;
- excessive chemical use;
- shifting cultivation without adequate fallow.

Conservation requires afforestation, contour farming, controlled grazing, watershed management, and careful irrigation.

# Unit 3E: Importance of Agriculture in India

Agriculture is important because it:

- provides food;
- supports rural livelihoods;
- supplies raw materials to industries;
- influences national income;
- supports livestock;
- affects trade;
- shapes culture and settlement.

Despite growth of industry and services, agriculture remains crucial for employment and food security.

# Unit 3F: Cropping Seasons

## Kharif

Kharif crops are associated with monsoon rainfall.

Examples:

- rice;
- maize;
- cotton;
- jute;
- groundnut;
- soybean;
- pulses.

## Rabi

Rabi crops grow in cooler season and often need irrigation.

Examples:

- wheat;
- gram;
- mustard;
- barley;
- peas.

## Zaid

Zaid is a short summer season between rabi and kharif.

Examples:

- watermelon;
- cucumber;
- vegetables;
- fodder crops.

# Unit 3G: Major Food Crops

## Rice

Rice needs high temperature, high humidity, and sufficient water. It is important in eastern, southern, and coastal India and irrigated northern plains.

## Wheat

Wheat grows in cool season with moderate rainfall or irrigation. Major regions include Punjab, Haryana, western Uttar Pradesh, and parts of Madhya Pradesh and Rajasthan.

## Millets

Millets such as jowar, bajra, and ragi are important in dry regions. They are hardy, nutritious, and suited to low rainfall areas.

## Pulses

Pulses are protein-rich and help restore soil fertility through nitrogen fixation.

# Unit 3H: Major Non-Food Crops

## Cotton

Cotton grows well in black soil and warm climate. It supports textile industries.

## Jute

Jute needs hot and humid climate and alluvial soil. It is important in eastern India, especially West Bengal and nearby regions.

## Sugarcane

Sugarcane needs hot climate, water, and fertile soil. It supports sugar industry.

## Tea and Coffee

Tea grows in humid hill slopes with well-distributed rainfall. Coffee is grown in parts of southern India.

## Oilseeds

Groundnut, mustard, soybean, and sesame are important oilseeds.

# Unit 3I: Green Revolution

Green Revolution involved high-yielding varieties, irrigation, fertilisers, pesticides, and mechanisation, especially for wheat and rice.

Positive impacts:

- increased foodgrain production;
- improved food security;
- higher farm incomes in some regions;
- growth of market-oriented agriculture.

Concerns:

- regional inequality;
- soil and water stress;
- chemical dependence;
- focus on limited crops;
- groundwater depletion.

# Unit 3J: Problems of Indian Agriculture

Major problems:

- small and fragmented holdings;
- dependence on monsoon;
- low irrigation in some regions;
- soil degradation;
- limited mechanisation for small farmers;
- lack of storage;
- price fluctuations;
- indebtedness;
- low productivity in some areas;
- climate risk.

# Unit 3K: Sustainable Agriculture

Sustainable agriculture aims to maintain production while protecting resources.

Methods:

- crop rotation;
- organic manure;
- efficient irrigation;
- watershed development;
- integrated pest management;
- soil testing;
- drought-resistant crops;
- agroforestry.

4. Suggest sustainable solutions.

# Unit 3M: Common Mistakes and Corrections

| Mistake | Correction |
|---|---|
| Confusing net sown area and gross cropped area | Gross cropped area counts multiple cropping. |
| Saying Green Revolution helped all regions equally | Benefits were regionally uneven. |
| Treating land as only agricultural | Land has many uses. |
| Ignoring sustainability | Agriculture depends on soil, water, and ecology. |
| Calling millets inferior | Millets are climate-resilient and nutritious. |
