# Chapter 5: Mineral and Energy Resources
### NCERT Class 12 - Geography India

## Chapter at a Glance

Mineral and energy resources form the base of industrial development. Minerals provide raw materials, while energy powers agriculture, industry, transport, households, and services. India has varied mineral resources, but they are unevenly distributed and many are exhaustible.

This chapter explains types of minerals, distribution patterns, major mineral belts, conventional and non-conventional energy resources, and the need for conservation.

## Learning Outcomes

By the end of this chapter, you should be able to:

- classify minerals into metallic, non-metallic, and energy minerals;
- explain mineral distribution in India;
- identify major mineral belts;
- describe coal, petroleum, natural gas, and electricity resources;
- distinguish conventional and non-conventional energy;
- explain solar, wind, biomass, tidal, and geothermal energy;
- understand environmental impacts of mining and energy use;
- suggest mineral and energy conservation methods.

## Key Terms

| Term | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Mineral | Naturally occurring substance with definite chemical and physical properties. |
| Ore | Mineral from which metal can be extracted profitably. |
| Metallic mineral | Mineral containing metal. |
| Non-metallic mineral | Mineral not containing metal, such as mica or limestone. |
| Energy resource | Resource used to produce power or fuel. |
| Conventional energy | Traditional energy such as coal, petroleum, natural gas, and hydel power. |
| Non-conventional energy | Renewable or alternative energy such as solar and wind. |
| Mineral belt | Region with concentration of mineral deposits. |

# Unit 5A: Importance of Minerals

Minerals support:

- industries;
- construction;
- transport;
- defence;
- energy production;
- machines;
- fertilisers;
- communication technology.

Minerals are unevenly distributed because they depend on geological structure.

# Unit 5B: Classification of Minerals

## Metallic Minerals

Metallic minerals contain metals.

Types:

- ferrous: iron ore, manganese;
- non-ferrous: copper, bauxite, lead, zinc.

## Non-Metallic Minerals

Examples:

- mica;
- limestone;
- gypsum;
- dolomite;
- salt.

## Energy Minerals

Examples:

- coal;
- petroleum;
- natural gas;
- uranium;
- thorium.

# Unit 5C: Major Mineral Belts of India

## North-Eastern Plateau Region

Includes parts of Jharkhand, Odisha, Chhattisgarh, and West Bengal. Rich in iron ore, coal, manganese, bauxite, and mica.

## South-Western Plateau Region

Includes Karnataka, Goa, Tamil Nadu, and Kerala. Important for iron ore, manganese, bauxite, and some non-metallic minerals.

## North-Western Region

Includes Rajasthan and Gujarat. Known for non-ferrous minerals, building stones, gypsum, salt, and petroleum in some areas.

## Himalayan Region

Minerals are present but extraction is difficult due to terrain and ecological sensitivity.

# Unit 5D: Iron Ore

Iron ore is the backbone of iron and steel industry.

Important types:

- hematite;
- magnetite.

Major regions include parts of Odisha, Jharkhand, Chhattisgarh, Karnataka, and Goa.

# Unit 5E: Manganese, Bauxite, and Mica

## Manganese

Used in steel-making and other industries. Found in Odisha, Karnataka, Maharashtra, Madhya Pradesh, and other regions.

## Bauxite

Ore of aluminium. Found in plateau and hill regions such as Odisha, Gujarat, Jharkhand, Maharashtra, and Chhattisgarh.

## Mica

Used in electrical and electronic industries. Important belts occur in Jharkhand, Bihar, Rajasthan, and Andhra Pradesh regions.

# Unit 5F: Coal

Coal is a major conventional energy resource in India.

Types by carbon content:

- anthracite;
- bituminous;
- lignite;
- peat.

Gondwana coalfields are the most important and occur mainly in Damodar, Mahanadi, Godavari, and Son river valleys.

Coal supports thermal power and industries, but causes air pollution and greenhouse gas emissions.

# Unit 5G: Petroleum and Natural Gas

Petroleum is used for transport fuels, petrochemicals, fertilisers, and energy.

Important regions include:

- Mumbai High;
- Gujarat fields;
- Assam region;
- eastern offshore and other sedimentary basins.

Natural gas is cleaner than coal and oil but still a fossil fuel. It is used in power, fertilisers, industry, and domestic supply.

# Unit 5H: Electricity

Electricity is produced from:

- thermal power;
- hydel power;
- nuclear power;
- solar power;
- wind power;
- biomass and other renewable forms.

Thermal power uses fossil fuels. Hydel power uses flowing water. Nuclear power uses atomic minerals.

# Unit 5I: Non-Conventional Energy Resources

## Solar Energy

India has high solar potential, especially in Rajasthan, Gujarat, Ladakh, and dry regions.

Uses:

- electricity generation;
- water heating;
- solar pumps;
- rooftop systems.

## Wind Energy

Wind power is important in coastal and plateau regions with steady winds.

Major states include Tamil Nadu, Gujarat, Maharashtra, Karnataka, Rajasthan, and Andhra Pradesh.

## Biomass Energy

Biomass uses organic matter such as crop residue, animal waste, and plant material.

Biogas is useful in rural areas for cooking and lighting.

## Tidal and Geothermal Energy

Tidal energy uses sea tides. Geothermal energy uses Earth's internal heat. Both have limited but location-specific potential.

# Unit 5J: Environmental Concerns

Mining and energy use can cause:

- deforestation;
- land degradation;
- displacement;
- air pollution;
- water pollution;
- mine waste;
- greenhouse gas emissions;
- health problems.

Responsible mining and cleaner energy are essential.

# Unit 5K: Conservation of Minerals and Energy

Minerals are finite and need careful use.

Measures:

- recycling metals;
- reducing wastage;
- efficient mining;
- technological improvement;
- substitution where possible;
- using renewable energy;
- energy-efficient appliances;
- public transport;
- strict environmental regulation.

# Unit 5L: Answer-Writing Framework

4. Give examples of minerals in each belt.
5. Add need for conservation.

For energy resources:

1. Classify conventional and non-conventional.
2. Explain fossil fuels and hydel power.
3. Explain renewable resources.
4. Mention environmental concerns.
5. Conclude with sustainable energy mix.

# Unit 5M: Common Mistakes and Corrections

| Mistake | Correction |
|---|---|
| Treating minerals as renewable | Most minerals are exhaustible. |
| Confusing mineral and ore | Ore is economically extractable mineral. |
| Saying all energy resources are minerals | Solar and wind are energy resources but not minerals. |
| Ignoring environmental costs | Mining and fossil fuels create serious impacts. |
| Treating non-conventional energy as optional only | It is essential for future energy security. |
