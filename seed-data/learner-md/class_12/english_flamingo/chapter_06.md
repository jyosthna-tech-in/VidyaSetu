# Chapter 6: Poets and Pancakes
### NCERT Class 12 - English Flamingo

## Chapter at a Glance

Asokamitran's "Poets and Pancakes" is a humorous and reflective account of Gemini Studios, a famous film studio in Madras. The chapter presents the studio world through sketches of makeup artists, office hierarchy, literary visitors, and cultural confusion. It is rich in satire, irony, and observational humour.

The chapter is not a conventional plot-driven story. It is a memoir-like prose piece that captures a workplace, its people, its absurdities, and the unexpected intersection of cinema, literature, and politics.

By the end of this chapter, you should be able to:

# Unit 6A: Gemini Studios

Gemini Studios was a major film production centre. The chapter presents it as a busy, hierarchical, and sometimes absurd workplace.

The studio brings together:

- actors;
- makeup artists;
- writers;
- assistants;
- office workers;
- technicians;
- visitors from literary and political worlds.

The narrator observes this world with humour rather than anger.

# Unit 6B: Pancake Makeup

"Pancake" refers to a brand of makeup used heavily in films. The makeup department transforms actors' appearances under studio lights.

The title pairs "Poets" with "Pancakes" to create contrast:

- poetry suggests high art and literary refinement;
- pancake suggests artificial film makeup and studio routine.

This contrast captures the chapter's comic world where art, glamour, labour, and confusion mix.

# Unit 6C: Makeup Department Hierarchy

The makeup department has strict hierarchy. Different workers handle different types of actors depending on status.

This hierarchy mirrors the larger studio system where rank, access, and recognition matter greatly.

The humour comes from treating makeup work with almost bureaucratic seriousness.

# Unit 6D: The Office Boy

The office boy is not literally a child. He is a grown man stuck in a low position. He feels frustrated because he believes he deserves creative recognition.

He blames Subbu for his lack of progress.

His character shows:

- workplace jealousy;
- frustrated ambition;
- gap between self-image and reality;
- comic bitterness.

# Unit 6E: Subbu

Subbu is a central figure in the studio. He is close to the boss and seems useful in every situation. If a problem arises, he can suggest solutions quickly.

## Why Subbu Is Successful

He is:

- loyal to the boss;
- creative;
- flexible;
- socially skilled;
- able to flatter without appearing crude;
- useful in practical situations.

The office boy dislikes him because Subbu's success seems effortless and blocks others' ambitions.

# Unit 6F: Subbu as Artist and Human Being

Subbu is not only a studio assistant. He writes, acts, and helps shape scenes. He also has a generous side, supporting many people around him.

The narrator's portrait is balanced. Subbu is comic, privileged, and dependent on patronage, but he is also talented and kind.

# Unit 6G: The Legal Adviser

The legal adviser is ironically described because his advice once harms an actress's career rather than protecting her.

This episode satirises:

- professional labels;
- misuse of authority;
- workplace power;
- lack of sensitivity toward artists.

The legal adviser's role shows that titles do not guarantee wisdom.

# Unit 6H: Anti-Communist Atmosphere

The studio world reflects political anxieties of the time. Communism is viewed with suspicion by many in the studio.

This background matters because the visit of an English poet is misunderstood through political assumptions. People are unsure who he is and why he has come.

# Unit 6I: Visit of Stephen Spender

An English poet visits Gemini Studios and gives a speech. The audience does not understand much of what he says. The visit becomes comic because there is a mismatch between speaker, audience, language, and expectations.

Years later, the narrator recognises the visitor as Stephen Spender, a known poet and editor.

## Why the Visit Is Comic

It involves:

- cultural distance;
- language barrier;
- lack of context;
- exaggerated seriousness;
- confusion about literary identity.

# Unit 6J: Major Themes

## Art and Artificiality

The film studio creates illusion. Makeup, hierarchy, and performance all show the artificial side of art production.

## Workplace Hierarchy

The chapter satirises how status operates in institutions.

## Frustrated Ambition

The office boy represents people who feel trapped below their imagined worth.

## Literary Pretension and Cultural Gap

The English poet's visit shows how literary events can become empty when audience and speaker do not connect.

## Humour in Observation

The narrator does not preach. He observes small absurdities and lets humour reveal truth.

# Unit 6K: Narrative Style

Asokamitran's style is:

- conversational;
- ironic;
- understated;
- full of character sketches;
- based on memory;
- humorous without loud exaggeration.

The chapter's structure is episodic. It moves from makeup to office politics to literary visitors, creating a collage of studio life.
