## Big Ideas

This chapter explores two kinds of communication.

The prose **Carrier of Words** shows how words physically travel through letters, money orders, and human effort. Khetaram carries mail across desert distances, but he also carries trust, news, comfort, and social connection.

The poem **Words** asks whether words alone can truly satisfy the heart. It warns that many words can be empty, while a few sincere words can bring deeper joy.

Together, the texts teach that communication is not only about sending messages. It is about sincerity, responsibility, empathy, and human trust.

## Who Is Khetaram?

Khetaram is the sole postman of Somarad Branch Post Office. For more than fifteen years, he has served remote hamlets in the Thar desert of Rajasthan. His shoulder has slumped from years of carrying a mailbag.

He works in harsh conditions:

- blazing heat;
- sand dunes;
- swirling sandstorms;
- long walking distances;
- remote settlements;
- lack of roads and phone lines.

Yet he remains a trusted figure for villagers.

## Money Orders and Social Trust

In arid regions, survival often depends on money sent by relatives. Money orders are therefore not just financial transactions; they are lifelines.

Villagers trust the post office because:

- it reaches remote places;
- it handles small sums reliably;
- it connects migrant workers with families;
- it keeps formal financial records;
- it is represented by familiar local workers like Khetaram.

## Long Answer Question Bank

### 1. Why does the Postmaster-General call the role of GDS invaluable?

The role is invaluable because GDS workers reach interior regions that may be inaccessible by regular transport. They connect remote people with families, financial services, news, and official systems. In places like deserts, islands, frozen areas, and riverine regions, they are often the only practical link between the state and citizens.

### 2. How was India Post different from the British postal system?

The British postal system mainly carried official mail between administrative centres. After independence, India Post aimed to include the entire population within the postal network. Its purpose became public service and national connection, not only administration.

### 3. Give two examples showing people trusted Gramin Dak Sewaks.

First, villagers allowed Khetaram to stand at their threshold, read letters aloud, and write replies. This shows personal trust. Second, people depended on the post office for money orders from relatives, showing financial trust in the postal system.

### 4. Why was Khetaram grateful to continue as GDS after 60?

He was grateful because the job gave him income, purpose, and social respect. In a region where farming was unreliable, the job helped him support his family even in old age.

### 5. Why does the author pay tribute to people like Khetaram?

The author pays tribute because people like Khetaram serve society silently under extreme difficulty. They connect isolated communities, carry emotional news, and sustain trust in public systems. Their work is physically hard and socially meaningful.

## Long Answer Questions

### 1. What comparison does the poet draw between words and empty air?

The poet suggests that many words disappear without leaving comfort or meaning, just as air cannot be held. Empty words may be spoken, but they do not remain in the heart.

### 2. Why are meaningful words more precious than many words?

Meaningful words are sincere and emotionally true. Even a few such words can comfort a lonely person, express love, or bring courage. Many empty words may sound impressive but fail to touch the heart.

### 3. Does the poem present contrasting ideas about words?

Yes. The poem contrasts empty words with sincere words. Empty words are compared to birds that depart, weeds, and plants without fruit. Sincere words are shown as dear, cheering, and capable of deep joy.

### 4. How does loneliness hover over the poem?

The poem refers to the heart as a pilgrim and mentions a lonely home needing cheer. These images suggest emotional isolation. The poem asks whether words can truly comfort a lonely heart.

### 5. How does the poet convey the superficial nature of words?

The poet compares empty words to gaudy plants that blossom but bear no fruit. This means they may look attractive but produce no real benefit. To address this, people should speak fewer words, but make them truthful, kind, and heartfelt.

## Mini Model Essay

### Words with a Heart

Mahatma Gandhi said, "It is better in prayer to have a heart without words than words without a heart." This quotation appeals to me because it reminds us that sincerity matters more than impressive language. The purpose of this essay is to show why words need feeling behind them.

First, words without emotion can sound empty. A person may apologise beautifully but not mean it. Such an apology does not heal the hurt. However, even a simple "I am sorry" can bring peace if it is spoken sincerely.

Second, silence can sometimes communicate more than speech. Sitting beside a grieving person, helping a friend, or listening patiently can express care without many words. In such moments, the heart speaks through presence.

Some people may argue that words are always necessary because thoughts must be expressed. This is partly true. However, words become meaningful only when they carry honesty. Without sincerity, they may impress the ear but fail to touch the heart.

Therefore, Gandhi's quotation teaches us to value truth in communication. Words should not be used merely to fill silence. They should carry kindness, respect, and feeling.
