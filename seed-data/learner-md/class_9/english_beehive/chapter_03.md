## Short Summary

The chapter explains the origin, history, and cultural importance of traditional Indian hand fans called pankhas. The word is connected with "pankh", meaning feather. Evidence of small fans appears in ancient art such as Ajanta wall paintings. In the past, pankhas were used in temples, royal courts, homes, and trade.

Different regions developed their own types of pankhas using local materials and designs. Rajasthan made applique, zardozi, temple, and painted hand fans. Gujarat developed mirror work, bead, and leather hand fans. Bengal used sola and palm leaves. Uttar Pradesh, Odisha, Bihar, and tribal communities also created distinctive forms.

Modern technology has reduced the everyday use of hand fans, and they are now often decorative craft items. Yet the craft still provides livelihood to artisans. The writer argues that workshops, exhibitions, appreciation, innovation, and cultural celebration can help preserve this craft.

---

## Why the Title "Winds of Change" Is Effective

The title works at two levels.

### Literal Meaning

Pankhas create wind or air movement.

### Symbolic Meaning

"Change" refers to:

- changing technology;
- changing use of hand fans;
- changing craft markets;
- changing consumer habits;
- changing role from utility to decoration;
- new ways to preserve old crafts.

So the title captures both the object and its cultural journey.

## Craft, Region, and Identity

The structure and design of pankhas show cultural identity because each region uses:

- local materials;
- local design patterns;
- local rituals;
- inherited skills;
- colours and motifs familiar to that region.

### Examples

Rajasthan's zardozi hand fan reflects ornamental textile traditions.

Gujarat's mirror work fans reflect local embroidery and bead craft.

Bengal's palm leaf and sola fans reflect local plant materials and household use.

Bihar's bamboo fans reflect sturdy practical craft using locally available bamboo.

---

## Changing Role of Pankhas

| Earlier Role | Modern Role |
|---|---|
| Daily cooling device. | Decorative craft item. |
| Used in homes, temples, and courts. | Displayed in homes and exhibitions. |
| Personal or ritual object. | Commercial product and souvenir. |
| Local handmade utility. | Heritage object representing craft identity. |

Technology has reduced the need for hand fans in everyday life, but craft awareness has created new markets.

---

## Preservation Through Innovation

Traditional crafts can survive when preservation and innovation work together.

| Preservation | Innovation |
|---|---|
| Protect old techniques. | Create new designs. |
| Respect cultural meaning. | Make products useful for modern buyers. |
| Support artisans. | Use exhibitions, workshops, digital markets. |
| Teach younger generations. | Adapt shape, colour, and display purpose. |

Balance is important. If a craft never changes, it may lose market relevance. If it changes too much, it may lose cultural identity.

## Short Answer Questions: Model Points

### 1. How does the title capture the chapter?

The title refers to the wind produced by fans and the cultural changes affecting pankhas. It captures both the physical object and its changing role in modern India.

### 2. Give two examples showing cultural identity.

Rajasthan's zardozi fans reflect ornate textile work, while Gujarat's mirror work fans reflect local embroidery traditions. Similarly, Bengal's palm leaf fans reflect local natural materials and household practices.

### 3. How can preservation and innovation help pankhas survive?

Preservation protects traditional techniques and meanings, while innovation creates modern designs and markets. Together they help the craft remain culturally authentic and economically useful.

### 4. How can workshops preserve the craft?

Workshops spread awareness, teach techniques, create interest among young learners, connect artisans with buyers, and make the craft visible beyond local communities.

### 5. How can celebrating pankhas benefit artisans?

Celebration increases respect, demand, exhibition opportunities, income, and cultural pride. It helps artisans see their work as valued heritage.

### 6. What does decorative use show about cultural change?

It shows that the pankha has moved from being mainly a practical cooling tool to becoming a symbol of heritage, craft, and decoration.

# Unit 3C: Grammar - Present Perfect Tense

## What Is Present Perfect?

The present perfect tense connects a past action with present relevance.

### Structure

| Subject | Helping Verb | Past Participle |
|---|---|---|
| I / you / we / they | have | worked |
| he / she / it | has | worked |

Examples:

- Pankhas have become traditional craft items.
- Many workers have preserved this craft.
- The craft has transformed into a business.

---

## Uses of Present Perfect

| Use | Example |
|---|---|
| Past action with present result | The fan has become a decorative item. |
| Recently completed action | The artisan has finished the design. |
| Life experience | I have seen a mirror work hand fan. |
| Continuing relevance | Craftspeople have worked for generations. |

---

## Paragraph Completion: Model Answers

Puppets **have long fascinated** audiences worldwide. Puppeteers **have created** intricate characters and captivating stories with their skillful artistry. They **have mastered** the delicate movements that bring these lifeless figures to life, entertaining both children and adults. Over the years, puppetry **has evolved**, using modern technology while preserving traditional techniques. Many puppeteers **have passed** down their craft through generations, ensuring its continuity. They **have performed** in theatres, on television, and at festivals, conveying important cultural narratives.

---

## Common Errors

| Error | Correction | Reason |
|---|---|---|
| She have made a fan. | She has made a fan. | Use "has" with she/he/it. |
| They has worked hard. | They have worked hard. | Use "have" with they/we/you/I. |
| He has make a pankha. | He has made a pankha. | Use past participle after has/have. |
| I have saw this craft. | I have seen this craft. | Past participle of see is seen. |

# Unit 3D: Speaking and Writing

## Speaking Activity: Speak as a Fan

Choose one fan:

- hand fan;
- ceiling fan;
- table fan;
- handheld electric fan.

### Speaking Structure

1. Introduce yourself.
2. Describe your shape, material, and use.
3. Explain what makes you useful.
4. State one strength.
5. State one weakness.
6. Explain what makes you unique.

### Model Speech: Hand Fan

```text
I am a traditional hand fan. I may be made of palm leaf, bamboo, cloth, beads, leather, or decorated paper. I do not need electricity, so I can be used anywhere. My strength is that I am light, portable, and connected with regional craft traditions. My weakness is that I require human effort and may not cool a large room. What makes me unique is that I am not only useful but also artistic. I carry the identity of the region where I am made.
```

---

## Writing Task: Factual Description of an Artefact

A factual description gives information, not personal opinion.

### What to Include

| Point | What to Write |
|---|---|
| Name | What is the object called? |
| Appearance | Shape, size, colour, design. |
| Material | What is it made of? |
| Region | Where does it come from? |
| Traditional use | How was it used earlier? |
| Making process | Main steps. |
| Present use | How is it used today? |
| Safety / care | How to handle it properly. |

### Model Factual Description

```text
A palm leaf hand fan is a traditional cooling object made from dried and shaped palm leaves. It is usually flat, light, and easy to hold. Some fans are plain, while others are coloured or decorated with simple patterns. The leaves are cut, dried, pressed, and attached to a handle. Traditionally, such fans were used in homes, temples, and outdoor spaces during hot weather. Today, they are also used as decorative craft items and eco-friendly alternatives to plastic products. They should be kept dry and handled gently to prevent tearing.
```

## Supplementary Reading: The Last Leaf

O. Henry's "The Last Leaf" is a story about hope, friendship, art, and sacrifice. Johnsy, a young artist, becomes seriously ill and believes she will die when the last ivy leaf outside her window falls. Her friend Sue tries to keep her hopeful. ## Short Summary

The poem compares gardening to painting. The earth becomes a palette, seeds become brushstrokes, flowers become colours, and each garden plot becomes a canvas. The gardener is like an artist who works with soil, seeds, seasons, and care. The poem suggests that nature and human effort come together to create living art.

---

## Stanza-Wise Explanation

### Stanza 1

The earth is imagined as a rich palette. Gardeners plant seeds like painters applying brushstrokes. The seeds wait for spring, when colour and growth will appear.

### Stanza 2

Flowers bloom in the morning light, creating a colourful scene. Green, red, and blue shades make the garden look like an artwork created by nature.

### Stanza 3

Each garden plot is compared to a wide canvas. The work of gardeners brings together art and life. A garden becomes a living painting.

## Short Answer Questions: Model Points

### 1. How does "Brushstrokes of seeds" enhance gardening as art?

It makes planting seem like painting. Each seed is like a stroke that will later become part of a colourful living picture.

### 2. What is the relationship between nature and creativity?

The poem suggests that creativity is not limited to paint and canvas. Gardening is also creative because it shapes natural life into beauty.

### 3. Does imagery create a vivid picture?

Yes. The references to colours, blossoms, morning light, and garden plots help readers imagine a bright, living garden.

### 4. Would yellow improve the imagery?

Yes. Yellow would add brightness and warmth and would expand the range of colours in the garden picture.

### 5. What does "Gardens become paintings still" suggest?

It suggests that a garden, though alive and changing, can appear like a timeless work of art when seen in a moment of beauty.

### 6. Justify the title "Canvas of Soil".

The title is suitable because the poem imagines soil as the surface on which gardeners create living art through seeds, colours, and growth.

## Speaking: Flower Garden or Vegetable Garden?

### Flower Garden Advantages

- Adds beauty and colour.
- Attracts butterflies and bees.
- Creates a peaceful atmosphere.
- Improves the look of a home.

### Vegetable Garden Advantages

- Provides fresh vegetables.
- Saves money.
- Teaches responsibility.
- Encourages healthy eating.

### Model Preference

## Study Strategy for Learners

For the prose:

1. Remember the regional examples of pankhas.
2. Understand the shift from practical object to decorative craft.
3. Connect every preservation answer to artisans and livelihood.
4. Separate fact from opinion in extract questions.

For the poem:

1. Learn the central metaphor: garden as painting.

4. Explain the deeper allegorical meaning: growth, harmony, and life.

For grammar:

2. Use it when a past action matters now.
3. Do not use simple past when the focus is present result.
