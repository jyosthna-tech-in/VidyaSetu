## Big Ideas

This chapter is built around two connected questions:

1. How should we care for people who are older than us?
2. How do memories keep loved ones present even when they are absent, distant, or no longer alive?

The prose story answers the first question through humour. Ravi's mother wants to protect Grandpa, but she sometimes forgets that protection without respect can feel like imprisonment. Ravi loves Grandpa and wants to help him, but he learns that care is not as simple as following someone secretly. Grandpa appears forgetful, yet he is observant, independent, generous, witty, and emotionally intelligent.

The poem answers the second question quietly. The speaker cannot remember his mother clearly, but fragments of song, fragrance, flowers, temple atmosphere, sky, and gaze bring her presence back. Memory is shown not as a complete picture, but as a collection of sensations.

Together, the prose and poem teach that family love requires attention, patience, respect, and sensitivity.

# Unit 4A: Before Reading

## Opening Theme: Caring for the Elderly

The chapter begins by asking learners to think about an elderly person at home or in the neighbourhood.

The organiser in the raw text asks four major questions:

| Question | Learner-Friendly Points |
|---|---|
| Why should we care for the elderly? | They have cared for families and society; they may need physical, emotional, and medical support; they deserve respect and companionship. |
| What problems do they face? | Loneliness, weak health, memory issues, difficulty walking, poor eyesight or hearing, dependence on others, and feeling ignored. |
| What causes these problems? | Age-related illness, loss of spouse or friends, lack of mobility, busy family schedules, unsafe surroundings, and social neglect. |
| How can we care for them? | Speak respectfully, listen patiently, help with medicines, accompany them outdoors, include them in decisions, and protect without humiliating them. |

## Important Balance

Caring for elderly people does not mean treating them like children.

Good care includes:

- safety;
- affection;
- patience;
- medical attention;
- emotional support;
- respect for independence;
- respect for memory and experience;
- willingness to listen.

Poor care may look helpful but can hurt dignity if it:

- ignores their choices;
- speaks to them loudly without need;
- makes all decisions for them;
- assumes they cannot think clearly;
- turns the home into a prison;
- forgets their past skills and identity.

## Why the Title "Vitamin-M" Is Important

The title looks playful, but it carries a serious idea.

In the story, Ravi's mother wishes there were a memory vitamin for old people. She thinks Grandpa is the one who needs memory help. By the end, the joke turns around: Grandpa remembers important things, but Ravi's mother forgets his birthday.

So "Vitamin-M" works at several levels:

| Level | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Literal joke** | A made-up vitamin for memory. |
| **Family humour** | Grandpa uses the idea to tease his daughter. |
| **Irony** | The person accused of weak memory remembers more than expected. |
| **Theme** | Memory is not only about small facts; it is also about remembering love, respect, duties, and family bonds. |

## Main Characters

| Character | Role in Story | Key Qualities |
|---|---|---|
| **Grandpa** | Elderly family member who has moved into the city flat | Independent, witty, proud, observant, generous, clever, affectionate |
| **Ravi** | Young boy asked to look after Grandpa | Caring, loyal, anxious, curious, embarrassed, affectionate |
| **Vidya / Ravi's mother** | Grandpa's daughter, worried about his safety | Protective, anxious, responsible, impatient at times, loving |
| **Ravi's father** | Appears near the end | Confused by the situation, part of the family humour |

## Central Conflict

The story does not have a villain.

The conflict comes from different needs:

| Person | What They Want | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Vidya | Grandpa must not go out alone. | She fears he may get lost, fall, or forget medicines. |
| Grandpa | He wants to go out by himself. | He has lived independently for many years and dislikes being treated helplessly. |
| Ravi | He wants to keep both promises. | He loves Grandpa and also promised his mother to keep him safe. |

This creates a practical and emotional dilemma for Ravi.

## Story Summary: Part 1

Ravi's mother is worried about Grandpa's memory and health. Grandpa has recently moved in with the family because living alone became unsafe. He had once taken the wrong amount of medicine, got lost after a walk, and had fallen in his garden when no one was there to help.

Grandpa dislikes the city. He misses his quiet brick house, mango tree, and familiar surroundings. He feels that his daughter's city flat is cramped and noisy. He also resents being stopped from going out alone.

Ravi's mother leaves for work and asks Ravi to take care of Grandpa. Ravi notices that she speaks to Grandpa too loudly, almost as if he were a small child. Ravi feels uncomfortable with this.

Grandpa says he wants to go to the corner shop for the Tamil newspaper. Ravi tries to offer help, but Grandpa becomes suspicious. He does not want to be treated like a prisoner. Ravi allows him to leave but secretly decides to follow him from a distance.

## Part 1: Check Your Understanding

### 1. Why did Grandpa dislike living in the city with his daughter?

Grandpa disliked the city because it was noisy, crowded, and unfamiliar. He missed his quiet town house with its garden and mango tree. In his own home, he had felt independent and useful. In the city flat, he felt restricted and treated like a dependent person.

### 2. Why did Vidya not want her father to go out alone?

Vidya was worried because Grandpa had recently shown signs of forgetfulness and physical weakness. He had taken a double dose of medicine, got lost during a walk, and had once fallen in the garden when living alone. She feared that something similar could happen in the city.

### 3. Was Ravi in favour of his mother treating Grandpa like a child? How can you say so?

No. Ravi was not comfortable with that treatment. He winced when his mother spoke to Grandpa too loudly. This reaction shows that Ravi felt embarrassed and uneasy because Grandpa was being spoken to as if he could not understand properly.

### 4. Would Ravi be able to keep up his promise to his mother?

Ravi tries to keep the promise, but not in a simple way. He lets Grandpa go out alone to avoid hurting him, then follows him secretly to keep him safe. This shows that Ravi is trapped between obedience to his mother and respect for Grandpa's dignity.

## Story Summary: Part 2

Ravi follows Grandpa through the city. Grandpa first stops at a children's park, buys peanuts, and watches children playing. Ravi hides behind a bush and gets into an embarrassing situation with a child and the child's mother.

Then Grandpa goes to a tea stall. Ravi hides behind a banyan tree, but local vendors become suspicious of him. Grandpa enjoys tea, bananas, and ice cream, which worries Ravi because these are restricted items for him.

Grandpa crosses the road, enters a barber shop, and later Ravi sees a man in Grandpa's yellow cap boarding a bus. Ravi follows the man onto the bus, only to discover that he is a stranger. Grandpa has given away his cap to the man. Ravi realises he has been tricked and loses track of Grandpa.

## Part 2: Check Your Understanding

### 1. Complete the flow chart by describing Grandpa's day out.

Use the table above. The most important stops are:

- children's park;
- tea stall;
- barber shop;
- bus stop;
- wrong bus chase.

### 2. Was Grandpa lost as feared by Ravi's mother?

No. Grandpa was not lost. Ravi lost track of him, but Grandpa had not lost himself. He knew what he was doing and returned home safely.

### 3. How would Ravi feel after seeing Grandpa?

Ravi would feel relieved, affectionate, embarrassed, and confused. He would be relieved because Grandpa was safe, affectionate because he realised how much he loved him, embarrassed because he had failed as a secret follower, and confused because Grandpa seemed to know more than he admitted.

### 4. Do you think Ravi's mother will get to know about what had happened?

She may not learn every detail, but Grandpa's comments suggest that he knows what happened and may tease Ravi indirectly. The story shows that Grandpa is clever enough to reveal things without directly exposing Ravi.

## Story Summary: Part 3

Ravi searches for Grandpa at the barber shop and park, but cannot find him. He becomes frantic because his mother had trusted him to look after Grandpa.

When Ravi returns home, he hears Grandpa snoring peacefully in his bedroom. Ravi feels deep relief and affection. He notices the smell of eucalyptus ointment and shaving cream and hugs Grandpa.

When Ravi's mother asks what they did that day, Grandpa says he had a quiet morning, while Ravi disappeared instead of looking after him. Ravi becomes confused and embarrassed.

Then Grandpa reveals gifts for Ravi, Vidya, and Ravi's father. It is Grandpa's birthday, and he has a family tradition of giving gifts to the children of the house on his own birthday. Vidya has forgotten the birthday, even though she had marked it on the calendar.

Grandpa jokingly says that his daughter needs Vitamin-M for her memory. Ravi receives a book of detective stories, and Grandpa's words suggest that he knows Ravi had been following him.

## Part 3: Check Your Understanding

### 1. How does Ravi's emotional reaction upon finding Grandpa safe reveal his feelings?

Ravi's relief shows that his concern was genuine. He was not merely obeying his mother; he truly loved Grandpa. His hug shows affection, tenderness, and guilt. The moment turns a comic chase into an emotional family scene.

### 2. What is the significance of Grandpa giving gifts on his own birthday?

This habit shows that Grandpa is generous and affectionate. It also shows that he still sees the family members as his children, regardless of their age. The tradition reverses normal expectation: instead of receiving love, Grandpa gives love.

### 3. Why did Grandpa give Ravi a detective story book?

Grandpa probably knew Ravi had been following him. The detective book is a humorous hint that Ravi needs better detective skills. It also shows Grandpa's affection because he teases Ravi gently instead of scolding him.

## Long Answer Question Bank

### 1. Grandpa is portrayed as forgetful in the beginning. Give two pieces of evidence to disprove it.

Grandpa may have some age-related weakness, but he is not simply forgetful. First, he remembers famous chess games and refers to players such as Karpov, Bobby Fischer, and Spassky while teaching Ravi. This shows sharp memory and mental alertness. Second, he remembers his own birthday and the family tradition of giving gifts to children on that day. He also seems to know that Ravi followed him, which shows observation and intelligence.

### 2. Give two traits of each character.

| Character | Trait 1 | Evidence | Trait 2 | Evidence |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Grandpa** | Independent | He insists on going out alone. | Witty | He jokes that Vidya needs Vitamin-M. |
| **Ravi** | Caring | He follows Grandpa to keep him safe. | Inexperienced | He is fooled by the yellow cap trick. |
| **Ravi's mother** | Protective | She warns Ravi not to let Grandpa go out alone. | Anxious | She worries because of Grandpa's earlier accidents. |

### 3. Why was Ravi worried about what Grandpa had at the tea stall?

Ravi was worried because Grandpa ate and drank things that were forbidden at home: sugary tea, bananas, and ice cream. Ravi knew his mother would be alarmed because these foods could be unsuitable for Grandpa's health.

### 4. Was it easy for Ravi to follow Grandpa?

No. Ravi had to hide in public places, avoid being noticed, deal with strangers, keep up through traffic, and finally chase a bus. He also faced embarrassment in the park, near the vendors, and outside the salon. The city setting made the chase comic and difficult.

### 5. "Ravi is a good detective." Give one argument in favour and one against.

| View | Argument |
|---|---|
| **In favour** | Ravi is observant and determined. He follows Grandpa across several places and notices the yellow cap. |
| **Against** | He is not careful enough. He attracts attention, enters the wrong place, and follows a stranger because he relies too much on the cap. |

### 6. Was Grandpa aware that Ravi was following him?

Yes, most likely. Grandpa's final comments and his gift of detective stories strongly suggest that he knew Ravi had followed him. He also gives Ravi indirect advice on how not to get fooled while trailing someone.

### 7. In your opinion, who needs Vitamin-M and why?

Vidya, Ravi's mother, needs Vitamin-M in the comic sense because she forgets Grandpa's birthday. In a deeper sense, the whole family needs "Vitamin-M" to remember that Grandpa is not only an old person needing care. He is also a father, lawyer, chess player, householder, generous elder, and independent human being.

### 8. Give an example of age-based bias.

Age-based bias happens when people assume a person's opinion is unimportant because the person is too young or too old.

Example:

A teenager may suggest a useful idea in a family discussion, but adults may dismiss it by saying, "You are too young to understand." Similarly, an elderly person may express a preference, but others may ignore it by saying, "You are too old to decide." Both situations show unfair judgment based on age instead of listening to the actual idea.

## Reported Speech Exercise: Model Completion

Original conversation:

- Arvind asks Priya whether she helps take care of her grandparents.
- Priya says yes and explains their family routine.
- Priya asks Arvind about his grandparents.
- Arvind explains his weekend visits and asks about medical needs.
- Priya explains the medicine and doctor schedule.

### Completed Reported Speech

Arvind asked Priya if she **helped in taking care of her grandparents at home**, for which Priya replied in the affirmative and said that they **took turns making sure they were alright**. Priya further asked Arvind **if he had grandparents living with him**. Arvind replied in the negative and said that **his parents and he visited his grandpa every weekend**. He further enquired **how she managed her grandparents' medical needs**. Priya responded by saying that they **had a schedule for giving medications and visits to the doctor**.

# Unit 4G: I Cannot Remember My Mother

## Text Identity

| Detail | Information |
|---|---|
| **Title** | I Cannot Remember My Mother |
| **Poet** | Rabindranath Tagore |
| **Genre** | Lyric poem / memory poem |
| **Central Emotion** | Tender longing for the mother |
| **Main Technique** | Sensory memory |
| **Main Message** | A loved one may not be remembered clearly, yet their presence can remain through sounds, scents, sights, and feelings. |

## Central Idea

The speaker says he cannot remember his mother clearly. However, he sometimes senses her presence in small details:

- a tune over his playthings;
- the smell of shiuli flowers;
- the atmosphere of temple service;
- the blue sky seen from his window;
- the feeling of a still, loving gaze.

The poem suggests that memory is not always a complete picture. Sometimes it appears as sound, fragrance, stillness, or emotion.

## Stanza-Wise Explanation

### Stanza 1

The speaker is in the middle of play. A tune seems to float over his playthings. The tune reminds him of a song his mother used to hum while rocking his cradle. He does not remember her face clearly, but he remembers the feeling created by her song.

| Key Sense | Auditory |
|---|---|
| Main image | lullaby-like tune |
| Emotion | tender childhood comfort |
| Setting | mostly indoor |

### Stanza 2

The speaker remembers his mother through smell. In early autumn, the fragrance of shiuli flowers floats in the air. This scent reminds him of temple morning service and becomes connected with his mother's presence.

| Key Sense | Olfactory |
|---|---|
| Main image | shiuli fragrance |
| Emotion | sacred tenderness |
| Setting | outdoor |

### Stanza 3

The speaker looks from his bedroom window into the distant blue sky. He feels that the stillness of his mother's gaze has spread over the sky. The mother's presence becomes vast, calm, and protective.

| Key Sense | Visual |
|---|---|
| Main image | blue sky and still gaze |
| Emotion | serenity and longing |
| Setting | indoor viewpoint, outdoor image |

## Poetic Devices

| Device | Example / Explanation | Effect |
|---|---|---|
| **Refrain** | The repeated idea that the speaker cannot remember his mother. | Shows loss, longing, and incomplete memory. |
| **Imagery** | Tune, flowers, scent, sky, gaze. | Makes memory vivid through the senses. |
| **Symbolism** | Shiuli flowers symbolise purity, devotion, and memory. | Gives the mother's presence a sacred quality. |
| **Alliteration** | Repeated soft sounds in phrases such as "my mother" and "stillness...spread". | Creates gentle musicality. |
| **Onomatopoeia** | "hum" suggests the sound of humming. | Connects sound and memory. |
| **Free verse** | The poem does not follow a strict rhyme scheme. | Makes the poem sound natural and reflective. |

## Why the Poem Is Enjoyable Without Rhyme

The poem has no regular rhyme scheme, but it is still musical because of:

- repetition;
- short lines;
- soft sound patterns;
- sensory images;
- emotional rhythm;
- the repeated return to the mother.

## Tone and Mood

| Term | Explanation |
|---|---|
| **Tone** | Tender, nostalgic, quiet, and reflective. |
| **Mood** | Serene, wistful, emotional, and gentle. |

The poet does not sound angry or dramatic. The grief is soft and inward.

## Title: Impact on the Poem

The title **I Cannot Remember My Mother** creates sadness immediately. It prepares the reader for absence and loss. However, the poem does not stay only in sadness. It shows that even when the mother cannot be remembered clearly, her presence survives through sensations and emotional memory.

## Why the Refrain Is Important

The repeated idea that the speaker cannot remember his mother shows:

- the pain of incomplete memory;
- the distance between childhood and present;
- the deep wish to recover the mother's presence;
- the way memory returns in fragments;
- the emotional truth that love can remain even without clear details.

## Symbolism in the Poem

| **Cradle** | Early childhood and dependence. |
| **Shiuli flowers** | Sacred memory, purity, fragrance, and tenderness. |
| **Morning service** | Devotion, prayer, and spiritual connection. |
| **Blue sky** | Vastness, peace, protection, and the mother's continuing presence. |

## Short Answer Questions

### 1. What is the emotional impact of the refrain?

The refrain creates sadness and longing. Each repetition reminds the reader that the speaker's memory is incomplete. At the same time, it deepens the poem because the speaker keeps finding traces of his mother in sound, smell, and sight.

### 2. Interpret the connection between the mother and shiuli flowers.

Shiuli flowers are fragrant and often associated with worship and early morning rituals. Their scent reminds the speaker of purity, devotion, and tenderness. Through the flowers, the mother's memory becomes sacred and delicate.

### 3. Interpret the connection between the mother and humming tune.

The humming tune suggests a lullaby or song from infancy. It connects the mother with comfort, sleep, protection, and childhood play. The speaker may not remember her face, but he remembers the emotional atmosphere created by her voice.

### 4. What role does nature play in the poem?

Nature becomes a carrier of memory. Flowers, fragrance, morning air, and the sky help the speaker feel his mother's presence. The natural world turns personal memory into something larger and more universal.

### 5. What can be inferred about the mother-child relationship?

The relationship was loving and tender. Even though the speaker cannot remember his mother clearly, he continues to feel her presence in deep emotional ways. This suggests that early love leaves lasting impressions.
